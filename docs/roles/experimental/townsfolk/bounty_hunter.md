# Bounty Hunter (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** You start knowing 1 evil player. If the player you know dies, you learn another evil player tonight. [1 Townsfolk is evil]

---

## 🧠 Overview

Bounty Hunter is a **targeted information role** that begins the game with knowledge of one evil player, but adds an extra evil Townsfolk to the game.

You provide:

- immediate evil-player information
- sustained evil-tracking over time
- strong execution pressure

Your power lies in:

- starting with unusually strong information
- turning executed evil players into further information
- helping good identify the evil team layer by layer

---

# 🟢 Playing Bounty Hunter (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an information role — you are:

> **A hunter who can track the evil team one player at a time**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to reveal immediately

Default strong play:

> “I know one evil player, but I need to decide whether using that now is worth the risk.”

Why:

- your information is extremely strong
- revealing early can get your target executed quickly
- revealing early can also get _you_ killed before you learn more

---

### 2. Study your target before acting

You should:

- watch how they behave socially
- see whether they fit other information
- consider whether they seem like a Minion, Demon, or possibly the evil Townsfolk

---

### 3. Build a plan, not just a read

You should decide:

- whether to push openly
- whether to tell one or two trusted players first
- whether waiting one day improves your chance of surviving and learning more later

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Turn one read into a chain

Move from:

> “I know one evil player.”

to:

> **“If this player dies, I may get the next link in the chain.”**

---

### Manage your own visibility

The more public your role becomes:

- the more likely the Demon is to kill you
- the more urgent it becomes to get your target executed
- the less likely you are to survive long enough to keep learning

---

### Remember the extra evil Townsfolk

The Bounty Hunter creates a more dangerous game for good:

- there is one more evil vote in play
- that evil player may look unusually believable
- your known evil player may even be that extra evil Townsfolk

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Revealing too fast with no support

Strong information still needs votes and trust.

---

### ❌ Waiting so long that you die with unused info

If the Demon kills you before your target dies, your chain ends.

---

### ❌ Forgetting the evil Townsfolk

The role is powerful, but it comes with a real cost.

---

### ❌ Treating your target as automatically executable

You still need to fit your information into the broader game state.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Bounty Hunter (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Use claimed certainty to pressure executions and split trust across the town**

---

## 🟢 Why Bounty Hunter is a strong bluff

- it gives immediate authority
- it justifies aggressive pushes on specific players
- it can split the town into camps
- it scales well if you prepare a believable sequence of evil reads

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I started knowing this player is evil.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose a believable target

### 🟢 Good approach

- pick someone who is already a little suspicious
- pick someone whose execution helps your team
- pick someone whose behavior can be framed as evil

---

### ⚠️ Risky approach

- targeting a strongly trusted player too early
- targeting someone heavily supported by mechanical info

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- choosing a target who breaks the whole bluff immediately
- accusing someone the town is extremely unlikely to kill

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Plan your sequence in advance

### 🟢 Safe choices

- know who your “next evil player” will be if your first target dies
- make sure your sequence stays believable over time

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- improvising every step
- creating a chain that becomes too convenient

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use the role to shape the social game

You should guide the table toward:

- executing your chosen targets
- distrusting players you want weakened
- accepting that your information is strong but not omniscient

---

### Example plays

- “I started knowing they were evil, and I’ve been trying to decide when to say it.”
- “If this player dies and I’m real, I should get another evil player tonight.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- reduce how hard you push certainty
- rely more on social reads than “hard info”
- pivot to a softer version of the claim if needed

---

### Use your claim strategically

- force difficult executions
- split good players into opposing camps
- pull heat away from the Demon

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overcommitting too hard

A brittle bluff breaks badly once one target looks wrong.

---

### ❌ Choosing a bad chain

Bounty Hunter is a long-game bluff, not a one-line bluff.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your accused players, timing, and social pressure must all fit together.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Extra evil Townsfolk

If the Bounty Hunter is in play at setup, one Townsfolk is evil. That player may even be the evil player the Bounty Hunter learns first.

---

## 🧩 Information progression

When the evil player you know dies, you learn another evil player that night. This means the role gets stronger if you can convert information into executions quickly enough.

---

## 🧠 Strategic impact

Bounty Hunter changes the game from:

- **finding the first evil player**

to:

- **turning each evil death into the next lead**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Bounty Hunter is not just about starting with strong information — it is about turning one evil player into a trail.**
