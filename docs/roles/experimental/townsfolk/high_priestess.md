# High Priestess (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, learn which player the Storyteller believes you should talk to most.

---

## 🧠 Overview

High Priestess is a **directional information role** that guides you toward the most relevant player each night.

You provide:

- targeted social direction
- prioritization of conversations
- guidance in complex or uncertain games

Your power lies in:

- following the direction effectively
- interpreting why a player was chosen
- using conversations to unlock information

---

# 🟢 Playing High Priestess (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an information role — you are:

> **A player guided toward the most important conversations**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Follow your direction

Default strong play:

> “Who should I talk to, and why might it be them?”

Why:

- your information is about **priority**, not alignment
- the value comes from interaction
- ignoring it wastes your ability

---

### 2. Ask meaningful questions

When you speak to your target:

- explore their role and reasoning
- compare their story to the game state
- look for inconsistencies

---

### 3. Track patterns over time

You should record:

- who you are pointed to each night
- how those players behave
- how your reads evolve

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Interpret the _reason_ for direction

Move from:

> “I should talk to this player.”

to:

> **“Why is this player important right now?”**

---

### Use direction as context, not proof

The player you are pointed to may be:

- evil
- good but important
- central to a key interaction

---

### Build connections

You should:

- compare multiple nights of results
- identify recurring players
- look for relationships between targets

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Treating direction as alignment

Being pointed to someone does not mean they are evil.

---

### ❌ Ignoring the ability

The value comes from using the direction actively.

---

### ❌ Asking shallow questions

You need meaningful interaction to extract value.

---

### ❌ Failing to track patterns

Single results are weak — patterns are strong.

---

# 😈 Bluffing High Priestess (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Simulate guided interactions and use them to justify your reads**

---

## 🟢 Why High Priestess is a strong bluff

- provides flexible, evolving information
- justifies talking to specific players
- explains changing priorities
- difficult to verify directly

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I was told I should talk to this player.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose believable targets

### 🟢 Good approach

- pick players relevant to the current game
- align with existing discussions
- ensure consistency

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- choosing random or irrelevant players
- contradicting the game state

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Build a believable pattern

### 🟢 Safe choices

- vary your targets naturally
- connect them to evolving situations

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- repeating targets without reason
- creating overly convenient patterns

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use it to justify behavior

You should:

- explain why you talk to certain players
- justify your evolving reads
- guide discussion direction

---

### Example plays

- “I was told to talk to them — that’s why I focused there.”
- “My information keeps pointing me toward these players.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- reduce reliance on it
- shift toward normal reads
- keep only light references

---

### Use your claim strategically

- guide attention
- support narratives
- influence conversation flow

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overcommitting to patterns

Too much structure can be suspicious.

---

### ❌ Choosing bad targets

Your targets must make sense.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your story must hold together over time.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Nature of direction

High Priestess does not give:

- alignment
- role information

It gives:

- **priority of interaction**

---

## 🧩 Interpretation layers

Each result can mean:

- “this player is important”
- “this player is suspicious”
- “this player connects to something else”

---

## 🧠 Strategic impact

High Priestess shifts the game from:

- **random conversations**

to:

- **targeted, meaningful interaction**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **High Priestess does not tell you who is evil — it tells you where to look.**
