# King (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, if the dead equal or outnumber the living, you learn 1 alive character. The Demon knows you are the King.

---

## 🧠 Overview

King is a **late-game information Townsfolk** that the Demon knows about from the start.

You provide:

- powerful endgame information
- pressure on Demon kill choices
- a focal point for social and strategic play

Your power lies in:

- surviving long enough to reach the late game
- using strong character information when player counts are low
- forcing the Demon to weigh the risk of killing you

---

# 🟢 Playing King (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just a Townsfolk — you are:

> **A known target who becomes powerful when the game gets small enough**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to go public

Default strong play:

> “I need to decide whether being openly dangerous helps me more than hiding.”

Why:

- the Demon already knows who you are
- public King play can shape the social game
- secret King play may help you survive to get strong late information

---

### 2. Stay alive long enough to matter

You should:

- avoid unnecessary execution pressure
- avoid becoming the easiest social target
- think about how likely the Demon is to kill you

---

### 3. Build trust before your information arrives

You do not get information immediately.

You should:

- establish credibility early
- build useful conversations
- prepare the table to take your later information seriously

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Plan for the late game

Move from:

> “I’m mostly a social role right now.”

to:

> **“If I survive, I may become a very strong information role.”**

---

### Use your position carefully

Because the Demon knows you are the King:

- they may avoid killing you if that feels suboptimal
- they may pressure you socially instead
- they may treat you as a problem to solve later

---

### Make your late information count

When the dead equal or outnumber the living, you learn an alive character each night.

You should:

- track your results clearly
- compare them to claims and worlds
- use them to confirm or challenge endgame lines

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Assuming you are all downside early

You are weak early, but potentially very strong later.

---

### ❌ Forgetting the Demon already knows you

Hiding from the Demon is impossible; hiding from the town is a separate choice.

---

### ❌ Wasting social influence before your info turns on

You often need trust first, information second.

---

### ❌ Treating one late result as complete proof

Your information can be powerful, but it still needs interpretation.

---

# 😈 Bluffing King (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Use the King claim to justify authority, survival, and late-game information**

---

## 🟢 Why King is a strong bluff

- explains why you want to survive
- justifies central or cautious play
- offers believable late-game information
- creates social influence even before the ability turns on

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m the King, so I’ll matter more later if I stay alive.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Decide whether to go public early

### 🟢 Good approach

- use the claim to become socially relevant
- build trust early
- prepare a believable late-game position

---

### ⚠️ Risky approach

- staying hidden too long and claiming late
- drawing too much early attention without a plan

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- bluffing King without a clear social strategy
- making the claim feel passive or meaningless

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Prepare your late-game information

### 🟢 Safe choices

- plan character results that fit the game
- use them to support evolving worlds
- keep them consistent with prior discussion

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- inventing overly perfect results
- contradicting established claims

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use the role’s social weight

You should guide the table toward:

- keeping you alive
- trusting your position
- valuing your future information

---

### Example plays

- “If I’m real, I become much stronger later, so keeping me alive matters.”
- “I don’t have much yet, but I should matter in the endgame.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If the bluff weakens:

- reduce emphasis on future certainty
- lean more on current social play
- keep the claim as supporting structure

---

### Use your claim strategically

- build a trusted position
- absorb attention
- influence late-game decisions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Treating King like a punishment role

That is not its function.

---

### ❌ Ignoring its late-game nature

Your bluff must reflect delayed information.

---

### ❌ Making results too convenient

Believable information is strong, not perfect.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Information timing

The King gains information only once the dead equal or outnumber the living, making it a late-game role.

---

## 🧩 Demon interaction

The Demon knows who the King is from the start, creating a unique pressure dynamic.

---

## 🧠 Strategic impact

King shifts the game from:

- **early social positioning**

to:

- **late-game character confirmation**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **The King is weak early, but becomes powerful if allowed to survive.**
