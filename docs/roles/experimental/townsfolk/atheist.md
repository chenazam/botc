# Atheist (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** The Storyteller can break the game rules, and if executed, good wins, even if you are dead. [No evil characters]

---

## 🧠 Overview

Atheist is not a normal Townsfolk — it creates a game where the Storyteller may simulate evil activity even though **there are no evil characters in play**.

You provide:

- structural uncertainty
- storyteller-driven misinformation
- an alternative win path for good

Your power lies in:

- making the group question whether the game itself is real in the normal sense
- turning “execute the Storyteller” into a legitimate solving line
- identifying when contradictions are systemic rather than player-driven

---

# 🟢 Playing Atheist (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an information role — you are:

> **The key to identifying when the game itself is the source of deception**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Do not reveal without reason

Default strong play:

> “I’ll only push Atheist if the game actually looks impossible.”

Why:

- early Atheist claims are rarely believed
- you need structural evidence
- premature claims help no one

---

### 2. Track impossible or inconsistent events

You should watch for:

- interactions that should not be possible
- information that does not fit the script
- outcomes that contradict multiple worlds

---

### 3. Compare explanations

You should always ask:

- does a normal game explain this?
- does Atheist explain this more cleanly?

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Build a coherent Atheist case

Move from:

> “This feels weird.”

to:

> **“This set of contradictions is best explained by Atheist.”**

---

### Treat the Storyteller as the source of signals

In an Atheist game:

- anomalies are intentional
- patterns still exist
- your job is to interpret them

---

### Time your reveal

Reveal when:

- normal worlds are collapsing
- your explanation is cleaner than alternatives
- the group can act on it

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Claiming Atheist without evidence

You need a case, not a feeling.

---

### ❌ Over-attributing randomness

Atheist games are not random — they are constructed.

---

### ❌ Forgetting the game must remain fair

The Storyteller is simulating a solvable game, not chaos.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Atheist (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Make the town distrust the game instead of suspecting players**

---

## 🟢 Why Atheist is a strong bluff

- redirects suspicion away from players
- creates doubt about all information
- can stall executions
- undermines confidence in “solved” worlds

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “What if this isn’t a real game?”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Introduce doubt gradually

### 🟢 Good approach

- connect Atheist to real contradictions
- let others consider it
- avoid forcing the idea

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- pushing Atheist in a clean, logical game
- overexplaining the theory too early

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Attack certainty

You should:

- question clean solutions
- challenge confident players
- make the table hesitate

---

### Example plays

- “Atheist explains this better than any normal world.”
- “We might be solving the wrong thing entirely.”

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control timing

- introduce Atheist when the game is unstable
- reinforce it when the town is about to commit
- avoid making it the only narrative

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If the bluff weakens:

- reduce emphasis
- pivot back to normal worlds
- keep only the doubt

---

### Use it strategically

- delay executions
- break consensus
- destabilize correct solves

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overcommitting

Atheist should create doubt, not replace all logic.

---

### ❌ Using it too early

It works best when the game already feels wrong.

---

### ❌ Making it too detailed

The more rigid the theory, the easier it is to break.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Storyteller behavior

In an Atheist game:

- the Storyteller simulates a believable game
- inconsistencies are intentional clues
- the game should still be solvable through reasoning

---

## 🧩 Solving pattern

Atheist is often identified when:

- multiple contradictions accumulate
- no normal world explains everything
- a Storyteller-driven explanation becomes cleaner

---

## 🧠 Strategic shift

Atheist changes the game from:

- **solving players**

to:

- **solving the game itself**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Atheist is not about finding who is lying — it is about realizing that the game itself is the lie.**
