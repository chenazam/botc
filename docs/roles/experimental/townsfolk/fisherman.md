# Fisherman (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, during the day, visit the Storyteller to receive advice to help your team win.

---

## 🧠 Overview

Fisherman is a **guidance role** that provides direct advice from the Storyteller.

You provide:

- strategic direction
- clarification in uncertain situations
- support in complex or confusing games

Your power lies in:

- asking at the right time
- interpreting the advice correctly
- applying it effectively

---

# 🟢 Playing Fisherman (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just a Townsfolk — you are:

> **A player who can receive direct guidance on how to win**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Do not use your ability immediately

Default strong play:

> “I should wait until I actually need advice.”

Why:

- early advice is often too general
- the game has not developed yet
- later use provides more specific value

---

### 2. Observe the game first

You should:

- watch claims and interactions
- identify confusing situations
- note where the game feels unclear

---

### 3. Stay alive

You only get one use:

- dying early removes your ability
- surviving increases your value

---

## 🧠 When to Use Your Ability

### Good timing includes:

- when the game feels stuck
- when multiple worlds exist with no clear direction
- when the group is unsure how to proceed
- when entering late game

---

## 🔑 How to Use the Advice

### Step 1: Listen carefully

The advice will:

- be helpful
- be relevant
- not directly solve the game

---

### Step 2: Interpret, don’t overinterpret

You should:

- take the advice at face value
- avoid twisting it too far
- consider multiple meanings

---

### Step 3: Apply it to the game

You can:

- guide discussion
- shift focus
- help the group move forward

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Using the ability too early

You lose the opportunity for more meaningful advice later.

---

### ❌ Treating the advice as absolute truth

It helps you — it doesn’t solve the game for you.

---

### ❌ Misinterpreting the advice

Overthinking can reduce its value.

---

### ❌ Not sharing the advice

Your team needs the benefit, not just you.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Fisherman (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Claim guidance to justify your decisions and influence the group**

---

## 🟢 Why Fisherman is a strong bluff

- explains sudden changes in direction
- justifies unusual decisions
- adds authority to your statements
- is difficult to verify

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I used Fisherman and got advice.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Time your claim

### 🟢 Good approach

- introduce it when the game is uncertain
- use it to explain a decision
- align it with the current situation

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming it too early
- using it without context

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Keep the advice believable

### 🟢 Safe choices

- keep advice simple
- make it relevant
- avoid overly specific or game-solving statements

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- giving perfect or overly precise advice
- contradicting the current game state

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use it to guide the group

You should:

- influence discussion
- shift focus
- justify your position

---

### Example plays

- “I got advice that we should focus on this area.”
- “The advice suggests we’re looking in the wrong place.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay consistent

If your bluff is working:

- stick to your advice
- reinforce it through your play
- avoid changing it too much

---

### Use your claim strategically

- justify decisions
- create authority
- influence outcomes

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Making advice too strong

It should guide, not solve.

---

### ❌ Using it too often

It’s a one-time ability.

---

### ❌ Contradicting yourself

Your advice must stay consistent with your actions.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Nature of advice

The Storyteller’s advice is:

- helpful
- relevant
- intentionally incomplete

---

## 🧩 Strategic use

Fisherman is strongest when:

- used at a moment of uncertainty
- applied thoughtfully
- shared effectively

---

## 🧠 Strategic impact

Fisherman shifts the game from:

- **uncertain direction**

to:

- **guided decision-making**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Fisherman does not give you answers — it helps you ask the right questions.**
