# General (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, you learn which alignment the Storyteller believes is winning: good, evil, or neither.

---

## 🧠 Overview

General is a **global information role** that provides insight into the overall state of the game.

You provide:

- high-level alignment information
- game-state evaluation
- directional guidance over time

Your power lies in:

- tracking how the game evolves
- interpreting shifts in alignment advantage
- combining global signals with local information

---

# 🟢 Playing General (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an information role — you are:

> **A player who reads the overall direction of the game**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Record your result every night

Default strong play:

> “Night 1: good / evil / neither.”

Why:

- your power comes from patterns
- single results are weak
- sequences are meaningful

---

### 2. Do not overinterpret early

You should:

- treat early results cautiously
- avoid drawing conclusions too quickly
- wait for trends to emerge

---

### 3. Stay consistent

You should:

- report results accurately
- maintain clear tracking
- avoid adjusting your narrative to fit the game

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Look for shifts

Move from:

> “What did I get?”

to:

> **“How is the game changing over time?”**

---

### Interpret trends

You should look for:

- consistent “good” → strong position
- consistent “evil” → danger
- frequent “neither” → unstable or unclear game

---

### Combine with other information

You should:

- compare with claims
- evaluate whether actions match the result
- use your info to validate or challenge worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Treating results as precise

General gives direction, not exact truth.

---

### ❌ Ignoring ambiguity

“Neither” is often the most important result.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing a single night

Your strength is in accumulation.

---

### ❌ Trying to force conclusions

Your role supports solving — it doesn’t replace it.

---

# 😈 Bluffing General (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Provide flexible, believable global information**

---

## 🟢 Why General is a strong bluff

- hard to verify
- naturally ambiguous
- easy to adapt
- fits many game states

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “Each night I learn who’s winning.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Keep results believable

### 🟢 Good approach

- reflect the current game state
- match visible momentum
- avoid extreme claims

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming perfect consistency
- contradicting obvious outcomes

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Build a pattern

### 🟢 Safe choices

- show gradual changes
- include “neither” results
- reflect uncertainty

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- overly convenient sequences
- results that perfectly justify your team

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use it to guide discussion

You should:

- influence perception of momentum
- support or weaken worlds
- shape decision-making

---

### Example plays

- “It felt like evil was ahead last night.”
- “Now it’s closer — I got neither.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If needed:

- reinterpret earlier results
- soften strong claims
- shift your narrative

---

### Use your claim strategically

- stabilize your team
- create doubt
- influence direction

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Making results too clean

Real General info is messy.

---

### ❌ Overcontrolling the narrative

Too much precision feels fake.

---

### ❌ Ignoring the game state

Your results must match reality.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Nature of information

General provides:

- subjective evaluation
- overall alignment advantage
- directional guidance

It does not provide:

- specific players
- exact roles
- definitive answers

---

## 🧩 Interpreting “neither”

“Neither” often means:

- the game is balanced
- multiple worlds remain viable
- uncertainty is high

---

## 🧠 Strategic impact

General shifts the game from:

- **player-focused solving**

to:

- **state-based evaluation**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **General does not tell you who is winning — it tells you how the game is leaning.**
