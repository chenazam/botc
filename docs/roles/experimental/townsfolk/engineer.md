# Engineer (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, at night, choose which Minions or which Demon is in play.

---

## 🧠 Overview

Engineer is a **one-time role-selection Townsfolk** that can reshape the evil team by changing **which Demon** or **which Minions** are in play.

You provide:

- controlled evil-team disruption
- a powerful reset tool
- strategic flexibility in unstable games

Your power lies in:

- choosing the right moment
- deciding whether Demon or Minion structure is the real problem
- forcing the game into a new configuration

---

# 🟢 Playing Engineer (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just a Townsfolk — you are:

> **A one-time opportunity to change the shape of the evil team**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Do not rush your ability

Default strong play:

> “I should only use this once I know what I’m trying to fix.”

Why:

- your ability is single-use
- early use is often blind
- the value comes from solving a specific problem, not creating random change

---

### 2. Identify what kind of problem the game has

You should ask:

- is the Demon the real issue?
- are the Minions creating the pressure?
- is the game unstable because of structure, or because of information?

---

### 3. Stay alive long enough to matter

You only get one use, so:

- dying early wastes enormous value
- surviving into mid-game gives you context
- patience usually improves your decision

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Decide what you are trying to accomplish

Move from:

> “I can change the evil team.”

to:

> **“What change improves good’s chances the most right now?”**

---

### Change the Demon when the Demon is the problem

This is strongest when:

- a specific Demon type is dominating the game
- the current Demon creates uniquely dangerous patterns
- you need to break a late-game trajectory

---

### Change the Minions when support structure is the problem

This is strongest when:

- Minion pressure is warping the game
- the Demon itself is manageable, but the support is not
- the game feels stuck in a harmful social or mechanical pattern

---

### Use your power as a reset, not a guess

Engineer is best when:

- the current evil team shape is hurting good badly
- the game has enough information for you to act intentionally
- your choice changes the game in a meaningful way

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Using it too early

A blind Engineer use often trades one unknown problem for another.

---

### ❌ Treating it as a panic button

It is strongest as a deliberate correction, not a desperate gamble.

---

### ❌ Ignoring what actually needs to change

Sometimes the Demon is the problem. Sometimes the Minions are.

---

### ❌ Waiting until you die with it unused

Holding forever is still wasting it.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Engineer (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Use Engineer to explain why the game’s evil structure feels inconsistent or changed**

---

## 🟢 Why Engineer is a strong bluff

- explains changing evil pressure
- accounts for shifting Demon or Minion behavior
- justifies mechanical instability
- fits naturally in high-complexity games

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “Engineer may have changed the evil team.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use it to explain shifts in game texture

### 🟢 Good approach

- tie it to real changes in pressure or behavior
- use it when the game already feels different than before
- let it explain a shift, not the whole game

---

### ⚠️ Risky approach

- introducing Engineer without any visible reason
- using it to explain every inconsistency at once

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- overcommitting to a very specific unseen change
- creating a theory that collapses if one detail is wrong

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Keep the explanation broad enough

### 🟢 Safe choices

- “The Demon may have changed.”
- “The Minion structure may not be the same anymore.”

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- claiming highly specific team changes with no support
- using Engineer to rewrite too much of the game’s history

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use it to destabilize confidence

You should guide the table toward:

- questioning whether earlier reads still apply
- doubting whether current pressure matches the original setup
- hesitating before locking in a final world

---

### Example plays

- “If Engineer is in play, we may not be dealing with the same evil team anymore.”
- “That shift in pressure could be explained by an Engineer use.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If the bluff weakens:

- reduce its importance
- keep it as one possible explanation, not the explanation
- pivot back to ordinary worlds if needed

---

### Use your claim strategically

- weaken confidence in earlier conclusions
- make the group re-open settled worlds
- blur the distinction between old reads and current reality

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overusing the bluff

Engineer should introduce uncertainty, not replace all logic.

---

### ❌ Using it too early

It works best after the game has already changed tone or pressure.

---

### ❌ Making the theory too rigid

A broad destabilizing explanation is much stronger than an overdefined one.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Scope of change

Engineer does not change the whole game freely.

It changes:

- **which Demon is in play**, or
- **which Minions are in play**

This makes it powerful, but much narrower than a full arbitrary role change.

---

## 🧩 Strategic use

The key choice is not just **when** to use Engineer, but **what layer of evil structure** to change:

- Demon layer
- Minion layer

That decision defines the value of the role.

---

## 🧠 Game-state impact

Engineer is strongest when:

- the current evil structure is identifiable
- the change meaningfully disrupts evil momentum
- good has enough context to benefit from the reset

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Engineer is not about changing the game randomly — it is about changing the part of evil that matters most.**
