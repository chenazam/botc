# Nightwatchman (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, at night, choose a player: they learn you are the Nightwatchman.

---

## 🧠 Overview

Nightwatchman is a **confirmation role** that creates a trusted connection between two players.

You provide:

- confirmed good information
- a reliable social anchor
- trust in an otherwise uncertain game

Your power lies in:

- choosing the right player
- creating a trustworthy link
- leveraging that trust effectively

---

# 🟢 Playing Nightwatchman (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just a Townsfolk — you are:

> **A source of confirmed trust in the game**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide when to use your ability

Default strong play:

> “I should use this when confirmation matters most.”

Why:

- your ability is one-time
- early use creates structure
- late use can stabilize endgame

---

### 2. Choose your target carefully

You should consider:

- who is likely to survive
- who will use the information responsibly
- who benefits the most from being trusted

---

### 3. Think about the impact

You should ask:

- will this create a strong good core?
- will this clarify the game?
- will this reduce chaos?

---

## 🧠 When to Use Your Ability

### Early use (Day 1 / Night 1)

- creates immediate trust
- builds a strong foundation
- helps organize the group

---

### Mid-game use

- reinforces key players
- clarifies contested situations
- supports developing worlds

---

### Late-game use

- stabilizes final decisions
- confirms a critical player
- reduces risk in endgame

---

## 🧠 How to Use the Confirmation

### As the Nightwatchman

You should:

- coordinate with your confirmed player
- share information effectively
- build a trustworthy network

---

### As the confirmed player

You should:

- trust the Nightwatchman
- use the information responsibly
- avoid overexposing the confirmation unnecessarily

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Choosing a poor target

Your value depends on who you confirm.

---

### ❌ Using the ability at the wrong time

Timing defines impact.

---

### ❌ Overexposing the confirmation

Too much visibility can attract evil attention.

---

### ❌ Wasting the trust

Confirmation is powerful — use it well.

# 😈 Bluffing Nightwatchman (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create false confirmation or simulate trust**

---

## 🟢 Why Nightwatchman is a strong bluff

- creates instant credibility
- forces reactions from other players
- difficult to disprove directly
- influences social structure

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I used my ability — this player can confirm me.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your “confirmation” target

### 🟢 Good approach

- pick a cooperative or uncertain player
- align with your team’s goals
- ensure your story is believable

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- picking players likely to contradict you
- creating easily disprovable claims

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Control the narrative

### 🟢 Safe choices

- present your confirmation calmly
- allow the other player to react
- build credibility gradually

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- forcing agreement
- overdefending your claim

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use it to shape trust

You should:

- create a trusted pair
- influence group dynamics
- stabilize your position

---

### Example plays

- “I used Nightwatchman — they can confirm me.”
- “We have a confirmed link here.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay consistent

If your bluff is working:

- maintain the relationship
- reinforce trust
- avoid contradictions

---

### Use your claim strategically

- build a false good core
- protect teammates
- influence executions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Picking the wrong target

A bad partner collapses the bluff.

---

### ❌ Overplaying confirmation

Too much certainty can attract scrutiny.

---

### ❌ Ignoring reactions

Your bluff depends on how others respond.

---
