# Balloonist (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, you learn a player of a different character type than last night.

---

## 🧠 Overview

Balloonist is a **structured information role** that builds knowledge over time.

You provide:

- gradual, reliable information
- coverage across character types
- long-term deduction potential

Your power lies in:

- tracking information across multiple nights
- combining results into a coherent picture
- narrowing down possible roles systematically

---

# 🟢 Playing Balloonist (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an information role — you are:

> **A structured information engine that reveals the shape of the game over time**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Start tracking immediately

Default strong play:

> “Night 1: I saw Player X.”

Why:

- your information builds over time
- early data is critical
- consistency matters

---

### 2. Record everything clearly

You should track:

- each player you learn
- the order of results
- which character types are still possible

---

### 3. Avoid early overinterpretation

You should:

- not jump to conclusions
- wait for multiple data points
- focus on collecting information first

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Build a structured picture

Move from:

> “I’ve seen some players.”

to:

> **“I know which character types are where.”**

---

### Use elimination

Over time:

- each result represents a different character type
- repeated coverage narrows possibilities
- missing types become meaningful

---

### Combine with other information

You should:

- cross-check with claims
- compare with known roles
- identify contradictions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Treating results as random

Your information is structured — use that structure.

---

### ❌ Forgetting the “different type” rule

Each result must be a different character type than the previous night.

---

### ❌ Revealing too early without context

Your power comes from accumulation.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Balloonist (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Simulate structured information over time**

---

## 🟢 Why Balloonist is a strong bluff

- provides flexible, evolving information
- difficult to verify immediately
- allows controlled narrative building
- fits naturally into many games

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “Each night I get a different character type — here’s what I’ve seen.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Plan your results

### 🟢 Good approach

- choose believable players
- track your own sequence
- ensure consistency

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- repeating character types incorrectly
- contradicting known roles

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Build a believable pattern

### 🟢 Safe choices

- spread results across the group
- avoid clustering suspicious players

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- overly convenient results
- results that perfectly solve the game

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control timing

You should:

- reveal gradually
- adapt your results as the game evolves
- avoid committing too early

---

### Example plays

- “So far I’ve hit different types each night — this is my spread.”
- “These results don’t overlap, so they should all be different types.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- adjust interpretation
- reinterpret earlier results
- reduce reliance on strict structure

---

### Use your claim strategically

- create structured confusion
- challenge other claims
- introduce alternative worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Breaking the type rule

Consistency is critical.

---

### ❌ Over-optimizing results

Perfect information looks fake.

---

### ❌ Ignoring interaction with other roles

Your results must fit the overall game state.

---
