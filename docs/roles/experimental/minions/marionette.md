# Marionette (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Minion  
**Ability:** You think you are a good character, but you are not. The Demon knows who you are. [You are seated next to the Demon]

---

## 🧠 Overview

Marionette is a deception role — but unlike most Minions, the deception is directed inward.

You provide:

- hidden evil presence
- misinformation through sincerity
- social distortion

Your power lies in:

- acting genuinely good while being evil
- spreading believable information
- unknowingly supporting the Demon

---

# 🔴 Playing Marionette (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just a Minion — you are:

> **An evil player who believes they are good**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Play normally

Default strong play:

> “I’m just playing my role as best I can.”

Why:

- your strength comes from authenticity
- you are more convincing than any bluff
- overthinking reveals nothing — just play

---

### 2. Share information freely

You should:

- act like a good player
- share your information naturally
- participate in solving

---

### 3. Trust your instincts

You do not need to:

- fake behavior
- coordinate consciously
- avoid helping the town

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Continue behaving as good

Move from:

> “I need to play carefully.”

to:

> **“I just need to play correctly.”**

---

### Be aware of social signals

While you do not know you are evil:

- the Demon may subtly support you
- your reads may align strangely with evil narratives
- you may feel unusually “correct”

---

### Stay consistent

Consistency is your greatest strength:

- your behavior is naturally believable
- your claims are naturally trusted
- your presence stabilizes evil’s position

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Marionette)

### ❌ Trying to act suspicious

You should not attempt to “hide” — you are already hidden.

---

### ❌ Overcomplicating your play

Simple, honest play is optimal.

---

### ❌ Distrusting your own role too early

Paranoia can reduce your effectiveness.

---

# 😈 Playing Around the Marionette (Evil Team)

## 🎯 Your Role

As the Demon (or other evil players), your goal is to:

> **Guide the Marionette without exposing them**

---

## 🟢 Key Strategy

- support their narratives subtly
- avoid directly signaling alignment
- let them act naturally

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Protect without revealing

### 🟢 Good approach

- agree with them when reasonable
- avoid contradicting them strongly
- let others trust them

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- obvious coordination
- over-defending them
- treating them differently from other players

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use their credibility

The Marionette is:

- trusted
- consistent
- believable

You should:

- reinforce their conclusions
- build worlds around their statements
- let them anchor discussions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil Team)

### ❌ Overprotecting the Marionette

This creates suspicion.

---

### ❌ Revealing them accidentally

Too much alignment exposes the connection.

---

### ❌ Ignoring them

They are one of your strongest assets.

---

# 🟢 Playing Against Marionette (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just finding evil players — you are:

> **Identifying when a sincere player may still be evil**

---

## 🧩 Step 1 — Question perfect alignment

Marionette players often:

- feel very trustworthy
- contribute meaningfully
- align well with evolving worlds

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Being helpful does not guarantee being good.**

---

## 🔍 Step 2 — Watch for structural oddities

You should consider:

- why a player is always “in the right place”
- whether their role fits cleanly
- whether their position is unusually stable

---

## 🧠 Step 3 — Consider seating

Marionette is always:

- seated next to the Demon

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **If two neighboring players feel unusually stable or aligned, consider Marionette.**

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Fully trusting consistent players

Consistency can be misleading.

---

### ❌ Ignoring seating patterns

Marionette has a positional constraint.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing sincerity

Sincere players can still be evil.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Information behavior

The Marionette:

- believes their information is real
- shares it naturally
- may accidentally spread misinformation

---

## 🧩 Social dynamics

Marionette creates:

- highly believable evil presence
- natural-looking worlds
- strong social anchors

---

## 🧠 Strategic impact

Marionette shifts the game from:

- **detecting lies**

to:

- **detecting misalignment**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **The Marionette is not dangerous because they deceive — they are dangerous because they don’t.**
