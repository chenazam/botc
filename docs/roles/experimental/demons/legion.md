# Legion (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Demon  
**Ability:** Each night\*, a player might die. Executions fail if only evil voted. You register as a Minion too. [Most players are Legion]

---

## 🧠 Overview

Legion is not a normal Demon — it turns the game into a hidden **many-evil-player** scenario where normal assumptions about executions, role distribution, and social reads become unreliable.

You provide:

- distributed evil presence
- execution distortion
- false role registration

Your power lies in:

- making the game feel flatter and less solvable
- hiding inside a wide field of plausible claims
- punishing towns that rely on normal execution logic

---

# 🔴 Playing Legion (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an evil player — you are:

> **Part of a hidden collective that wins by making the game look normal when it is not**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Blend into the group

Default strong play:

> “I’m a normal good role.”

Why:

- Legion wins by avoiding early recognition
- you do not need a heroic bluff, just a believable one
- the more normal the game looks, the stronger Legion becomes

---

### 2. Spread claims naturally

You should:

- avoid obvious clustering
- avoid too many identical claims
- distribute yourselves across believable claim space

---

### 3. Exploit normal expectations

You want the town to:

- build ordinary worlds
- expect one Demon and a small evil team
- misread the structure of the game

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Preserve uncertainty

Move from:

> “We need to avoid being caught.”

to:

> **“We need the game to keep feeling unresolved.”**

---

### Use executions against the town

Legion thrives when:

- executions fail to produce clarity
- the town keeps killing good players
- suspicion remains broad rather than focused

---

### Maintain a flat social field

You should:

- avoid becoming too coordinated
- avoid pushing one perfect world
- keep multiple players equally plausible

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Legion)

### ❌ Acting like a normal evil team

Legion is not about protecting one obvious central Demon.

---

### ❌ Over-coordinating socially

Too much alignment between players becomes visible.

---

### ❌ Creating sharp contradictions too early

Legion is strongest when the game remains broadly plausible and hard to resolve.

---

# 🟢 Playing Against Legion (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just solving for one Demon — you are:

> **Recognizing that the structure of the game itself may be different from normal**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Ask whether the game feels unusually flat

Default strong play:

> “If nothing sharpens, I should consider Legion.”

Why:

- Legion games often lack the normal shape of good vs evil tension
- many players may seem equally plausible
- ordinary worlds often fail to truly resolve the game

---

### 2. Watch execution behavior carefully

You should:

- note who votes and who does not
- pay attention to failed executions
- consider whether vote patterns reveal structural information

---

### 3. Stop solving for a single culprit

You should:

- test whether the game makes more sense as a Legion game
- compare “normal game” worlds against “Legion world”
- treat broad plausibility as suspicious rather than reassuring

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Change the question

Move from:

> “Who is the Demon?”

to:

> **“Is this a Legion game?”**

---

### Re-evaluate failed executions

If an execution fails:

- do not read it like a normal protection or survival effect by default
- consider whether only evil voted
- compare the vote pattern to a Legion explanation

---

### Use structure, not just claims

Legion is often identified through:

- game texture
- voting behavior
- lack of meaningful resolution

more than through one dramatic contradiction

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Playing a Legion game like a normal game

Normal expectations about evil distribution can fail badly.

---

### ❌ Over-executing without learning

Legion punishes executions that do not actually clarify the structure.

---

### ❌ Trusting broad plausibility

If too many players seem reasonable, that may itself be the clue.

---

# 😈 Bluffing as Legion

## 🎯 Your Role

As Legion, your bluff is not about flashy deception — it is about:

> **Maintaining the illusion that the game has a normal structure**

---

## 🟢 Why Legion is inherently strong

- many evil players can cover a lot of claim space
- the game may feel socially normal for longer than it should
- failed executions can be misread
- registering as a Minion distorts information in useful ways

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m a normal good role in a normal game.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Claim conservatively

### 🟢 Good approach

- choose believable roles
- avoid overly ambitious bluffs
- keep the game feeling ordinary

---

### ⚠️ Risky approach

- flashy mechanical claims
- aggressive world control too early

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- too many overlapping team claims
- obvious social coordination
- treating Legion like a standard minion-demon structure

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Control the game’s texture

You should:

- keep multiple players plausible
- avoid helping the town focus sharply
- support broad uncertainty rather than specific chaos

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use executions and votes intelligently

You should guide the table toward:

- unproductive executions
- uncertainty around failed executions
- continued belief in a normal game structure

---

### Example plays

- “We still don’t have enough to lock anything in.”
- “That failed execution could mean a lot of things.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as Legion

### Stay flexible

If suspicion rises:

- reduce visibility
- let other players occupy attention
- avoid hard commitment unless necessary

---

### Use your claim strategically

- keep the game looking ordinary
- support worlds that never fully resolve
- make structure harder to read

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Acting as a unified bloc

Legion should feel distributed, not coordinated.

---

### ❌ Overplaying failed executions

The more forced your interpretation, the more suspicious it becomes.

---

### ❌ Making the game too weird too early

Legion is strongest when the town discovers the truth late.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Registration and information

Legion registers as a Minion too, which means:

- information roles may receive distorted role-identity reads
- “Minion” information does not behave normally
- standard setup expectations become less reliable

---

## 🧩 Failed executions

Executions fail if only evil voted, which means:

- failed executions can contain real voting information
- the town must examine _who voted_, not just what happened
- a failed execution may be one of the strongest Legion signals in the game

---

## 🧠 Structural effect

Legion changes the game from:

- **finding one hidden center of evil**

to:

- **recognizing that evil is distributed throughout the game**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Legion is not solved by identifying one liar — it is solved by recognizing that the whole game is shaped differently.**
