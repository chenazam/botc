# Heretic (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** Whoever wins, loses & whoever loses, wins, even if dead.

---

## 🧠 Overview

Heretic is not a normal Outsider — it inverts the game’s win condition for everyone.

You provide:

- win-condition inversion
- endgame uncertainty
- pressure on “correct” play

Your impact lies in:

- making normal victory logic unreliable
- forcing both teams to question whether they should pursue their usual win condition
- creating a game where the right move may look wrong

---

# 🟢 Playing Heretic (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an Outsider — you are:

> **A hidden inversion of the game’s victory condition**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Do NOT reveal casually

Default strong play:

> “I’m a normal role.”

Why:

- if evil becomes confident Heretic is in play, they can play toward the reversed outcome
- your power is strongest when uncertain
- public certainty helps the wrong team adapt correctly

---

### 2. Blend into the game

You should:

- claim a plausible role
- avoid becoming a focal point
- avoid making yourself an obvious puzzle piece

---

### 3. Watch how the game is developing

You should track:

- whether good is close to a normal win
- whether evil is controlling pace
- whether the table is beginning to suspect a non-standard game

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Protect uncertainty

Move from:

> “I need to tell people the truth.”

to:

> **“I need to make sure the wrong team does not solve the real win condition.”**

---

### Think in reverse

If Heretic is in play:

- a normal good win becomes an evil win
- a normal evil win becomes a good win

So you must ask:

- what outcome looks good for evil?
- what outcome looks good for good?
- which team is accidentally playing toward the wrong result?

---

### Reveal only if it helps

A Heretic reveal can be powerful, but only if:

- it changes the team’s decisions in time
- it does not simply hand the solution to evil
- the group can still act on it correctly

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Revealing too early

This often helps evil more than good.

---

### ❌ Playing for a normal good win

If Heretic is in play, “solving the game” normally can lose it.

---

### ❌ Forgetting that dead players still matter

Heretic affects the final result even if dead.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Heretic (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Introduce doubt about whether the game should be solved normally**

---

## 🟢 Why Heretic is a powerful bluff

- it attacks the table’s confidence directly
- it creates hesitation around executions and endgames
- it can freeze otherwise correct play
- it works especially well in unstable games

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “What if this is a Heretic game?”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Introduce doubt, not certainty

### 🟢 Good approach

- float the possibility
- connect it to strange or high-pressure situations
- let the table argue about it

---

### ⚠️ Risky approach

- hard-claiming Heretic immediately
- making the whole game about Heretic too early

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- introducing Heretic when the game is still clean and stable
- forcing the bluff before the table is ready to believe it

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use it to disrupt endgame logic

### 🟢 Safe uses

- create hesitation around executing the “obvious Demon”
- make the group doubt a standard winning line

---

### 🔴 Risky uses

- overcommitting so hard that the bluff becomes the only explanation
- creating contradictions that make Heretic impossible

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control the emotional temperature

Heretic works best when it causes:

- doubt
- hesitation
- second-guessing

You should guide the table toward:

- questioning whether a normal win is actually correct
- slowing down decisive play
- mistrusting “obvious” solutions

---

### Example plays

- “Are we sure winning normally is actually how we win here?”
- “This endgame feels too straightforward.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If the bluff weakens:

- reduce emphasis
- keep only the doubt, not the full theory
- pivot back to ordinary worlds if needed

---

### Use your claim strategically

- disrupt final-day certainty
- make executions feel dangerous
- force good players to hesitate when they should act

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overcommitting too early

Heretic is strongest as doubt, not as a lecture.

---

### ❌ Making it the only narrative

It should destabilize normal play, not replace the whole game immediately.

---

### ❌ Ignoring timing

Heretic is usually strongest when the game is already tense or close to resolution.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Win-condition inversion

Heretic flips the result of the game:

- if good would normally win, evil wins instead
- if evil would normally win, good wins instead
- this applies even if the Heretic is dead

---

## 🧩 Strategic impact

Heretic changes the question from:

- “How do we win?”

to:

- “What does winning actually mean in this game?”

---

## 🧠 Endgame dynamics

Heretic is strongest in games where:

- a normal win condition seems close
- one team feels confident
- the table is tempted to act quickly

That is exactly where Heretic is most dangerous.

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Heretic does not hide information — it makes the meaning of victory itself uncertain.**
