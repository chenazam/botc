# Snitch (Experimental)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** Each Minion gets 3 bluffs.

---

## 🧠 Overview

Snitch is an Outsider that gives the evil team a strong **bluffing advantage at the start of the game**.

You provide:

- stronger evil bluff quality
- reduced early uncertainty for Minions
- more believable claim space for evil

Your impact lies in:

- making evil claims cleaner and safer
- reducing accidental bluff conflicts
- forcing good to rely less on weak bluff detection

---

# 🟢 Playing Snitch (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an Outsider — you are:

> **A hidden source of information for the evil team**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Consider revealing early

Default strong play:

> “If Snitch is in play, evil already has safer bluffs.”

Why:

- the benefit is already active
- revealing helps good adjust expectations
- it explains unusually strong early claims

---

### 2. Watch for unusually clean claims

You should look for:

- confident, well-fitting role claims
- fewer accidental double-claims
- stable early narratives

---

### 3. Adjust how you evaluate claims

You should assume:

- evil players may have safe, high-quality bluffs
- claim quality alone is less meaningful
- behavior matters more than initial claims

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Reinterpret strong claims

Move from:

> “That claim looks solid.”

to:

> **“That claim may be solid because evil had better options.”**

---

### Focus on behavior over claims

You should:

- track consistency over time
- compare actions to claims
- rely less on first impressions

---

### Help the group adapt

You can:

- remind players that bluff quality is inflated
- reduce overconfidence in clean worlds
- encourage deeper reasoning

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Ignoring Snitch’s impact

Leads to overtrusting evil claims.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing clean narratives

Clean structure is not proof of good alignment.

---

### ❌ Failing to communicate the effect

If the group doesn’t account for Snitch, they misread the game.

---

# 😈 Bluffing with Snitch (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Use strong bluffing information to appear completely legitimate**

---

## 🟢 Why Snitch is powerful for evil

- each Minion has safe bluff options
- bluff overlap is easier to avoid
- early claims can look natural and consistent
- evil presence becomes harder to detect

---

## 🧩 Core Approach

> “I can claim something cleanly and confidently.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use your safe bluffs

### 🟢 Good approach

- choose a strong, believable role
- ensure it fits your position
- commit confidently

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- risky or unnecessary claims
- ignoring your safe options

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Match behavior to your claim

### 🟢 Safe choices

- act naturally within the role
- provide believable reasoning
- stay consistent

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- overexplaining
- sounding rehearsed
- acting too perfectly

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use the cleaner social field

You should:

- reinforce stable worlds
- avoid unnecessary chaos
- let good players overtrust structure

---

### Example plays

- “My role fits cleanly with everything we’ve seen.”
- “Nothing really contradicts my claim.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay consistent

If your bluff is working:

- keep it stable
- avoid unnecessary changes
- let trust build naturally

---

### Use your claim strategically

- anchor discussions
- support teammates indirectly
- maintain a believable presence

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Wasting the advantage

Use the strong bluffing options you’re given.

---

### ❌ Overplaying confidence

Too much perfection can look suspicious.

---

### ❌ Ignoring social play

Behavior still matters.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Setup impact

Snitch gives Minions strong bluffing information before the game begins.

---

## 🧩 Social effect

Snitch creates:

- cleaner evil play
- more believable claims
- reduced early-game chaos

---

## 🧠 Strategic impact

Snitch shifts the game from:

- **guess-based bluffing**

to:

- **informed deception**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Snitch does not change the rules of the game — it changes how convincing evil can be.**
