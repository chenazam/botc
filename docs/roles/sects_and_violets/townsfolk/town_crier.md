# Town Crier (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, you learn if a Minion nominated today.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Town Crier is a **vote-phase detection role** that identifies whether a Minion made a nomination.

You provide:

- a yes/no signal about Minion nomination behavior

Your power lies in:

- linking Minions to visible actions
- narrowing down possible evil players
- creating pressure around nomination choices

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- recurring-info
- social
- detection

---

# 🟢 Playing Town Crier (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just tracking nominations — you are:

> **Identifying which players could (or could not) be Minions based on their actions**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Track nominations precisely

You must:

- note who nominated
- track all nominations, not just successful ones

---

### 2. Share your result

Default strong play:

- share daily

Example:

> “Town Crier: yes” (a Minion nominated)

or

> “Town Crier: no” (no Minion nominated)

---

### 3. Pair results with nomination data

Your info requires:

- accurate recall of nominators
- clear communication of who acted

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Eliminate possibilities

If:

- result = **no**  
  → none of the nominators are Minions

If:

- result = **yes**  
  → at least one nominator is a Minion

---

### Use multiple days

Your power grows through:

- repeated observations
- changing nomination patterns

---

### Apply pressure

You can:

- encourage or discourage nominations
- influence who nominates
- create controlled situations

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Missing nominations

- breaks your information

---

### ❌ Overvaluing single results

- requires multiple data points

---

### ❌ Ignoring non-nominators

- silence can be meaningful

---

# 😈 Bluffing Town Crier (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Provide believable nomination-based information that protects your team**

---

## 🟢 Why Town Crier is a strong bluff

- simple yes/no information
- based on visible actions
- flexible interpretation
- easy to maintain

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “Town Crier: yes/no”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with nominations

You must:

- ensure your result matches plausible events
- avoid contradictions

---

### Example

If:

- no suspicious nominations occurred  
  → claiming “no” is safer

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use ambiguity

Nominations:

- can be interpreted in multiple ways
- do not always reveal intent

Use this to:

- support your team
- create uncertainty

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay consistent

You should:

- track nominations carefully
- maintain internal logic
- adapt to new information

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Contradicting public actions

- easy to expose

---

### ❌ Ignoring nomination patterns

- reduces credibility

---

### ❌ Being too rigid

- limits flexibility

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 What counts as a nomination

Town Crier detects:

- whether a Minion initiated a nomination

This includes:

- any valid nomination attempt

---

## 🧩 Interpretation limits

Town Crier does not tell you:

- which Minion nominated
- how many Minions nominated
- which nomination triggered the result

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Town Crier often:

- influences who nominates
- creates pressure on active players
- rewards careful observation

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Town Crier does not reveal Minions — it reveals whether Minions are taking action.**
