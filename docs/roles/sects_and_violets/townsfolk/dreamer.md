# Dreamer (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a player: you learn 1 good and 1 evil character, but only one is correct.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Dreamer is a **high-information, high-ambiguity role** that receives **two possible character identities**, one of which is correct.

You provide:

- strong, recurring information about players
- a constrained set of possibilities for each target

Your power lies in:

- narrowing down possible identities
- creating connections between players and roles
- building structured but uncertain worlds

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- recurring-info
- ambiguous
- high-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Dreamer (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just gathering information — you are:

> **Mapping players into overlapping sets of possible identities**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Start collecting data

Each night:

- choose targets deliberately
- prioritize players likely to matter

---

### 2. Share carefully

Default strong play:

- share results, but with context

Example:

> “Alice is either Fortune Teller or Poisoner.”

---

### 3. Avoid overcommitment

You should:

- treat each result as a **pair**, not a conclusion
- avoid assuming which side is correct too early

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Build overlapping worlds

Your results become powerful when:

- multiple checks intersect
- roles start to conflict or align

---

### Use cross-referencing

Compare:

- your results with other information roles
- claims made by players
- observed behavior

---

### Revisit earlier results

As the game progresses:

- reinterpret previous results
- update which option seems more likely

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Treating results as definitive

- one option is always false

---

### ❌ Ignoring one side

- both possibilities must be considered

---

### ❌ Failing to update beliefs

- Dreamer requires constant re-evaluation

---

# 😈 Bluffing Dreamer (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Provide believable but flexible information that supports your team**

---

## 🟢 Why Dreamer is a strong bluff

- high complexity → hard to verify
- naturally ambiguous
- allows flexible interpretation
- fits many situations

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Dreamer, I got X or Y on [player].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose believable pairs

You should:

- include at least one plausible role
- avoid impossible combinations
- align with known claims

---

### Example

- “Alice is either Empath or Poisoner.”
- “Bob is either Clockmaker or Demon.”

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- pairs that contradict known facts

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use ambiguity

Your results:

- allow multiple interpretations
- can be adjusted over time

Use this to:

- support evolving narratives
- avoid being pinned down

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay consistent

You should:

- maintain internal logic
- avoid contradictions
- align with the game state

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Adapt your story

You can:

- reinterpret earlier results
- shift which option you believe
- respond to new claims

---

### Support your team

- protect evil players
- cast doubt on good players
- reinforce plausible worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Creating impossible pairs

- breaks credibility immediately

---

### ❌ Overcomplicating

- makes your story hard to follow

---

### ❌ Inconsistency

- contradictions expose you

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Nature of the information

Each result contains:

- one correct character
- one incorrect character

But:

- you do not know which is which

---

## 🧩 Interaction with misinformation

Dreamer is highly sensitive to:

- poisoning
- drunkenness
- other distortions

This can:

- produce entirely misleading pairs
- create convincing false worlds

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Dreamer often:

- becomes central to deduction
- creates complex world structures
- drives mid- to late-game decisions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Dreamer does not give answers — it gives possibilities that must be resolved.**
