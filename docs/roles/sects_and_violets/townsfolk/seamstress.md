# Seamstress (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, at night, choose 2 players: you learn if they are the same alignment.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Seamstress is a **single-use, alignment-checking role** that determines whether two players share the same alignment.

You provide:

- a yes/no answer about alignment matching

Your power lies in:

- resolving key relationships
- confirming or denying partnerships
- narrowing down possible worlds

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- single-use
- confirmation
- targeted

---

# 🟢 Playing Seamstress (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just checking alignment — you are:

> **Testing whether two players belong to the same team**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Do NOT rush

Default strong play:

- wait

Why:

- better targets emerge later
- early checks are low value

---

### 2. Identify key pairs

Look for:

- suspicious partnerships
- conflicting claims
- important players

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Choose impactful pairs

Strong targets:

- two suspected evil players
- a suspected evil + a trusted good
- two conflicting claims

---

### Interpret carefully

If:

- result = same → both good or both evil
- result = different → one good, one evil

---

### Combine with other info

Seamstress becomes powerful when:

- combined with other roles
- used in structured worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Checking random players

- low value

---

### ❌ Using too early

- wastes potential

---

### ❌ Overinterpreting result

- needs context

---

# 😈 Bluffing Seamstress (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Claim a result that shapes relationships between players**

---

## 🟢 Why Seamstress is a strong bluff

- simple yes/no
- flexible interpretation
- high impact
- hard to verify

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “Seamstress: X and Y are same/different alignment.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your pair

You should:

- support your team
- create confusion
- align with game state

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use the result

You can:

- link players together
- separate players
- create tension

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay consistent

Your claim must:

- align with other information
- remain plausible

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Contradicting known info

- easy to expose

---

### ❌ Choosing bad pairs

- reduces impact

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

- limits flexibility

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Nature of the result

Seamstress gives:

- alignment comparison only

Not:

- specific roles
- exact identities

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Seamstress often:

- creates strong connections
- influences trust
- shapes group dynamics

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Seamstress does not tell you who is evil — it tells you who belongs together.**
