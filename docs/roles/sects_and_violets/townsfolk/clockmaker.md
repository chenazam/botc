# Clockmaker (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** You start knowing how many steps from the Demon to its nearest Minion.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Clockmaker is a **first-night information role** that provides **distance-based structural information** about the evil team.

You provide:

- a number indicating the minimum distance between the Demon and a Minion

Your power lies in:

- constraining possible team configurations
- eliminating impossible worlds
- shaping early-game deduction

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- early-info
- structural
- world-building

---

# 🟢 Playing Clockmaker (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just reporting a number — you are:

> **Defining the possible shapes of the evil team**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Share your information

Default strong play:

- usually share early

Example:

> “I’m Clockmaker, I got a 2.”

Why:

- your info is structural, not targeted
- it helps the town build initial worlds
- early sharing maximizes its value

---

### 2. Frame your result correctly

You should clarify:

- this is **minimum distance**
- it refers to **Demon ↔ nearest Minion**

---

### 3. Avoid overinterpretation

Do not:

- jump to conclusions
- assign exact roles immediately

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Use your number to eliminate worlds

Your info helps answer:

- which player pairs are impossible
- which team configurations cannot exist

---

### Combine with other roles

Clockmaker becomes stronger when combined with:

- Dreamer
- Empath
- Snake Charmer
- other alignment-based roles

---

### Stay flexible

Your number:

- constrains possibilities
- does not define a single solution

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Treating your info as exact

- it only gives a minimum distance

---

### ❌ Ignoring alternative worlds

- multiple setups can fit the same number

---

### ❌ Forgetting misinformation

Your result may be wrong due to:

- poisoning
- drunkenness
- script effects

---

# 😈 Bluffing Clockmaker (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Provide believable structural information that supports your team’s position**

---

## 🟢 Why Clockmaker is a strong bluff

- simple, single number
- difficult to immediately disprove
- flexible interpretation
- fits many game states

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Clockmaker, I got a X.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose a believable number

You should:

- pick a number that fits your team’s position
- avoid obviously contradictory values

---

### General guidance

- low numbers (1–2): suggest tight evil grouping
- higher numbers: suggest spread-out team

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- numbers that are instantly impossible

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use ambiguity

Clockmaker results:

- are open to interpretation
- allow multiple worlds

Use this to:

- support your team
- create uncertainty

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay consistent

You should:

- align your reasoning with your number
- support worlds that fit your claim
- avoid contradictions

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Adapt your interpretation

You can:

- reinterpret your number
- shift which worlds you support
- respond to new information

---

### Support your team indirectly

- protect your Demon
- redirect suspicion
- reinforce plausible structures

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Choosing a bad number

- can create contradictions later

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

- limits your flexibility

---

### ❌ Ignoring setup reality

- your number must remain plausible

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Distance calculation

“Steps” refers to:

- player seating order
- counting positions between players

The result is:

- the shortest distance between Demon and any Minion

---

## 🧩 Interaction with misinformation

Clockmaker can be:

- poisoned
- drunk
- otherwise misled

This creates:

- incorrect structural assumptions
- misleading world-building

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Clockmaker often:

- anchors early discussion
- shapes initial world-building
- influences how players group suspicion

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Clockmaker does not tell you where evil is — it tells you where evil cannot be.**
