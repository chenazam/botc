# Artist (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, during the day, privately ask the Storyteller any yes/no question.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Artist is a **single-use, high-impact information role** that can ask **any yes/no question** about the game.

You provide:

- precise, targeted information chosen by you

Your power lies in:

- asking the right question
- resolving critical uncertainty
- breaking otherwise unsolvable situations

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- single-use
- high-impact
- flexible

---

# 🟢 Playing Artist (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just asking a question — you are:

> **Choosing the most important uncertainty in the game to resolve**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Do NOT rush your question

Default strong play:

- wait

Why:

- early game has too many unknowns
- better questions emerge later
- your ability becomes more valuable over time

---

### 2. Gather context first

You should:

- observe claims
- track information
- identify key conflicts

---

### 3. Think in terms of uncertainty

Your question should target:

- the biggest unresolved issue
- the most impactful ambiguity

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Ask the right question

Strong Artist questions:

- resolve between two worlds
- confirm or deny a key assumption
- eliminate large sets of possibilities

---

### Examples of good questions

- “Is Alice the Demon?”
- “Is there a Poisoner in play?”
- “Did Bob receive false information last night?”

---

### Examples of weaker questions

- overly vague
- low-impact
- not tied to a decision

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Asking too early

- wastes potential

---

### ❌ Asking unfocused questions

- yields low-value answers

---

### ❌ Asking something already implied

- redundant information

---

# 😈 Bluffing Artist (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Claim to have asked a question that supports your team’s narrative**

---

## 🟢 Why Artist is a strong bluff

- unprovable
- flexible
- high authority
- can resolve key moments

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Artist. I asked: [question]. The answer was: yes/no.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose a believable question

You should:

- align with the game state
- reflect real uncertainties
- avoid contradictions

---

### Example

- confirm a teammate as good
- discredit a Townsfolk
- reinforce an existing world

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- questions that contradict known facts

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Time your claim

Best used:

- when the game needs direction
- to resolve a critical moment
- to protect your team

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use authority carefully

Artist claims:

- carry weight
- influence decisions

Use this to:

- guide executions
- shape final worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Poor question choice

- reduces credibility

---

### ❌ Bad timing

- limits impact

---

### ❌ Overuse of authority

- draws suspicion

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Question scope

Artist questions:

- must be yes/no
- can be about game state
- can reference players, roles, or events

---

## 🧩 Interpretation limits

Even correct answers:

- depend on how the question is phrased
- may not fully resolve ambiguity

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Artist often:

- breaks deadlocks
- resolves key uncertainties
- influences endgame decisions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Artist is not about asking a question — it is about asking the right question at the right time.**
