# Philosopher (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, at night, choose a good character: gain its ability. If this character is in play, it becomes drunk.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Philosopher is a **flexible, high-impact role** that can **copy another Townsfolk ability**, potentially disabling the original.

You provide:

- access to a chosen ability
- disruption of an existing role

Your power lies in:

- adapting to the game state
- filling missing roles
- reshaping information flow

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- flexible
- high-impact
- decision-heavy

---

# 🟢 Playing Philosopher (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just choosing a role — you are:

> **Deciding which ability the town needs most right now**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Do NOT rush your choice

Default strong play:

- wait

Why:

- early choices are uninformed
- better targets emerge later
- your flexibility is your strength

---

### 2. Observe the game state

You should track:

- claimed roles
- missing roles
- suspicious patterns

---

### 3. Identify gaps

Look for:

- roles not in play
- roles that would be useful
- roles that might be compromised

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Choose based on need

Good Philosopher choices:

- provide missing information
- confirm key players
- resolve uncertainty

---

### Consider the downside

If the role is in play:

- the original becomes drunk

This can:

- weaken good information
- create confusion

---

### Timing matters

You should:

- choose when your ability will matter most
- avoid acting too late

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Waiting too long

- never using your ability

---

### ❌ Choosing randomly

- wastes flexibility

---

### ❌ Ignoring the drunk effect

- can harm your team

---

# 😈 Bluffing Philosopher (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Claim to have chosen a role that supports your narrative**

---

## 🟢 Why Philosopher is a strong bluff

- highly flexible
- difficult to verify
- explains many behaviors
- adapts to the game

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Philosopher. I chose [role].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose a believable role

You should:

- align with the game state
- avoid contradictions
- support your team

---

### Example

- choosing a role that explains your behavior
- choosing a role that discredits another player

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- impossible combinations

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use flexibility

You can:

- adapt your claim
- shift your narrative
- respond to new information

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Manage the drunk effect

If your claim implies:

- another player is drunk

You can:

- explain their incorrect information
- create doubt

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Inconsistent claims

- exposes you

---

### ❌ Ignoring the drunk implication

- reduces credibility

---

### ❌ Overcomplicating

- makes your story fragile

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Role copying

You gain:

- the full ability of the chosen role

But:

- only once per game
- timing is critical

---

## 🧩 Drunk interaction

If the chosen role is in play:

- that player becomes drunk

This can:

- invalidate their information
- create confusion

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Philosopher often:

- reshapes the game state
- introduces new information sources
- destabilizes existing trust

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Philosopher is not about choosing the best role — it is about choosing the right role for the moment.**
