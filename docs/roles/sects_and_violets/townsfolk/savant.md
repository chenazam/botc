# Savant (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each day, you may visit the Storyteller to learn 2 things in private: 1 is true & 1 is false.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Savant is a **high-information, high-ambiguity role** that receives **two statements each day**, one true and one false.

You provide:

- structured but uncertain information

Your power lies in:

- interpreting statements correctly
- building worlds from partial truths
- resolving contradictions

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- recurring-info
- ambiguous
- high-complexity

---

# 🟢 Playing Savant (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just receiving information — you are:

> **Evaluating which parts of reality are true and which are false**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Share your statements

Default strong play:

- share both statements

Example:

> “Savant: (A) Alice is evil OR (B) there are no Outsiders.”

---

### 2. Keep both possibilities alive

You must:

- treat both statements seriously
- avoid early commitment

---

### 3. Record everything

Your value comes from:

- tracking statements over time
- revisiting them later

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Cross-reference statements

Your info becomes powerful when:

- combined with other roles
- compared across days

---

### Eliminate contradictions

As the game progresses:

- one statement becomes impossible
- the other becomes true

---

### Build worlds

Each statement pair creates:

- two possible realities

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Picking one statement too early

- leads to wrong worlds

---

### ❌ Ignoring statements later

- value increases over time

---

### ❌ Failing to track history

- loses context

---

# 😈 Bluffing Savant (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Provide believable but misleading structured information**

---

## 🟢 Why Savant is a strong bluff

- inherently ambiguous
- hard to verify
- flexible
- fits chaotic games

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “Savant: (A) X OR (B) Y”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Create plausible pairs

You should:

- include one believable statement
- include one misleading statement
- align with the game state

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Maintain flexibility

You can:

- shift which statement you support
- adapt to new information

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use complexity

You can:

- create confusion
- overwhelm simpler reasoning
- support false worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Impossible statements

- easily exposed

---

### ❌ Overcomplication

- reduces credibility

---

### ❌ Inconsistency

- breaks trust

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Nature of the information

Each day:

- one statement is true
- one is false

But:

- you do not know which

---

## 🧩 Interpretation challenges

Savant requires:

- long-term tracking
- cross-referencing
- flexible thinking

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Savant often:

- drives complex discussions
- creates competing worlds
- influences mid- and late-game

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Savant does not give answers — it gives structured uncertainty that must be resolved.**
