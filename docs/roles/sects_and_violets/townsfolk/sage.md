# Sage (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** If the Demon kills you, you learn that it is 1 of 2 players.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Sage is a **death-triggered information role** that provides a **2-player Demon candidate set** when killed by the Demon.

You provide:

- a narrowed pool of possible Demon candidates

Your power lies in:

- forcing the Demon into a constrained endgame
- creating high-value information at the moment of death
- enabling strong final deductions

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- death-trigger
- high-impact
- endgame

---

# 🟢 Playing Sage (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just trying to survive — you are:

> **Positioning yourself to die at the right time for maximum impact**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Stay hidden

Default strong play:

- do not reveal early

Why:

- you need the Demon to target you
- revealing makes you less likely to be killed
- your power only triggers on Demon kill

---

### 2. Appear useful, but not threatening

You should:

- contribute to discussion
- avoid becoming a top priority kill
- avoid being executed

---

### 3. Avoid execution

You must:

- survive the day
- aim to die at night

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Position yourself as a good kill

You want to:

- look like a valuable Townsfolk
- be worth removing for the Demon

---

### Plan for your result

When you die:

- your information will be critical
- you must communicate it clearly

---

### Interpret your result carefully

If you learn:

> “The Demon is either Alice or Bob”

You must:

- evaluate both options
- use other information to resolve the pair

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Revealing too early

- reduces your chance of being killed

---

### ❌ Getting executed

- your ability does not trigger

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to one candidate

- both options must be considered

---

# 😈 Bluffing Sage (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Introduce high-impact but controlled uncertainty into the game**

---

## 🟢 Why Sage is a strong bluff

- powerful but delayed information
- difficult to verify
- creates immediate pressure
- fits many game states

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I was Sage. The Demon is either X or Y.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your pair carefully

You should:

- include at least one non-Demon
- avoid impossible combinations
- align with the current world

---

### Example

- include a suspicious player + a trusted player
- create a difficult decision

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- pairs that can be easily resolved

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Time your claim

Best used:

- after death
- at a critical moment
- when the game needs direction

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Shape the endgame

Your claim should:

- force difficult choices
- create uncertainty
- protect your Demon

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Choosing obvious pairs

- easy to solve

---

### ❌ Contradicting known information

- reduces credibility

---

### ❌ Poor timing

- weakens impact

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Trigger condition

Sage only triggers if:

- killed by the Demon

It does not trigger if:

- executed
- killed by other effects

---

## 🧩 Nature of the result

The result:

- always contains the Demon
- also contains one incorrect player

But:

- you do not know which is which

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Sage often:

- defines the late game
- forces high-stakes decisions
- creates pressure on final executions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Sage does not solve the game — it forces the game into a solvable state.**
