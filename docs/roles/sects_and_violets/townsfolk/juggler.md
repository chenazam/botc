# Juggler (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** On your 1st day, publicly guess up to 5 players' characters. That night, you learn how many you got correct.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Juggler is a **single-use, high-impact information role** that tests multiple role hypotheses at once.

You provide:

- a count of correct guesses among your selections

Your power lies in:

- testing multiple theories simultaneously
- creating structured information from chaos
- generating early-game anchors

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- early-info
- social
- high-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Juggler (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just guessing — you are:

> **Designing a test that extracts maximum information from a single result**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Plan your juggle

Before speaking:

- think about what you want to learn
- avoid random guesses
- structure your picks

---

### 2. Make meaningful guesses

Strong juggle sets:

- test specific hypotheses
- cover important players
- create interpretable outcomes

---

### 3. Communicate clearly

Your guesses must be:

- public
- clear
- unambiguous

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Interpret your result carefully

If you learn:

> “You got 2 correct”

You must:

- consider all combinations
- cross-reference with claims
- eliminate impossible worlds

---

### Use structure, not randomness

Your guesses should allow you to ask:

- which subsets could be correct?
- which combinations make sense?

---

### Combine with other info

Juggler becomes powerful when combined with:

- Dreamer
- Clockmaker
- Artist
- social reads

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Random guessing

- reduces value

---

### ❌ Overcomplicated sets

- hard to interpret

---

### ❌ Ignoring combinations

- result requires analysis

# 😈 Bluffing Juggler (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create believable early-game information that supports your team**

---

## 🟢 Why Juggler is a strong bluff

- single-use → hard to verify later
- public action → looks transparent
- flexible interpretation
- strong early influence

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I juggled X, Y, Z… I got N correct.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose plausible guesses

You should:

- align with likely roles
- avoid obvious contradictions
- support your team

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Pick a believable result

Your number should:

- match your guesses
- feel realistic
- not be extreme without reason

---

### Example

- 0 → everything wrong
- 1–2 → most common
- high numbers → risky

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- impossible combinations

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use flexibility

You can:

- reinterpret your guesses
- support evolving worlds
- adapt to new information

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Impossible results

- easily exposed

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

- reduces flexibility

---

### ❌ Poor guess design

- limits usefulness

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Nature of the information

You learn:

- how many guesses are correct

You do NOT learn:

- which ones are correct

---

## 🧩 Interpretation complexity

Each result creates:

- multiple valid combinations
- overlapping possibilities
- a need for cross-referencing

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Juggler often:

- anchors early discussion
- creates shared reference points
- influences early suspicion

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Juggler is not about guessing correctly — it is about designing guesses that are useful regardless of the outcome.**
