# Mathematician (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, you learn how many players' abilities worked abnormally (since dawn) due to another character's ability.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Mathematician is a **meta-information role** that detects **how many abilities behaved abnormally**.

You provide:

- a count of abnormal ability interactions

Your power lies in:

- detecting when something is wrong
- identifying the presence of disruptive roles
- validating or challenging other players' information

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- meta-info
- detection
- high-complexity

---

# 🟢 Playing Mathematician (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not identifying roles — you are:

> **Detecting when the game state has been distorted**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Share your numbers

Default strong play:

- share early and consistently

Example:

> “Mathematician: 1”

Why:

- your info gains value over time
- patterns matter more than individual results

---

### 2. Track changes over time

You should:

- record each night’s result
- look for increases or decreases

---

### 3. Stay neutral early

Avoid:

- overinterpreting early numbers
- jumping to conclusions

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Interpret patterns, not single values

Your results become meaningful when:

- compared across multiple nights
- correlated with known events

---

### Use your info to validate or challenge

Ask:

- does this match what should have happened?
- are abilities behaving as expected?

---

### Narrow down sources of abnormality

Abnormal behavior can come from:

- poisoning
- drunkenness
- madness effects
- role-specific interactions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Treating numbers as direct information

- they require interpretation

---

### ❌ Ignoring timing

- when the number changes matters

---

### ❌ Overcommitting early

- early results are often ambiguous

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 What counts as “abnormal”

An ability is abnormal if:

- it did not function as intended
- it was altered by another ability

This includes:

- poisoning
- drunkenness
- madness penalties
- role-specific effects

---

## 🧩 Interpretation challenges

Mathematician does not tell you:

- which players were affected
- which abilities were abnormal
- what caused the abnormality

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Mathematician often:

- detects hidden interference
- challenges assumptions
- supports or undermines other claims

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Mathematician does not tell you what is happening — it tells you that your assumptions may be wrong.**
