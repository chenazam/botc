# Flowergirl (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, you learn if a Demon voted today.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Flowergirl is a **vote-tracking information role** that detects whether the Demon participated in voting.

You provide:

- a yes/no signal about Demon voting behavior

Your power lies in:

- narrowing down the Demon based on voting patterns
- confirming or challenging suspicion on specific players
- creating pressure through vote analysis

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- recurring-info
- social
- detection

---

# 🟢 Playing Flowergirl (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just tracking votes — you are:

> **Linking Demon identity to visible player behavior**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Track votes carefully

You must:

- pay close attention to who votes
- note late votes and hesitant votes
- remember edge cases (ties, no execution, etc.)

---

### 2. Share your result

Default strong play:

- share daily

Example:

> “Flowergirl: yes” (a Demon voted)

or

> “Flowergirl: no” (no Demon voted)

---

### 3. Pair results with voting data

Your info is only useful if:

- you know who voted
- the group can reconstruct vote patterns

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Narrow down suspects

If:

- result = **no**  
  → none of the voters are the Demon

If:

- result = **yes**  
  → at least one voter is the Demon

---

### Use multiple days

Your power grows with:

- repeated results
- changing vote patterns

---

### Combine with other roles

Flowergirl works well with:

- Town Crier
- Dreamer
- general social reads

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Not tracking votes accurately

- invalidates your information

---

### ❌ Treating single results as decisive

- needs multiple data points

---

### ❌ Ignoring abstentions

- non-voters can be just as important

---

# 😈 Bluffing Flowergirl (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Provide believable voting-based information that protects your team**

---

## 🟢 Why Flowergirl is a strong bluff

- simple yes/no information
- depends on public data
- flexible interpretation
- easy to maintain

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “Flowergirl: yes/no”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with visible votes

You must:

- ensure your result matches plausible interpretations
- avoid obvious contradictions

---

### Example

If:

- clearly no suspicious players voted  
  → claiming “no” is safer

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use ambiguity

Voting patterns:

- are often unclear
- allow multiple interpretations

Use this to:

- support your team
- avoid being pinned down

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Adapt over time

You can:

- shift interpretations
- respond to changing vote patterns
- adjust your narrative

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Contradicting visible reality

- easy to expose

---

### ❌ Being too precise

- overconfidence can be suspicious

---

### ❌ Ignoring vote context

- results must feel natural

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 What counts as a vote

Flowergirl detects:

- whether the Demon raised their hand during a vote

This includes:

- any valid vote during the day

---

## 🧩 Interpretation limits

Flowergirl does not tell you:

- which vote included the Demon
- which player is the Demon
- how many times the Demon voted

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Flowergirl often:

- creates pressure on voting behavior
- encourages transparency
- rewards careful observation

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Flowergirl does not reveal the Demon — it reveals whether the Demon is participating.**
