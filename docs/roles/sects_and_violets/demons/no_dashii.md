# No Dashii (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Demon  
**Ability:** Each night\*, choose a player: they die. Your 2 Townsfolk neighbors are poisoned.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The No Dashii is a Demon that creates **persistent, hidden misinformation** by poisoning its neighbors.

You provide:

- ongoing deaths
- continuous information distortion

Your power lies in:

- corrupting otherwise reliable information
- creating believable false worlds
- hiding within structured logic

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- misinformation
- passive-control
- high-impact

---

# 😈 Playing No Dashii (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just killing players — you are:

> **Corrupting the foundation of the town’s reasoning**

---

## 🟢 Core Ability

- each night: kill a player
- your two neighbors are poisoned

---

## 🧠 Core Strategies

---

## 🧠 1. Passive Misinformation (Core Play)

### Concept

Let your poisoned neighbors:

- generate incorrect information

---

### Result

- town builds incorrect worlds
- conclusions feel logical
- but are wrong

---

👉 This is your main weapon

---

## 🧠 2. Hide in Structure

### Concept

Let the town:

- believe their reasoning is correct

---

### Example

- poisoned Dreamer gives consistent but wrong pairs
- poisoned Clockmaker gives believable number

---

👉 The more “logical” the world feels, the stronger it is

---

## 🧠 3. Kill Around Suspicion

### Concept

Choose kills that:

- support the false world
- avoid exposing poison

---

### Example

- avoid killing poisoned players too early
- remove players who might question the logic

---

## 🧠 4. Protect Your Poison

### Concept

Your neighbors are valuable

---

You should:

- keep them alive
- let them spread misinformation
- avoid drawing attention to them

---

## 🧠 5. Blend In

### Concept

You benefit from:

- structured reasoning
- logical worlds

---

👉 Lean into “clean logic” — it helps you

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Killing poisoned neighbors too early

- removes your advantage

---

### ❌ Creating obvious contradictions

- reveals poison

---

### ❌ Ignoring information flow

- weakens your impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Against No Dashii (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just solving the game — you are:

> **Detecting when “correct-looking logic” is actually wrong**

---

## 🧠 Core Strategies

---

## 🧠 1. Expect misinformation

You must assume:

- some information is wrong

---

## 🧠 2. Identify likely poison zones

Look for:

- consistent but incorrect info
- players whose info doesn’t match reality

---

## 🧠 3. Test alternative worlds

Ask:

> ❗ “What if this player is poisoned?”

---

## 🧠 4. Avoid tunnel vision

Do not:

- fully commit to one world
- ignore contradictions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Trusting clean logic too much

- poisoned info can still look perfect

---

### ❌ Ignoring small inconsistencies

- often key clues

---

### ❌ Overcommitting early

- leads to false worlds

---

# 😈 Bluffing No Dashii

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create believable worlds that appear logically consistent**

---

## 🧩 Approach

You should:

- support structured reasoning
- avoid obvious lies
- reinforce poisoned information

---

## 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poison mechanics

- affects both neighbors
- persists as long as they remain neighbors

---

## 🧩 Information impact

Poisoned players:

- receive incorrect information
- do not know they are poisoned

---

## 🧠 Social impact

No Dashii often:

- creates strong but incorrect worlds
- rewards logical reasoning (ironically)
- punishes overconfidence

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **No Dashii wins when the town is confidently wrong.**
