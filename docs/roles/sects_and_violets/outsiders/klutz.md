# Klutz (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** When you learn that you died, publicly choose 1 alive player: if they are evil, your team loses.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Klutz is a **death-triggered Outsider** that creates a **high-stakes final decision** upon death.

You provide:

- a forced public choice that can immediately lose the game

Your impact lies in:

- introducing execution risk for the good team
- shaping endgame decisions
- creating pressure around trust

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- death-trigger
- high-risk
- endgame

---

# 🟢 Playing Klutz (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just avoiding death — you are:

> **Managing the risk of a game-ending decision**

---

## 🟢 Core Rule

When you die:

- you must choose a living player
- if that player is evil → good loses immediately

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Avoid execution

Default strong play:

- do not get executed early

Why:

- early Klutz death is extremely dangerous
- little information → high risk of failure

---

### 2. Stay low profile

You should:

- avoid suspicion
- not attract execution
- blend in

---

### 3. Consider claiming

You may:

- reveal to avoid execution
- or stay hidden depending on the situation

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Manage your death timing

If you die:

- your decision must be informed
- more information = better odds

---

### Build trust

You should:

- identify players you trust
- track consistent behavior
- avoid chaotic reads

---

### Prepare for your decision

Before death, consider:

- who is most likely good
- who is safest to pick

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Dying too early

- low information → high risk

---

### ❌ Panic choosing

- leads to mistakes

---

### ❌ Picking based on weak reads

- unreliable

---

# 😈 Bluffing Klutz (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Use the threat of Klutz to manipulate execution decisions**

---

## 🟢 Why Klutz is a strong bluff

- high stakes
- influences execution choices
- creates hesitation
- easy to understand

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Klutz. If you execute me, I could lose the game.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use fear

You can:

- discourage execution
- create doubt
- slow the game

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Shape trust

You may:

- imply who you would pick
- influence alliances
- redirect suspicion

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Time your claim

Best used:

- when under threat
- to avoid execution
- to protect your team

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overusing fear

- becomes suspicious

---

### ❌ Poor timing

- reduces effectiveness

---

### ❌ Inconsistent behavior

- weakens claim

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Trigger condition

Klutz triggers when:

- you learn that you died

This includes:

- execution
- night death

---

## 🧩 Nature of the decision

The choice:

- must be public
- must be immediate
- has permanent consequences

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Klutz often:

- creates execution hesitation
- shifts endgame dynamics
- forces trust-based decisions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Klutz is not about information — it is about making the right choice under pressure.**
