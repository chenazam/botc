# Sweetheart (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** When you die, 1 player is drunk from now on.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Sweetheart is an Outsider that introduces **delayed misinformation** into the game.

You provide:

- a future source of incorrect information

Your power (or impact) lies in:

- disrupting otherwise reliable information
- creating uncertainty after your death
- forcing the town to question assumptions

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- misinformation
- delayed-effect
- disruptive

---

# 🟢 Playing Sweetheart (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just an Outsider — you are:

> **A future source of hidden misinformation**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Stay low impact

Default strong play:

- avoid early execution

Why:

- your death creates a problem for good
- delaying your effect is beneficial

---

### 2. Avoid unnecessary attention

You should:

- blend in
- avoid being a priority execution
- not create suspicion

---

### 3. Be cautious with claims

You may:

- claim a Townsfolk
- stay vague

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Manage your death timing

If you are:

- executed early → early misinformation
- alive longer → delay disruption

---

### Consider controlled execution

Sometimes:

- dying at the right time is beneficial
- especially if the town can anticipate the effect

---

### Communicate risk

You may:

- warn the group before dying
- highlight the incoming uncertainty

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Dying too early

- introduces unnecessary chaos

---

### ❌ Drawing attention

- increases execution risk

---

### ❌ Ignoring your impact

- your death changes the game

---

# 😈 Bluffing Sweetheart (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain misinformation or create doubt about reliable players**

---

## 🟢 Why Sweetheart is a strong bluff

- explains incorrect information
- introduces uncertainty
- fits many game states
- difficult to verify

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I was Sweetheart. Someone is drunk now.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use timing

Best used:

- after death
- when misinformation appears
- to explain contradictions

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Create doubt

You can:

- challenge trusted players
- undermine strong information
- destabilize confidence

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay vague

You should:

- avoid naming the drunk player
- let uncertainty spread

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overusing the claim

- becomes suspicious

---

### ❌ Contradicting known facts

- reduces credibility

---

### ❌ Being too specific

- limits flexibility

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Drunk effect

When Sweetheart dies:

- one player becomes drunk
- their ability may produce incorrect information

---

## 🧩 Hidden impact

The drunk player:

- does not know they are drunk
- may trust their information

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Sweetheart often:

- undermines trust
- creates doubt in strong information
- complicates deduction

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Sweetheart does not act while alive — it changes the game after death.**
