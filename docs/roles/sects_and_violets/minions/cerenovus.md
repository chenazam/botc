# Cerenovus (Sects and Violets)

**Role Type:** Minion  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a player & a good character: they are mad they are this character tomorrow, or might be executed.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Cerenovus is a **madness-based control role** that forces players to **publicly behave as a specific Townsfolk**.

You provide:

- behavioral constraints on players
- social disruption
- controlled misinformation

Your power lies in:

- shaping what players can safely say
- creating believable false narratives
- controlling information flow

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- madness
- control
- social-manipulation

---

# 😈 Playing Cerenovus (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just creating chaos — you are:

> **Controlling what information can safely exist in the game**

---

## 🟢 Core Ability

Each night:

- choose a player
- choose a **good character**

That player must:

- convincingly act as that character
- or risk execution

---

## 🧠 Core Strategies

---

## 🎭 1. Identity Overwrite (Basic Play)

### Concept

Force a player to:

- claim a role they do not have

---

### Example

- target Alice
- choose “Dreamer”

Now:

- Alice must act like Dreamer
- or risk execution

---

### Use case

- create fake claims
- introduce confusion
- disrupt information

---

## 🧠 2. Information Suppression (Advanced)

### Concept

Force a real Townsfolk to:

- stop sharing correct information

---

### Example

- target real Dreamer
- force them to be “Clockmaker”

Now:

- they cannot safely share Dreamer info
- their real info is suppressed

---

👉 This is MUCH stronger than random chaos

---

## 🧠 3. Role Collision

### Concept

Force a player into:

- an already claimed role

---

### Example

- Bob claims Empath
- you make Alice “Empath”

Now:

- double claim
- confusion
- suspicion

---

👉 You create conflict, not randomness

---

## 🧠 4. Frame a Player

### Concept

Force someone into a role that:

- makes them look suspicious

---

### Example

- make someone “Savant”  
  → messy, hard-to-explain info

- make someone “Juggler”  
  → weird Day 1 behavior

---

👉 You shape how they are perceived

---

## 🧠 5. Protect Your Team

### Concept

Control what your Demon / Minions must claim

---

### Example

- force your Demon into a safe role
- give them a consistent cover

---

👉 You reduce risk for your team

---

## 🧠 6. Endgame Control

### Concept

Force critical players into:

- roles that limit their influence

---

### Example

- force key player into a weak or confusing role
- prevent clear reasoning

---

👉 You shape final decisions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Random targeting

- wastes your power

---

### ❌ Overusing Mutant

- predictable
- loses effectiveness

---

### ❌ Ignoring game state

- reduces impact

---

### ❌ Breaking madness too obviously

- makes your ability visible

---

# 🟢 Bluffing Cerenovus (Good)

_(rare but possible via Philosopher / madness interactions)_

## 🎯 Your Role

As good, your goal is to:

> **Explain strange behavior or forced claims**

---

## 🧩 Use case

- explain inconsistent players
- justify strange claims
- create alternative worlds

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Madness behavior

Madness requires:

- convincing behavior
- not just statements

The Storyteller evaluates:

- whether the player is believable

---

## 🧩 Execution risk

If madness is broken:

- player may be executed

This creates:

- real pressure
- social consequences

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Cerenovus often:

- creates false claims
- suppresses true information
- generates social tension

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Cerenovus is not about making players lie — it is about controlling what truths are allowed to exist.**
