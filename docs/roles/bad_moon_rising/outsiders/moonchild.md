# Moonchild (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** When you learn that you died, publicly choose 1 player: if they are good, they die too.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Moonchild is a **death-triggered Outsider** that can cause an additional death based on a **public accusation**.

You provide:

- a delayed extra kill
- strong social pressure
- information through your choice

Your impact lies in:

- influencing behavior while alive
- shaping how your death is interpreted
- creating consequences for trust and suspicion

---

# 🟢 Playing Moonchild (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just choosing someone — you are:

> **Turning your death into a powerful statement about the game**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Build a read on the table

Default mindset:

> “If I die, I need to know who I trust the least.”

Why:

- your ability depends on your judgment
- your choice has real consequences
- preparation matters

---

### 2. Be aware of your influence

While alive:

- players will consider your potential choice
- your suspicions matter more
- your interactions carry extra weight

---

### 3. Consider your claim timing

Early claim can:

- create pressure
- influence behavior

But also:

- make you a target
- attract manipulation

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Prepare for your death

Move from:

> “I’ll decide when I die.”

to:

> **“I already know who I’ll choose if I die.”**

---

### Make a meaningful choice

When you die:

- choose based on your best read
- consider how your choice affects the game
- avoid random or emotional picks

---

### Communicate your reasoning

Your choice is more valuable when:

- explained clearly
- tied to your logic
- integrated into the game state

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Choosing randomly

Your choice can kill a good player — it must be thoughtful.

---

### ❌ Letting emotions decide

Frustration or pressure can lead to poor decisions.

---

### ❌ Ignoring social impact

Your threat influences how others play around you.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Moonchild (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create pressure and control behavior through your potential choice**

---

## 🟢 Why Moonchild is a strong bluff

- creates immediate social pressure
- influences how players interact with you
- can protect teammates
- introduces fear and hesitation

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “If I die, I’m choosing X.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use threat effectively

### 🟢 Good approach

- threaten players you want to pressure
- protect teammates indirectly
- influence execution decisions

---

### ⚠️ Risky approach

- threatening too broadly
- making inconsistent claims

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- threats that contradict your behavior
- choices that make no strategic sense

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose targets strategically

### 🟢 Good targets

- players you want to eliminate
- players who threaten your team
- players already under suspicion

---

### 🔴 Risky targets

- players strongly confirmed as good
- players whose death would expose you

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control your narrative

You should guide the table toward:

- taking your threat seriously
- reacting to your potential choice
- altering behavior around you

---

### Example plays

- “If I die, I’m taking them with me.”
- “You don’t want to execute me if you’re good.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

- adjust your threats
- adapt to the game state
- avoid locking into one choice too early

---

### Use your claim strategically

- protect teammates
- redirect suspicion
- influence execution outcomes

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Empty threats

If your threats don’t align with your behavior, they lose power.

---

### ❌ Poor target choice

A bad choice can hurt your team or expose your bluff.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

Flexibility is key to maintaining credibility.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Death-triggered ability

When you die:

- you must choose a player publicly
- if they are good, they die
- if they are evil, nothing happens

---

## 🧩 Social leverage

Moonchild is one of the strongest roles for:

- influencing behavior
- creating pressure
- shaping social dynamics

---

## 🧠 Moonchild in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- multiple deaths
- volatile nights
- complex interactions

Moonchild adds:

- player-driven consequences
- delayed effects
- another layer of death interpretation

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Moonchild is not about who you kill — it is about how your potential to kill shapes the game before you die.**
