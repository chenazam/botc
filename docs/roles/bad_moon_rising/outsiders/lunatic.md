# Lunatic (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** You think you are a Demon, but you are not. The Demon knows who you are & who you choose at night.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Lunatic is an **illusion-based Outsider** who believes they are the Demon, while the real Demon observes and can react to their choices.

You provide:

- false Demon information
- misdirected kills
- confusion for both yourself and the town

Your impact lies in:

- acting as a fake Demon
- influencing real Demon decisions
- creating chaos in deduction and trust

---

# 🟢 Playing Lunatic (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just mistaken — you are:

> **A fake Demon whose actions can still influence the real one**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Accept uncertainty

Default mindset:

> “I might not actually be the Demon.”

You should consider:

- your information may be false
- your minions may not be real
- your kills may not be happening

---

### 2. Test your reality

You should:

- compare expected kills with actual deaths
- observe whether your choices match outcomes
- look for inconsistencies

---

### 3. Communicate carefully

You can:

- hint at your situation
- share inconsistencies
- test reactions

But avoid:

- full commitment too early
- blindly trusting your perspective

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Confirm or reject your identity

Move from:

> “I am the Demon.”

to:

> **“Does the game behave as if I am the Demon?”**

---

### Use discrepancies as information

If:

- your kills don’t match deaths
- outcomes don’t align

Then:

- you are likely the Lunatic
- your information is unreliable

---

### Transition to helping the town

Once confirmed:

- share what you know
- explain your experience
- help interpret your actions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Fully committing to being the Demon

This can mislead both you and the town.

---

### ❌ Ignoring inconsistencies

The game will give you signals — pay attention.

---

### ❌ Withholding information too long

Your perspective is valuable once understood.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Lunatic (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Use the Lunatic claim to explain confusion and control narrative**

---

## 🟢 Why Lunatic is a strong bluff

- explains inconsistent information
- justifies strange behavior
- creates uncertainty
- fits naturally into BMR chaos

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I thought I was the Demon, but something didn’t add up.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Build the narrative

### 🟢 Good approach

- describe your “experience”
- explain mismatches in kills
- present your realization

---

### ⚠️ Risky approach

- overcomplicating the story
- contradicting known events

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claims that cannot fit the game state
- unnecessary detail

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use confusion strategically

You should:

- disrupt clean worlds
- create doubt
- redirect suspicion

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay believable

Your story should:

- evolve naturally
- align with observed outcomes
- remain internally consistent

---

### Example plays

- “I thought I was killing people, but it didn’t match.”
- “That’s why my perspective has been off.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

- adapt your narrative
- respond to new information
- avoid rigid claims

---

### Use your claim strategically

- explain inconsistencies
- protect teammates
- create alternative worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overcomplicating

Too much detail can make your bluff suspicious.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your story must match the game.

---

### ❌ Revealing too early

Timing matters for credibility.

---

As the Lunatic:

- you receive false information
- you believe you are the Demon
- your choices may influence the real Demon

---

## 🧩 Interaction with the real Demon

The Demon:

- knows who you are
- sees your choices
- can use your behavior to their advantage

---

In a script with:

- volatile nights
- multiple kill sources
- complex interactions

Lunatic adds:

- perspective confusion
- misaligned expectations
- psychological complexity

> **Lunatic is not about being wrong — it is about realizing you might be wrong, and using that realization correctly.**
