# Tinker (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** You might die at any time.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Tinker is a **random-death Outsider** that introduces **unpredictable deaths**, breaking clean interpretation of night outcomes.

You provide:

- an additional source of death
- uncertainty in kill patterns
- disruption of otherwise logical worlds

Your impact lies in:

- making deaths unreliable
- creating false assumptions about Demon behavior
- forcing players to question every death

---

# 🟢 Playing Tinker (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just vulnerable — you are:

> **A built-in source of chaos that makes death patterns unreliable**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide your claim strategy

Default strong play:

> “I’m an Outsider — my death might not mean what it looks like.”

Why:

- your role explains unexpected deaths
- early clarity can prevent misreads
- but full claims may attract execution

---

### 2. Prepare for sudden death

You should assume:

- you may die at any time
- your death may look like a Demon kill
- your death may mislead the town

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Use your death as information

Move from:

> “I died.”

to:

> **“What conclusions will the town draw from my death?”**

---

### Clarify your role if needed

If you die:

- your claim can explain the death
- you can prevent incorrect worlds
- you can reduce confusion

---

### Be aware of timing

Your death can:

- disrupt otherwise correct deductions
- create false suspicion
- shift the entire game state

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Not claiming when needed

If your death creates confusion, your role should explain it.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing your survival

Your role is designed to die unpredictably.

---

### ❌ Ignoring impact

Your death can mislead the game significantly if not handled well.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Tinker (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain unexpected deaths and distort how the town reads kill patterns**

---

## 🟢 Why Tinker is a strong bluff

- explains random or extra deaths
- fits naturally into BMR chaos
- removes pressure to provide information
- introduces uncertainty into death interpretation

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “That death might have been me — I’m the Tinker.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with deaths

### 🟢 Good situations

- unexplained deaths
- extra deaths
- chaotic or inconsistent nights

---

### ⚠️ Risky situations

- clean, predictable death patterns
- games with strong explanations already established

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming Tinker when deaths are clearly explained
- contradicting obvious game events

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use your claim to shape interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- questioning whether deaths were Demon kills
- reconsidering kill patterns
- avoiding clean conclusions

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay flexible

Tinker works best when:

- multiple explanations remain possible
- you avoid rigid narratives
- uncertainty persists

---

### Example plays

- “That death doesn’t have to be the Demon.”
- “If I’m Tinker, that explains the extra death.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay adaptive

- shift your explanation as needed
- align with evolving death patterns
- avoid contradictions

---

### Use your claim strategically

- protect teammates
- disrupt correct deductions
- create alternative worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overusing the explanation

Not every death should be attributed to Tinker.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your claim must still fit the broader game state.

---

### ❌ Claiming too late

If the narrative is already set, your bluff may not work.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Random death timing

Tinker can die:

- during the night
- outside of normal kill patterns
- at moments that disrupt expectations

---

## 🧩 Death ambiguity

Tinker introduces:

- uncertainty in kill counts
- confusion about cause of death
- difficulty in reading the night

---

## 🧠 Tinker in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- multiple kill sources
- protection
- volatile nights

Tinker adds:

- another independent cause of death
- further distortion of patterns
- increased interpretive complexity

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Tinker is not about dying — it is about making every death harder to understand.**
