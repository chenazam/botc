# Goon (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** Each night, the first player to choose you with their ability makes you drunk until dusk. You become their alignment.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Goon is an **alignment-shifting Outsider** that changes alignment based on who targets them, while also becoming drunk.

You provide:

- dynamic alignment changes
- disruption of information roles
- uncertainty in trust and identity

Your impact lies in:

- breaking assumptions about alignment
- interfering with abilities
- creating shifting, unstable worlds

---

# 🟢 Playing Goon (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just a player — you are:

> **A moving variable that changes alignment and disrupts the game state**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Understand your volatility

Default mindset:

> “My alignment may change at any time.”

You must assume:

- you may become evil
- you may become good again
- your information may be unreliable

---

### 2. Decide your claim strategy

Early claim can:

- explain strange behavior
- help others interpret results

But also:

- make you a target
- attract manipulation

---

### 3. Track who targets you

You should:

- note who might be using abilities on you
- observe patterns in night outcomes
- consider how your alignment may have changed

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Track your alignment shifts

Move from:

> “I think I’m good.”

to:

> **“When did I last become good or evil?”**

---

### Interpret your own information carefully

You may:

- be drunk
- have been evil at the time
- have influenced other players’ results

---

### Communicate uncertainty

You should:

- be transparent about your situation
- explain possible alignment changes
- avoid overclaiming certainty

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Assuming stable alignment

You may not be on the same team as before.

---

### ❌ Trusting your own information blindly

Your results may be wrong due to drunkenness.

---

### ❌ Ignoring your impact

You may be affecting other players’ abilities and conclusions.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Alignment change mechanics

When targeted:

- you become drunk
- you adopt the alignment of the player who targeted you

This creates:

- shifting allegiance
- unreliable information
- unpredictable outcomes

---

## 🧩 Interaction with other roles

Goon affects:

- information roles (results may be wrong)
- protection roles (targets may change alignment)
- deduction (alignment is unstable)

---

## 🧠 Goon in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- complex interactions
- multiple overlapping effects
- volatile game states

Goon adds:

- alignment instability
- additional uncertainty
- another layer of complexity

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Goon is not about what team you are on — it is about how often that answer changes.**
