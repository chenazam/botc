# Devil's Advocate (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Minion  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a living player (different to last night): if executed tomorrow, they don't die.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Devil’s Advocate is an **execution-protection Minion** that prevents a player from dying if they are executed, creating **massive ambiguity around execution results**.

You provide:

- targeted execution protection
- disruption of confirmation mechanics
- control over who survives the day

Your power lies in:

- protecting key players (often the Demon)
- breaking execution-based logic
- creating confusion around alignment

---

# 😈 Playing Devil’s Advocate (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just protecting players — you are:

> **Making executions unreliable at the most critical moments**

---

## 🟢 Early Game Strategy

### 1. Choose your first targets carefully

Default strong play:

> “I want to protect someone likely to be executed.”

You should:

- identify early suspicion targets
- anticipate execution candidates
- protect high-value teammates

---

### 2. Protect subtly

You want:

- your protection to look like something else
- ambiguity around why someone survived
- the town to misinterpret the result

---

### 3. Coordinate with your team

You should:

- align with the Demon’s plans
- avoid conflicting effects
- maximize confusion

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Break execution logic

Move from:

> “They were executed and lived.”

to:

> **“Why didn’t they die?”**

You want the answer to be unclear.

---

### Protect key players

Strong plays often involve:

- saving the Demon from execution
- preserving a key Minion
- disrupting a confirmed-good narrative

---

### Use timing strategically

Your protection is strongest when:

- used on critical executions
- applied unpredictably
- combined with other effects

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Predictable protection

If the same type of player always survives, suspicion builds quickly.

---

### ❌ Wasting protection

Saving low-impact players reduces your influence.

---

### ❌ Making it obvious

If the town clearly identifies Devil’s Advocate, your power weakens.

---

# 🟢 Bluffing as Good (Devil’s Advocate Cover)

## 🎯 Your Goal

As evil, your bluff should:

> **Explain why an executed player survived**

---

## 🟢 Strong bluff choices

Devil’s Advocate pairs well with:

- Pacifist (execution survival)
- Tea Lady (conditional protection)
- Sailor / Innkeeper (general survival ambiguity)

---

## 🧩 Bluff Strategy

You should:

- attribute survival to other roles
- reinforce uncertainty
- avoid drawing attention to execution protection

---

### Example plays

- “That could have been Pacifist.”
- “There are multiple reasons they might have lived.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game Bluffing

### Stay subtle

- let others suggest explanations
- avoid claiming credit
- support believable alternatives

---

### Use your position carefully

- guide interpretation without controlling it
- avoid appearing too informed
- maintain flexibility

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Bluffing)

### ❌ Overexplaining

Too much explanation makes you suspicious.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your story must align with observed outcomes.

---

### ❌ Claiming responsibility

Revealing your role removes ambiguity.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Execution protection

If your target is executed:

- they do not die
- the execution still resolves
- the result becomes ambiguous

---

## 🧩 Interaction with other roles

Devil’s Advocate overlaps with:

- Pacifist (execution survival)
- Tea Lady (conditional protection)
- other protection roles

This creates multiple explanations for the same outcome.

---

## 🧠 Devil’s Advocate in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- volatile deaths
- multiple protection effects
- complex interpretation

Devil’s Advocate adds:

- targeted execution disruption
- another layer of ambiguity
- increased difficulty in confirming alignment

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Devil’s Advocate is not about saving someone — it is about making their survival impossible to interpret cleanly.**
