# Mastermind (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Minion  
**Ability:** If the Demon dies by execution, play for 1 more day. If a player is then executed, their team loses.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Mastermind is a **game-extension Minion** that creates a **false victory state**, forcing one final execution that determines the winner.

You provide:

- a hidden extra day
- disruption of endgame certainty
- a final high-stakes decision point

Your power lies in:

- turning a “win” into uncertainty
- forcing the town into a risky final execution
- creating one of the most dramatic moments in the game

---

# 😈 Playing Mastermind (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just extending the game — you are:

> **Turning the town’s victory into their final test**

---

## 🟢 Early Game Strategy

### 1. Stay hidden

Default mindset:

> “My power only matters at the end.”

You should:

- avoid drawing attention
- survive as long as possible
- maintain flexibility

---

### 2. Support your team normally

Before the endgame:

- play like a standard Minion
- support the Demon
- help create confusion

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Prepare for the endgame

You should:

- think about how the game might end
- consider execution patterns
- anticipate final scenarios

---

### Stay alive

Your value depends on:

- being alive when the Demon dies
- remaining undetected
- reaching the final phase

---

## 🧠 Endgame Strategy

### Trigger condition

If the Demon is executed:

- the game continues
- the town believes they may have won
- a final execution decides everything

---

### Control the final execution

Move from:

> “The Demon is dead.”

to:

> **“Now we need to make them execute the wrong player.”**

---

### Use confusion

You should:

- introduce doubt about the Demon execution
- suggest alternative explanations
- push suspicion onto good players

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Revealing too early

If the town knows you exist, your power weakens.

---

### ❌ Dying before the endgame

Your ability only matters if you survive.

---

### ❌ Poor final manipulation

The last execution is everything — you must control it.

---

# 🟢 Bluffing as Good (Mastermind Cover)

## 🎯 Your Goal

As evil, your bluff should:

> **Explain why the game continues after a Demon execution**

---

## 🟢 Strong bluff choices

Mastermind pairs well with:

- roles that create uncertainty
- roles that explain unusual outcomes
- roles that fit chaotic endgames

---

## 🧩 Bluff Strategy

You should:

- suggest alternative explanations
- question whether the Demon actually died
- create doubt in the town’s conclusion

---

### Example plays

- “Are we sure that was the Demon?”
- “Something doesn’t add up.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game Bluffing

### Stay subtle

- avoid revealing your role
- let confusion grow naturally
- support multiple interpretations

---

### Use your position carefully

- guide discussion without controlling it
- avoid appearing too certain
- maintain flexibility

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Bluffing)

### ❌ Overexplaining

Too much clarity reduces your advantage.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your narrative must still fit the game state.

---

### ❌ Forcing conclusions

Let the town doubt itself.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 False victory state

When triggered:

- the game appears to be over
- but continues for one more day
- the next execution decides the winner

---

## 🧩 Final execution logic

On the extra day:

- if a good player is executed → evil wins
- if an evil player is executed → good wins

This creates a high-pressure final decision.

---

## 🧠 Mastermind in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- volatile game states
- complex interactions
- unclear outcomes

Mastermind adds:

- endgame deception
- delayed resolution
- maximum tension

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Mastermind is not about extending the game — it is about turning certainty into doubt at the final moment.**
