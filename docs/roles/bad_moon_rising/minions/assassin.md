# Assassin (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Minion  
**Ability:** Once per game, at night\*, choose a player: they die, even if for some reason they could not.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Assassin is a **guaranteed-kill Minion** that can eliminate any player, ignoring protection and normal limitations.

You provide:

- a reliable, unstoppable kill
- the ability to bypass protection
- a powerful tempo swing

Your power lies in:

- removing key players at critical moments
- breaking otherwise safe situations
- creating unexpected deaths

---

# 😈 Playing Assassin (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just killing a player — you are:

> **Overriding the game’s rules at the most important moment**

---

## 🟢 Early Game Strategy

### 1. Do not rush your ability

Default strong play:

> “I will wait until my kill has maximum impact.”

Why:

- you only get one use
- early kills are often low value
- late kills can decide the game

---

### 2. Identify high-value targets

You should look for:

- confirmed or trusted players
- strong information roles
- players controlling the narrative

---

### 3. Coordinate with your team

You should:

- align with the Demon’s kills
- avoid redundant targeting
- plan for maximum disruption

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Choose the right moment

Move from:

> “I can kill anyone.”

to:

> **“Killing this player now breaks the game in our favor.”**

---

### Break protection

Strong Assassin plays often:

- kill protected players
- bypass defensive roles
- invalidate safe assumptions

---

### Create impact

Your kill should:

- disrupt a stable world
- remove a key threat
- shift the game’s direction

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Using it too early

Early kills rarely maximize your impact.

---

### ❌ Targeting low-value players

Your ability is too powerful to waste.

---

### ❌ Poor coordination

Overlapping with the Demon reduces efficiency.

---

# 🟢 Bluffing as Good (Assassin Cover)

## 🎯 Your Goal

As evil, your bluff should:

> **Explain unexpected or “impossible” deaths**

---

## 🟢 Strong bluff choices

Assassin pairs well with:

- Gambler (self-caused death)
- Gossip (conditional death)
- Tinker (random death)

---

## 🧩 Bluff Strategy

You should:

- attribute deaths to other roles
- reinforce uncertainty
- avoid revealing a clear cause

---

### Example plays

- “That could have been Gossip.”
- “There are multiple explanations for that death.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game Bluffing

### Stay subtle

- do not claim responsibility
- let chaos explain the kill
- support believable alternatives

---

### Use your position carefully

- guide interpretation indirectly
- avoid appearing too powerful
- maintain flexibility

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Bluffing)

### ❌ Overexplaining

The kill should feel unexplained, not forced.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your explanation must fit the game state.

---

### ❌ Drawing attention

If the town suspects Assassin, your advantage decreases.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Guaranteed kill

Your ability:

- ignores protection
- bypasses survival effects
- ensures the target dies

---

## 🧩 Interaction with protection

Assassin cuts through:

- Sailor
- Innkeeper
- Tea Lady
- Fool

This makes it uniquely powerful.

---

## 🧠 Assassin in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- heavy protection
- multiple survival effects
- complex night outcomes

Assassin adds:

- certainty
- decisive action
- a break in the chaos

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Assassin is not about killing anyone — it is about killing the one person the game says should survive.**
