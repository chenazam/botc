# Shabaloth (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Demon  
**Ability:** Each night\*, choose 2 players: they die. A dead player you chose last night might be resurrected.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Shabaloth is a **high-chaos Demon** that creates **multiple deaths and possible resurrection**, making night outcomes extremely difficult to interpret.

You provide:

- two kills per night
- potential resurrection of a previous victim
- massive disruption of death-based logic

Your power lies in:

- overwhelming the town with deaths
- reversing deaths unpredictably
- breaking assumptions about finality

---

# 😈 Playing Shabaloth (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just killing players — you are:

> **Overloading the game state with deaths while selectively undoing them**

---

## 🟢 Early Game Strategy

### 1. Use your double kill wisely

Default mindset:

> “Two deaths give me control over tempo.”

You should:

- remove key players quickly
- create pressure early
- shape the pace of the game

---

### 2. Plan for resurrection

You should consider:

- who you might want to bring back
- how resurrection will be interpreted
- how it affects trust and information

---

### 3. Blend chaos into the script

You want:

- your actions to feel consistent with BMR chaos
- multiple explanations for outcomes
- the town to struggle with interpretation

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Control tempo aggressively

Move from:

> “I kill two players.”

to:

> **“Which two deaths create the most confusion or impact?”**

---

### Use resurrection strategically

Strong Shabaloth play often:

- revives a player to create confusion
- restores a player to manipulate trust
- disrupts otherwise stable worlds

---

### Shape narrative through death patterns

You can:

- create misleading kill patterns
- reinforce false worlds
- break correct deductions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Killing randomly

Your double kill is powerful — it must be intentional.

---

### ❌ Ignoring resurrection value

Revival is a key part of your strength.

---

### ❌ Creating obvious patterns

Even chaos must feel natural.

---

# 🟢 Bluffing as Good (Shabaloth Cover)

## 🎯 Your Goal

As the Demon, your bluff should:

> **Explain multiple deaths and possible resurrection through other roles**

Shabaloth pairs well with:

---

- adapt your narrative

- support multiple possibilities

---

### Use your position carefully

- guide interpretation subtly
- avoid drawing attention
- maintain ambiguity

---

Your explanations must still fit the game.

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Double kill + resurrection

Each night:

- you choose 2 players to die
- one previously killed player may return

This creates:

- fluctuating player counts
- unclear cause of death
- disrupted timelines

---

## 🧩 Interaction with BMR roles

Shabaloth overlaps with:

- Gossip / Gambler (extra deaths)
- Professor (revival)
- protection roles (survival ambiguity)

---

## 🧠 Shabaloth in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- volatile nights
- multiple death sources
- complex interactions

Shabaloth adds:

- maximum chaos
- death reversal
- overwhelming complexity

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Shabaloth is not about killing more players — it is about making death itself unreliable.**
