# Zombuul (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Demon  
**Ability:** Each night*, if no-one died today, choose a player: they die. The 1st time you die, you don’t, but you appear to.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Zombuul is a **survival-based Demon** that can fake its own death, creating **false confirmations and broken endgame logic**.

You provide:

- a fake Demon death  
- control over kill timing  
- disruption of execution-based conclusions  

Your power lies in:

- surviving your own “death”  
- manipulating when kills happen  
- exploiting the town’s belief that the game is over  

---

# 😈 Playing Zombuul (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just surviving — you are:

> **Convincing the town the game is over while still being alive**

---

## 🟢 Early Game Strategy

### 1. Control death tempo

Default mindset:

> “Fewer deaths can be more powerful.”

You should:

- consider when to allow no-death days  
- manage expectations around kills  
- avoid predictable patterns  

---

### 2. Blend into the game

You want:

- to avoid early suspicion  
- to build a believable identity  
- to stay out of execution range  

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Set up your “death”

Move from:

> “I need to survive.”

to:

> **“When is the best time to appear to die?”**

---

### Use the fake death strategically

A good Zombuul “death”:

- creates a false victory state  
- removes suspicion from you  
- shifts focus onto others  

---

### Control kills after revival

After your fake death:

- your kill condition changes  
- you must manage no-death days carefully  
- your actions become more visible  

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Dying too early

If your fake death happens before the game is ready, it loses impact.

---

### ❌ Creating obvious patterns

Zombuul relies on subtlety — obvious manipulation exposes you.

---

### ❌ Poor timing

The value of your fake death depends entirely on when it happens.

---

# 🟢 Bluffing as Good (Zombuul Cover)

## 🎯 Your Goal

As the Demon, your bluff should:

> **Support the illusion that the Demon is dead — or never you**

---

## 🟢 Strong bluff choices

Zombuul pairs well with:

- roles that explain survival (Fool, Tea Lady)  
- roles that explain missing deaths (Exorcist, protection roles)  
- roles that thrive in chaos  

---

## 🧩 Bluff Strategy

You should:

- support the “Demon is dead” narrative when appropriate  
- avoid contradicting your own fake death  
- reinforce false certainty  

---

### Example plays

- “That execution must have hit the Demon.”  
- “The game should be over — something else is going on.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game Bluffing

### Stay consistent

- align your story with the game state  
- avoid contradictions  
- adapt as needed  

---

### Use your position carefully

- guide the narrative  
- avoid drawing attention  
- maintain credibility  

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Bluffing)

### ❌ Contradicting the fake death

If your actions don’t match the narrative, suspicion returns to you.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

Leave room for ambiguity.

---

### ❌ Ignoring the game state

Your bluff must match observed outcomes.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Fake death mechanic

The first time you die:

- you do not actually die  
- you appear to die  
- the game continues  

---

## 🧩 Kill condition

Zombuul only kills if:

- no one died during the day  

This creates:

- unusual kill patterns  
- strategic no-death days  
- tempo control  

---

## 🧠 Zombuul in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- protection  
- multiple death sources  
- chaotic nights  

Zombuul adds:

- false Demon death  
- broken endgame logic  
- another layer of uncertainty  

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Zombuul is not about surviving death — it is about making the town believe you didn’t.**
