# Pukka (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Demon  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a player: they are poisoned. The previously poisoned player dies, then becomes healthy.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Pukka is a **delayed-death Demon** that kills one night later, creating **predictable—but highly disruptable—death patterns**.

You provide:

- consistent delayed kills
- poisoning that distorts information
- structured but fragile night patterns

Your power lies in:

- controlling who dies _and when_
- poisoning key players before they die
- exploiting interference in the delay

---

# 😈 Playing Pukka (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just killing players — you are:

> **Creating a timeline of death that the town must try (and often fail) to track**

---

## 🟢 Early Game Strategy

### 1. Establish your kill rhythm

Default mindset:

> “Every night, I set up the next death.”

You should:

- choose targets carefully
- think one step ahead
- consider both poisoning and future death

---

### 2. Poison strategically

Your target:

- becomes poisoned immediately
- may give false information
- will likely die the following night

---

### 3. Blend your pattern into chaos

You want:

- your delayed deaths to feel natural
- interference to obscure your pattern
- the town to misread timing

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Manage the death timeline

Move from:

> “They will die next.”

to:

> **“How will this death be interpreted when it happens?”**

---

### Use poisoning actively

Strong Pukka play often:

- poisons key information roles
- disrupts important abilities
- creates misleading results

---

### Exploit interference

If something affects your target:

- protection may delay or alter outcomes
- drunkenness may distort interpretation
- your clean pattern becomes messy

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Making your pattern too obvious

A clean, predictable timeline can expose you.

---

### ❌ Poor target selection

You must think about both poisoning and future death.

---

### ❌ Ignoring interference

Other roles can break your expected pattern.

---

# 🟢 Bluffing as Good (Pukka Cover)

## 🎯 Your Goal

As the Demon, your bluff should:

> **Explain delayed or unusual deaths through other mechanics**

---

## 🟢 Strong bluff choices

Pukka pairs well with:

- Gossip (delayed or conditional deaths)
- Gambler (self-caused deaths)
- Tinker (unexpected deaths)

---

## 🧩 Bluff Strategy

You should:

- attribute deaths to other roles
- obscure the timing pattern
- reinforce alternative explanations

---

### Example plays

- “That death could have been Gossip.”
- “The timing doesn’t have to be consistent.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game Bluffing

### Stay flexible

- adapt your narrative
- avoid committing to patterns
- support multiple explanations

---

### Use your position carefully

- guide interpretation subtly
- avoid appearing too structured
- maintain ambiguity

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Bluffing)

### ❌ Ignoring timing

If your bluff contradicts the timeline, it collapses.

---

### ❌ Overexplaining

Let chaos cover your pattern.

---

### ❌ Drawing attention

The less your pattern is noticed, the better.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poison → death cycle

Each night:

1. You poison a player
2. The previously poisoned player dies
3. That player becomes healthy again

---

## 🧩 Timeline disruption

Your pattern can be altered by:

- protection roles
- drunkenness
- other effects

This creates complex interpretations.

---

## 🧠 Pukka in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- multiple death sources
- protection
- volatile nights

Pukka adds:

- delayed causality
- structured but fragile patterns
- another layer of timing complexity

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Pukka is not about who dies — it is about making the town fail to understand when and why they died.**
