# Gambler (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night\*, choose a player & guess their character: if you guess wrong, you die.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Gambler is a **high-risk, high-reward information role** that can confirm identities — at the cost of potentially killing yourself.

You provide:

- strong, direct role verification
- influence over night outcomes (through your own survival or death)

Your power lies in:

- confirming key players
- accelerating the game state
- forcing meaningful risk decisions

---

# 🟢 Playing Gambler (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just guessing roles — you are:

> **Trading your own survival for clarity in a chaotic game**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide your risk level

Default strong play:

> “I’ll only gamble when I have strong confidence.”

Why:

- random guesses are extremely dangerous
- your life is a resource
- your value increases if you survive multiple nights

---

### 2. Identify high-confidence targets

You should:

- focus on players with strong claims
- look for confirmed or near-confirmed roles
- avoid uncertain or conflicting players

---

### 3. Consider the impact of your death

You must think:

- what happens if I die tonight?
- how will that affect the game?
- will my death create confusion or clarity?

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Use your life as a resource

Move from:

> “I need to stay alive.”

to:

> **“When is it worth risking my life for information?”**

---

### Target key players

Strong Gambler plays often involve:

- confirming important Townsfolk
- testing suspicious players
- resolving critical uncertainties

---

### Control how your death is interpreted

If you die:

- the town must interpret whether it was your fault
- your guess matters
- your reasoning matters

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Gambling randomly

Low-confidence guesses are usually not worth the risk.

---

### ❌ Ignoring the impact of your death

Your death can:

- mislead the town
- create false narratives
- disrupt otherwise correct worlds

---

### ❌ Overvaluing confirmation

Not every situation requires immediate resolution.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Gambler (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain deaths and shape how the town interprets them**

---

## 🟢 Why Gambler is a strong bluff

- explains unexpected deaths
- fits naturally into BMR chaos
- allows flexible narratives
- can justify both survival and death

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I gambled on X as [role].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with deaths

### 🟢 Good situations

- nights where an extra death occurred
- nights with unclear death causes
- volatile or confusing nights

---

### ⚠️ Risky situations

- nights with very clear death explanations
- consistent patterns that contradict your claim

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claims that contradict obvious game events
- over-specific or provable statements

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose believable guesses

### 🟢 Good guesses

- roles that fit the player’s behavior
- roles already suggested by the game state
- roles that support your narrative

---

### 🔴 Risky guesses

- roles already strongly confirmed elsewhere
- roles that contradict visible information

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control death interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- seeing deaths as your mistake
- or seeing survival as your success
- questioning whether deaths were caused by you or the Demon

---

### Example plays

- “If I died, I must have been wrong.”
- “That death could have been me or the Demon.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- shift your narrative
- reinterpret past nights
- lean into ambiguity

---

### Use your claim strategically

- explain extra deaths
- create confusion about cause of death
- reinforce chaotic interpretations

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your claims must align with the game’s overall death pattern.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to specific guesses

Leave room for interpretation.

---

### ❌ Misreading the game state

A bad Gambler bluff can collapse quickly if it contradicts observable events.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Death attribution

If you die:

- it may be unclear whether:
  - you guessed wrong
  - the Demon killed you

This ambiguity is central to the role.

---

## 🧩 Gambler as a tempo tool

Gambler can:

- accelerate the game
- force earlier conclusions
- disrupt slow, cautious play

---

## 🧠 Gambler in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- multiple deaths
- protection
- volatile nights

The Gambler adds:

- another possible cause of death
- another layer of uncertainty
- another reason not to trust obvious explanations

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Gambler is not just about being right — it is about deciding when being right is worth dying for.**
