# Grandmother (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** You start knowing a good player & their character. If the Demon kills them, you die too.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Grandmother is an **early confirmation and relationship role** that begins the game with a **known good player and their role**, but pays for that certainty with a dangerous death link.

You provide:

- one confirmed good player-character pair
- a strong trust anchor for the town

Your power lies in:

- establishing an early trustworthy connection
- creating a strong starting point for world-building
- forcing the Demon to weigh the cost of killing your grandchild

---

# 🟢 Playing Grandmother (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just confirming a player — you are:

> **Creating a trusted relationship that can anchor the game early**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide how widely to share your information

Default strong play:

> “I know one player and their role, and I’m checking how best to use that before I reveal everything.”

Why:

- your info is strong, but sensitive
- revealing too broadly can paint a kill target
- your role creates a dangerous death link

---

### 2. Speak to your grandchild privately

You should:

- confirm whether they understand their role
- compare what they know with what you were shown
- judge whether they seem consistent and believable

---

### 3. Think about the Demon’s incentives

You should consider:

- whether revealing the pair makes your grandchild safer or more dangerous
- whether your own death link changes the risk of open confirmation
- how much trust value you can gain before the Demon reacts

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Build from the confirmed pair

Move from:

> “I know this one player is good and this is their role.”

to:

> **“How does this confirmed player’s information shape the rest of the game?”**

---

### Use the relationship carefully

A confirmed good pair can:

- stabilize a chaotic game
- validate another information source
- become a very strong social anchor

But it can also:

- attract the Demon
- force a double death if handled too openly

---

### Decide when full public reveal is worth it

In some games:

- a full reveal creates a powerful trusted core

In others:

- staying partially hidden protects both of you longer

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Revealing too fast

Your information is strong, but it also creates a very visible target.

---

### ❌ Treating the pair as the whole game

Your confirmed player is a starting point, not a complete solution.

---

### ❌ Ignoring the death link

If the Demon kills your grandchild, you die too. That changes how aggressively you should expose the connection.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Grandmother (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create a believable confirmed-good relationship that shapes trust and kill interpretation**

---

## 🟢 Why Grandmother is a strong bluff

- it creates immediate social credibility
- it explains strong trust in another player
- it gives you a reason to support a teammate
- the death link creates believable tension

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Grandmother. I know that X is [role].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your “grandchild” carefully

### 🟢 One evil teammate

- gives you a built-in reason to trust and defend them
- helps create a believable good core

---

### ⚠️ One good player

- can work if you want to tie yourself to a trusted player
- riskier if they later expose inconsistencies

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- choosing a player whose actual claim is likely to conflict quickly
- choosing a player who is already central to a different confirmed world

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose the role carefully

### 🟢 Safe choices

- a role that fits their likely behavior
- a role that is useful but not too easy to hard-check

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- roles that create immediate mechanical expectations
- roles already strongly implied elsewhere

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use the relationship to shape trust

You should guide the table toward:

- trusting your chosen player
- reading your defense of them as mechanical, not suspicious
- seeing you as part of a believable good core

---

### Example plays

- “I trust them because I started knowing them.”
- “If they die and I die too, that should tell you something.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- reduce how hard you lean on the relationship
- suggest interference or misinformation if needed
- shift from “certainty” to “best explanation”

---

### Use your claim strategically

- defend a teammate with built-in justification
- explain why you are hard-clearing someone
- shape how night deaths are interpreted

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Picking a bad grandchild

If your chosen player does not fit the story, the bluff collapses fast.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to certainty

Grandmother is strong, but hard certainty can become suspicious if the game shifts.

---

### ❌ Ignoring the death-link narrative

If the pair is exposed, you must account for how the Demon would or would not interact with it.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Death-link interaction

If the Demon kills your grandchild, you die too.

This means:

- the pair can become a powerful confirmation engine
- but also a dangerous liability
- and the Demon’s kill choices around the pair become highly meaningful

---

## 🧩 Grandmother as a trust anchor

Grandmother is one of the clearest “trusted pair” roles in Bad Moon Rising.

That can:

- stabilize deduction
- create a strong social center
- or bait the Demon into awkward kills

---

## 🧠 Grandmother in BMR specifically

Because Bad Moon Rising is so focused on night outcomes, Grandmother changes not just trust, but also:

- how deaths are read
- how protection is prioritized
- how much weight is given to known good players

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Grandmother is not just about knowing one good player — it is about deciding how much certainty the town can safely afford.**
