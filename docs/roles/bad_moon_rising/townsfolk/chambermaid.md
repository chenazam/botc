# Chambermaid (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, choose 2 alive players (not yourself): you learn how many woke tonight due to their ability.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Chambermaid is a **behavior-tracking information role** that detects **night activity**, not alignment or identity.

You provide:

- information about who acted at night
- a way to cross-check role claims
- insight into the structure of the night

Your power lies in:

- validating or contradicting claims
- identifying active vs inactive roles
- building patterns over multiple nights

---

# 🟢 Playing Chambermaid (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just gathering information — you are:

> **Tracking who is actually participating in the night**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Choose targets strategically

Default strong play:

> “I’m checking two players who are likely to have night actions.”

Why:

- your ability is strongest when testing meaningful targets
- random checks are low value
- you want to validate real claims early

---

### 2. Focus on claimed roles

You should:

- check players who claim night actions
- compare results against expected behavior
- identify inconsistencies early

---

### 3. Start building a pattern

Your strength is not one result — it is:

- repeated checks
- evolving information
- consistency over time

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Build consistency across nights

Move from:

> “This player woke (or didn’t) last night.”

to:

> **“Does this player’s behavior match their claimed role over time?”**

---

### Use pair comparisons

Your ability shines when:

- comparing two players directly
- testing competing claims
- checking suspicious pairs

---

### Interpret results carefully

A result of:

- **0** → neither player woke
- **1** → exactly one player woke
- **2** → both players woke

But:

- “woke” ≠ “used ability successfully”
- drunkenness or interference may apply

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Checking random players

You need intentional targets — otherwise your information has little value.

---

### ❌ Treating one result as conclusive

Your power comes from patterns, not single data points.

---

### ❌ Ignoring interference

Players may:

- be drunk
- be blocked
- behave differently than expected

---

# 😈 Bluffing Chambermaid (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create believable night-activity patterns that support your team’s claims**

---

## 🟢 Why Chambermaid is a strong bluff

- produces flexible numeric information
- is hard to directly verify
- fits naturally into BMR’s night-heavy structure
- allows controlled narrative shaping

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I checked X and Y — I got [0/1/2].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose believable targets

### 🟢 Good targets

- players with known or plausible night actions
- players central to the game
- players whose behavior you want to shape

---

### ⚠️ Risky targets

- players whose activity is already well established
- combinations that contradict obvious patterns

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claims that conflict with known role behavior
- impossible or inconsistent results

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use results to shape the narrative

### 🟢 Good approach

- support your Demon or Minions
- cast doubt on key Townsfolk
- reinforce ambiguity

---

### 🔴 Risky approach

- overcommitting to rigid interpretations
- making your results too precise or restrictive

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay flexible

Chambermaid bluff works best when:

- results evolve naturally
- you adapt to new information
- you avoid locking yourself into one story

---

### Example plays

- “At least one of them is lying about waking.”
- “That pair doesn’t behave how I’d expect.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay adaptive

If your bluff weakens:

- reinterpret earlier results
- introduce uncertainty
- shift focus to patterns rather than specifics

---

### Use your claim strategically

- challenge conflicting claims
- support your team’s narrative
- create doubt in otherwise clean worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Giving impossible results

You must understand which roles wake and when.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to one interpretation

Leave room for ambiguity — it’s your strength.

---

### ❌ Ignoring evolving game state

Your results must still make sense as the game develops.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 “Waking” vs. “acting”

A player counts as waking if:

- they are prompted to act at night

Even if:

- their ability fails
- they are drunk
- the outcome is unusual

---

## 🧩 Interaction with BMR roles

In Bad Moon Rising:

- many roles act at night
- multiple players may wake
- patterns can be complex

This makes Chambermaid:

- powerful but difficult to interpret

---

## 🧠 Pattern-based information

Chambermaid is strongest when:

- results are tracked across multiple nights
- comparisons are intentional
- conclusions are drawn gradually

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Chambermaid is not about single results — it is about building a consistent picture of who is truly active at night.**
