# Professor (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, at night\*, choose a dead player: if they are a Townsfolk, they are resurrected.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Professor is a **revival role** that can bring a Townsfolk back to life, introducing **reversal of death and major game-state shifts**.

You provide:

- the ability to undo a death
- a powerful confirmation opportunity
- a way to reintroduce key information

Your power lies in:

- choosing the right player
- timing your revival
- controlling how the resurrection is interpreted

---

# 🟢 Playing Professor (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just reviving a player — you are:

> **Rewriting the game state by bringing someone back at the right moment**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Do not rush your ability

Default strong play:

> “I will wait until a high-value target dies.”

Why:

- your ability is single-use
- early revives are often low impact
- later revives can swing the game

---

### 2. Identify high-value Townsfolk

You should look for:

- strong information roles
- trusted or confirmed players
- players whose return would meaningfully change the game

---

### 3. Track deaths carefully

You need to understand:

- who died
- why they likely died
- what their return would imply

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Choose the right revival target

Move from:

> “This player was good.”

to:

> **“Reviving this player changes the game in a meaningful way.”**

---

### Time your ability carefully

Strong Professor plays often:

- revive a key information role
- restore a trusted player
- disrupt the Demon’s plan

---

### Control the narrative

When someone is revived:

- explain why you chose them
- guide how the town interprets their return
- integrate them back into the current world

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Reviving too early

Early revives often lack impact and waste your ability.

---

### ❌ Choosing low-impact targets

Reviving the wrong player can fail to shift the game.

---

### ❌ Ignoring narrative impact

A revival changes how the entire game is read — you must help the town understand it.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Professor (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain unexpected revivals or claim control over them**

---

## 🟢 Why Professor is a strong bluff

- explains a player returning to life
- fits naturally into BMR’s chaotic structure
- introduces major uncertainty
- is difficult to verify directly

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I revived X last night.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with the game state

### 🟢 Good situations

- when a player returns unexpectedly
- when the game is already chaotic
- when multiple explanations are possible

---

### ⚠️ Risky situations

- when no revival occurred
- when the game is otherwise clean

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming a revival that did not happen
- contradicting visible game events

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose believable targets

### 🟢 Good targets

- players who were valuable
- players whose return makes sense
- players central to the game

---

### 🔴 Risky targets

- players whose revival would be implausible
- players whose role conflicts with your claim

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Shape interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- trusting your explanation of the revival
- interpreting the revived player in a way that benefits your team
- maintaining uncertainty where needed

---

### Example plays

- “I brought them back because they were important.”
- “Their return should help us solve the game.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- adjust your explanation
- introduce alternative interpretations
- avoid overcommitting

---

### Use your claim strategically

- explain unexpected events
- influence trust in revived players
- disrupt otherwise stable worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Claiming impossible revivals

Your claim must match what actually happened.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to the revived player

If they behave inconsistently, your bluff collapses.

---

### ❌ Ignoring timing

A poorly timed claim can feel unnatural or suspicious.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Revival mechanics

If you choose a dead Townsfolk:

- they return to life
- they re-enter the game as a normal player
- their information and role continue

---

## 🧩 Revival as confirmation

A successful revival can:

- strongly suggest the revived player is Townsfolk
- create a powerful trust anchor
- reshape the game’s social structure

---

## 🧠 Professor in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- volatile deaths
- multiple kill sources
- uncertain outcomes

Professor adds:

- reversal of death
- disruption of finality
- another layer of interpretation

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Professor is not about bringing someone back — it is about choosing the moment when bringing them back changes everything.**
