# Innkeeper (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night\*, choose 2 players: they can't die tonight, but 1 is drunk until dusk.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Innkeeper is a **dual-target protection role** that prevents death while introducing **hidden drunkenness**, creating extremely ambiguous night outcomes.

You provide:

- strong multi-target protection
- control over who survives
- indirect information through survival patterns

Your power lies in:

- blocking Demon kills
- influencing tempo and death count
- forcing complex interpretations of the night

---

# 🟢 Playing Innkeeper (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just protecting players — you are:

> **Shaping the night while making its outcome harder to interpret**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Choose targets with intention

Default strong play:

> “I’m covering players likely to be attacked or important to keep alive.”

Why:

- your ability is strongest when protecting valuable targets
- random picks reduce impact
- you want interpretable outcomes

---

### 2. Consider combinations, not individuals

You should:

- think about how two players interact
- consider what their survival would imply
- avoid pairing players that create impossible reads

---

### 3. Accept built-in uncertainty

You must assume:

- one of your targets is drunk
- your protection may not behave as expected
- survival is not clean confirmation

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Interpret survival carefully

Move from:

> “They survived, so I protected them.”

to:

> **“What are all the possible reasons they survived?”**

---

### Use repeated patterns

Over multiple nights:

- consistent survival may indicate protection
- inconsistent outcomes may indicate drunkenness
- patterns matter more than single nights

---

### Coordinate cautiously

You can:

- align with other protective roles
- reinforce key players

But:

- too much coordination can create misleading conclusions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Treating survival as proof

Survival can come from:

- your protection
- other protection
- Demon choice
- or drunkenness interactions

---

### ❌ Ignoring the drunk effect

One of your targets is drunk — this can:

- break their ability
- distort information
- create misleading outcomes

---

### ❌ Poor target pairing

Bad combinations make the night impossible to interpret meaningfully.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Innkeeper (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain complex survival patterns while introducing controlled uncertainty**

---

## 🟢 Why Innkeeper is a strong bluff

- explains multiple survivors
- fits naturally into chaotic BMR nights
- includes built-in ambiguity (drunkenness)
- difficult to fully verify

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I protected X and Y — one of them might have been drunk.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose believable targets

### 🟢 Good targets

- players who survived unexpectedly
- players likely to be attacked
- players central to the game

---

### ⚠️ Risky targets

- players whose survival is already clearly explained
- combinations that contradict observed deaths

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claims that cannot fit the night’s death pattern
- over-specific explanations

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use ambiguity properly

### 🟢 Good approach

- lean into uncertainty
- allow multiple explanations
- avoid claiming exact outcomes

---

### 🔴 Risky approach

- claiming you know who was drunk
- treating your protection as guaranteed

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Shape night interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- accepting unclear survival
- questioning clean explanations
- avoiding rigid conclusions

---

### Example plays

- “That night could be explained by my picks… or something else.”
- “One of them being drunk complicates things.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- shift from explanation to ambiguity
- reduce reliance on specific claims
- let the night remain unclear

---

### Use your claim strategically

- explain multiple survivors
- protect teammates indirectly
- disrupt otherwise clean worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overexplaining

Innkeeper is strongest when the night stays messy.

---

### ❌ Ignoring death patterns

Your claims must still broadly fit what happened.

---

### ❌ Forcing clarity

Trying to “solve” the night weakens your bluff.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Protection + drunkenness interaction

Each night:

- both targets cannot die
- one is drunk

This creates layered outcomes:

- protection may succeed or be irrelevant
- drunkenness may affect abilities
- survival may have multiple explanations

---

## 🧩 Multi-layer ambiguity

Innkeeper often overlaps with:

- Sailor (single-target protection + drunkenness)
- Exorcist (missing kills)
- other protection roles

This creates highly complex night states.

---

## 🧠 Innkeeper in BMR specifically

In a script already defined by:

- volatile nights
- multiple deaths
- protection effects

Innkeeper amplifies:

- interpretive difficulty
- uncertainty in survival
- complexity of deduction

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Innkeeper is not about knowing who you saved — it is about making the night impossible to interpret cleanly.**
