# Tea Lady (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** If both your alive neighbors are good, they can't die.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Tea Lady is a **position-based protection role** that creates **conditional survival tied to alignment and seating**.

You provide:

- passive protection based on neighbor alignment
- information through survival _and_ failure of survival
- a strong link between seating and deduction

Your power lies in:

- creating a protected pair
- detecting evil through broken protection
- turning executions and deaths into alignment clues

---

# 🟢 Playing Tea Lady (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just protecting players — you are:

> **Turning seating relationships into alignment information**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Identify your neighbors

Default mindset:

> “My neighbors are the key to my ability.”

You should:

- immediately note who your neighbors are
- start evaluating their behavior
- consider how their alignment affects your power

---

### 2. Decide your claim timing

Early claim can:

- protect your neighbors
- create a strong trust cluster

But also:

- attract Demon attention
- invite disruption

---

### 3. Observe execution and death patterns

You should watch:

- whether your neighbors die
- whether they survive unexpectedly
- how those outcomes fit your ability

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Use survival as information

Move from:

> “My neighbors survived.”

to:

> **“What does their survival (or death) imply about their alignment?”**

---

### Pay attention to failure

If a neighbor dies:

- at least one of your neighbors is evil
- or your ability is being interfered with

This is often _more informative_ than successful protection.

---

### Build a seating-based world

Tea Lady allows you to:

- connect alignment to physical position
- validate or invalidate claims
- create strong local deductions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Assuming protection is always active

Your ability only works if **both neighbors are good**.

---

### ❌ Ignoring failed protection

A neighbor dying is often the most important signal you get.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to early reads

Early assumptions about your neighbors may be wrong.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Tea Lady (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create a believable protection narrative tied to seating**

---

## 🟢 Why Tea Lady is a strong bluff

- explains survival patterns
- ties naturally into seating
- creates believable trust clusters
- produces both positive and negative information

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “My neighbors should be protected if they’re both good.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your positioning narrative

### 🟢 Good setups

- sit between players you want to protect or defend
- create a believable “good cluster”
- align with existing trust

---

### ⚠️ Risky setups

- sitting between conflicting players
- positioning that contradicts observed deaths

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claims that are immediately disproven by neighbor deaths
- ignoring obvious contradictions

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use survival and death flexibly

### 🟢 Good approach

- explain survival as your protection
- explain death as “one of them must be evil”

---

### 🔴 Risky approach

- forcing one interpretation too strongly
- ignoring alternative explanations

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Shape seating-based logic

You should guide the table toward:

- trusting certain seating clusters
- questioning others
- interpreting deaths through your claim

---

### Example plays

- “If they lived, that supports my ability.”
- “If they died, one of them must be evil.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- shift your interpretation
- introduce interference explanations
- avoid rigid conclusions

---

### Use your claim strategically

- defend teammates
- cast suspicion on neighbors
- influence execution choices

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Ignoring obvious contradictions

If both neighbors are clearly good and one dies, your bluff collapses.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to protection

Tea Lady is conditional — use that flexibility.

---

### ❌ Misreading seating dynamics

Your claim depends heavily on positioning.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Conditional protection

Your ability only works if:

- both neighbors are good
- they are alive

This creates layered outcomes:

- protection may or may not apply
- deaths may or may not mean something
- interpretation is key

---

## 🧩 Negative information role

Tea Lady is strongest when:

- protection fails
- contradictions appear
- seating relationships are tested

---

## 🧠 Tea Lady in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- protection
- multiple deaths
- volatile nights

Tea Lady adds:

- positional logic
- alignment inference through survival
- another layer of ambiguity

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Tea Lady is strongest not when your neighbors live — but when their survival or death tells you why.**
