# Minstrel (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** When a Minion dies by execution, all other players (except Travellers) are drunk until dusk tomorrow.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Minstrel is a **global disruption role** that triggers after a Minion execution, causing a full day and night of **drunkenness across the game**.

You provide:

- a temporary reset of all abilities
- information through a “silent” night
- confirmation tied to executions

Your power lies in:

- creating a controlled disruption
- revealing information through absence
- shaping how executions are interpreted

---

# 🟢 Playing Minstrel (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just reacting to executions — you are:

> **Using a global shutdown to extract information from what does NOT happen**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Stay aware of execution candidates

Default mindset:

> “If a Minion is executed, the next cycle becomes extremely important.”

Why:

- your ability depends on execution outcomes
- you must be ready to interpret the following night
- you gain value from awareness, not action

---

### 2. Track claims carefully

You should:

- note which players rely on night abilities
- understand what should happen normally
- prepare to detect when it does not

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Identify Minion executions

Move from:

> “Someone was executed.”

to:

> **“Did that execution trigger a global shutdown?”**

---

### Read the “silent” night

If your ability triggers:

- all abilities fail
- no one acts meaningfully
- the night becomes abnormal

You should:

- compare expected vs actual outcomes
- identify inconsistencies
- use the absence of effects as information

---

### Use it as confirmation

A Minstrel-triggered cycle can:

- confirm that a Minion was executed
- invalidate certain claims
- disrupt both teams equally

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Missing the trigger

Failing to recognize when your ability has activated wastes its value.

---

### ❌ Ignoring the silent night

The absence of effects is the most important signal you get.

---

### ❌ Overinterpreting chaos

Remember:

- everything is drunk
- unusual results are expected

---

# 😈 Bluffing Minstrel (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain abnormal nights and influence how executions are interpreted**

---

## 🟢 Why Minstrel is a strong bluff

- explains nights where nothing works
- fits naturally into BMR’s chaos
- creates believable “global disruption”
- ties into execution outcomes

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “That night everything was drunk — I think we executed a Minion.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with the game state

### 🟢 Good situations

- nights with missing or strange results
- unclear or chaotic outcomes
- executions that could plausibly hit a Minion

---

### ⚠️ Risky situations

- clean, consistent nights
- clear evidence of functioning abilities

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming a shutdown when abilities clearly worked
- contradicting visible outcomes

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use execution pressure

You should:

- suggest certain executions were Minions
- guide the town toward or away from specific conclusions
- shape how deaths and results are interpreted

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- accepting the “everything was drunk” explanation
- questioning otherwise reliable information
- reconsidering previous assumptions

---

### Example plays

- “That night doesn’t make sense unless we hit a Minion.”
- “Everything failing lines up with my role.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- shift from certainty to possibility
- suggest multiple explanations
- avoid rigid claims

---

### Use your claim strategically

- explain confusing nights
- reinforce uncertainty
- influence execution logic

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your claim must fit observed behavior across the game.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

Minstrel is strongest when it introduces doubt, not certainty.

---

### ❌ Misreading executions

If the executed player is clearly not a Minion, your bluff collapses.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Global drunkenness

When triggered:

- all players (except Travellers) are drunk
- abilities do not function correctly
- information becomes unreliable

---

## 🧩 Interpreting absence

Minstrel is one of the few roles where:

> **“Nothing happened” is the most important information**

---

## 🧠 Interaction with BMR chaos

In a script already full of:

- protection
- multiple deaths
- ambiguous outcomes

Minstrel adds:

- full-system disruption
- a temporary reset
- another explanation for strange nights

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Minstrel is not about what happens — it is about understanding what should have happened, but didn’t.**
