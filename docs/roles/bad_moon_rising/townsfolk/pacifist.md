# Pacifist (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Executed good players might not die.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Pacifist is an **execution-interference role** that can prevent the death of good players, creating **ambiguity around executions**.

You provide:

- protection from execution
- disruption of execution-based information
- additional uncertainty in death interpretation

Your power lies in:

- preventing key deaths
- interfering with confirmation mechanics
- reshaping how executions are read

---

# 🟢 Playing Pacifist (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just preventing deaths — you are:

> **Turning executions into unreliable information sources**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Stay aware of execution dynamics

Default mindset:

> “Executions may not mean what they seem.”

Why:

- your ability changes how deaths are interpreted
- you gain value from observing outcomes
- you influence the game indirectly

---

### 2. Decide whether to claim

Early claim can:

- explain survival
- protect players

But also:

- reduce ambiguity
- attract attention

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Interpret survival after execution

Move from:

> “They didn’t die.”

to:

> **“Why didn’t they die?”**

Possible reasons:

- Pacifist ability
- they are evil (and protected differently)
- other effects

---

### Use failure as information

If a good player dies:

- your ability may not have triggered
- or other effects interfered

If they survive:

- your ability may have worked
- or something else happened

---

### Influence execution choices

You can:

- encourage testing players
- reduce fear of execution
- allow the town to take risks

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Assuming you always trigger

Your ability is not guaranteed.

---

### ❌ Treating survival as confirmation

Survival does not guarantee alignment.

---

### ❌ Ignoring ambiguity

Execution results are inherently unclear.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Pacifist (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain failed executions and create uncertainty around alignment**

---

## 🟢 Why Pacifist is a strong bluff

- explains why players survive execution
- disrupts confirmation mechanics
- fits naturally into BMR uncertainty
- is hard to verify

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “They didn’t die because I’m Pacifist.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with executions

### 🟢 Good situations

- a player survives execution
- unclear execution outcomes
- chaotic game states

---

### ⚠️ Risky situations

- clean executions with clear deaths
- patterns that contradict your claim

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming Pacifist when no relevant events exist

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Shape interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- questioning execution results
- avoiding firm conclusions
- reconsidering alignment

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay flexible

Pacifist works best when:

- outcomes remain ambiguous
- multiple explanations exist
- you avoid rigid claims

---

### Example plays

- “They might be good — I could have saved them.”
- “That execution doesn’t prove anything.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay adaptive

- adjust your narrative
- avoid contradictions
- lean into uncertainty

---

### Use your claim strategically

- protect teammates
- weaken confirmation
- influence execution decisions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overexplaining

Pacifist works best when ambiguity remains.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your claim must fit the game’s events.

---

### ❌ Forcing outcomes

Let the uncertainty exist naturally.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Execution ambiguity

Pacifist introduces:

- uncertainty in execution results
- difficulty in confirming alignment
- risk in relying on executions

---

## 🧩 Interaction with BMR

In a script with:

- volatile nights
- multiple death sources

Pacifist adds:

- ambiguity during the day
- disruption of confirmation
- another layer of uncertainty

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Pacifist is not about saving players — it is about making executions unreliable.**
