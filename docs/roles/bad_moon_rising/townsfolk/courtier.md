# Courtier (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, at night, choose a character: they are drunk for 3 nights & 3 days.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Courtier is a **targeted shutdown role** that can disable a specific character for an extended period.

You provide:

- long-term control over a key role
- disruption of Demon or Minion abilities
- a way to stabilize chaotic game states

Your power lies in:

- choosing the right target
- timing your ability correctly
- understanding the impact of removing a role from the game

---

# 🟢 Playing Courtier (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just choosing a target — you are:

> **Removing a key piece from the game at the most critical moment**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Do not rush your ability

Default strong play:

> “I will wait until I have a strong reason to use my ability.”

Why:

- you only get one use
- early guesses are often low-value
- better information increases impact

---

### 2. Identify high-value targets

You should think about:

- which roles are most dangerous
- which roles are most influential
- which roles are shaping the game

---

### 3. Observe the game before acting

You gain strength by:

- watching night patterns
- listening to claims
- identifying consistent threats

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Choose the right moment

Move from:

> “I think this role is strong.”

to:

> **“Shutting down this role now changes the game in our favor.”**

---

### Target with intent

Strong Courtier plays often involve:

- disabling the Demon at a critical moment
- shutting down a dangerous Minion
- neutralizing a role that is distorting the game

---

### Understand long-term impact

Your choice affects:

- multiple nights
- multiple days
- the entire tempo of the game

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Using the ability too early

Premature use often wastes your strongest tool.

---

### ❌ Targeting the wrong role

A low-impact target reduces your influence significantly.

---

### ❌ Ignoring timing

Even the correct target can be ineffective if used at the wrong time.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Courtier (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain changes in behavior or outcomes while creating a believable “shutdown” narrative**

---

## 🟢 Why Courtier is a strong bluff

- explains missing or weakened abilities
- introduces long-term effects
- fits naturally into BMR’s complexity
- is difficult to verify directly

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I used my ability on [character], they should be drunk for the next few days.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose a believable target

### 🟢 Good targets

- roles that are not visibly active
- roles that can plausibly “fail”
- roles that are difficult to confirm

---

### ⚠️ Risky targets

- roles with obvious ongoing effects
- roles strongly confirmed by the group

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- targets that contradict clear game evidence
- claims that can be immediately disproven

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Align with the game state

### 🟢 Good situations

- when a role appears to stop working
- when confusion already exists
- when multiple explanations are possible

---

### 🔴 Risky situations

- when the game is clean and consistent
- when your claim introduces contradictions

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use your claim strategically

You should:

- explain unusual behavior
- weaken strong Townsfolk claims
- justify inconsistencies

---

### Example plays

- “If they’re not getting results, it’s because I hit their role.”
- “That role hasn’t been working — this explains it.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- reinterpret your target
- suggest alternative explanations
- reduce reliance on the claim

---

### Use your claim carefully

- reinforce uncertainty
- support your team’s narrative
- avoid creating rigid expectations

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Claiming obvious contradictions

If the targeted role is clearly functioning, your bluff collapses.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

Courtier is strongest when it explains uncertainty, not when it creates rigid claims.

---

### ❌ Ignoring duration

Your claim must account for the full 3 night / 3 day effect.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Long-term drunkenness

Your target:

- is drunk for 3 nights and 3 days
- cannot use their ability correctly
- may produce misleading results

---

## 🧩 Timing is everything

Courtier is one of the most timing-sensitive roles in the script.

A well-timed use can:

- stop a Demon
- stabilize the game
- confirm a world

A poorly timed use can:

- waste your only ability
- create confusion
- reduce your impact

---

## 🧠 Courtier in BMR specifically

In a script defined by:

- chaotic nights
- multiple overlapping effects
- difficult interpretation

Courtier adds:

- controlled disruption
- long-term influence
- strategic timing decisions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Courtier is not about who you target — it is about when removing them matters most.**
