# Exorcist (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a player (different from last night): if they are the Demon, they do not wake tonight.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Exorcist is a **Demon-disruption role** that can prevent the Demon from acting, creating **missing or altered night outcomes**.

You provide:

- direct interference with the Demon
- information through disrupted nights
- control over night tempo

Your power lies in:

- stopping kills entirely
- confirming or strongly suggesting Demon candidates
- shaping how the night must be interpreted

---

# 🟢 Playing Exorcist (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just guessing the Demon — you are:

> **Testing players by attempting to shut down the night itself**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Choose meaningful targets

Default strong play:

> “I’m targeting players who are plausible Demon candidates.”

Why:

- your ability is only impactful if you hit the Demon
- random targeting reduces your value
- you want interpretable night results

---

### 2. Track night outcomes carefully

You should:

- compare expected deaths vs actual deaths
- note when kills are missing or reduced
- connect outcomes to your choices

---

### 3. Stay disciplined with target rotation

You must:

- choose a different player each night
- plan your sequence ahead of time
- avoid wasting checks on low-value targets

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Interpret disrupted nights

Move from:

> “There were no deaths — maybe something happened.”

to:

> **“If I hit the Demon, this explains the entire night.”**

---

### Combine with other information

Exorcist becomes much stronger when:

- paired with Chambermaid results
- compared against Sailor or protection effects
- used alongside death pattern analysis

---

### Narrow the Demon pool

Over multiple nights:

- eliminate players who could not be the Demon
- focus on players who match disrupted outcomes
- build a consistent world around your hits

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Targeting randomly

Your ability is strongest when aimed at likely Demon candidates.

---

### ❌ Ignoring alternative explanations

No deaths can also be caused by:

- protection
- other roles
- unusual Demon mechanics

---

### ❌ Overcommitting after one hit

A single disrupted night is strong, but not always conclusive.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Exorcist (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain missing or unusual night outcomes while shaping suspicion toward others**

---

## 🟢 Why Exorcist is a strong bluff

- explains nights with no deaths
- fits naturally into BMR’s volatility
- creates believable “I stopped the Demon” moments
- allows targeted suspicion

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I picked X last night — if there were no deaths, that might mean they’re the Demon.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with night outcomes

### 🟢 Good situations

- nights with no deaths
- nights with fewer than expected deaths
- confusing or ambiguous outcomes

---

### ⚠️ Risky situations

- nights with clear, multiple deaths
- patterns that contradict Demon shutdown

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming to have blocked the Demon when deaths clearly contradict it
- making claims that cannot fit the night

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose targets carefully

### 🟢 Good targets

- players you want to frame as Demon
- players already under suspicion
- players whose behavior fits your narrative

---

### 🔴 Risky targets

- players with strong alibis
- players supported by multiple information sources

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- reading certain nights as “blocked Demon” nights
- narrowing suspicion toward your chosen targets
- accepting uncertainty where needed

---

### Example plays

- “If I hit them, that explains everything about last night.”
- “That night only makes sense if the Demon didn’t act.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- shift from certainty to possibility
- suggest multiple explanations
- avoid being tied to one interpretation

---

### Use your claim strategically

- justify unusual nights
- push executions on targeted players
- reinforce confusion around night patterns

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Ignoring night consistency

Your claims must align with observed death patterns.

---

### ❌ Overstating certainty

Exorcist is strongest when it leaves room for doubt.

---

### ❌ Targeting poorly

Choosing the wrong player can collapse your narrative quickly.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Demon shutdown interaction

If you hit the Demon:

- they do not wake
- no Demon kill occurs
- the entire night may look different

---

## 🧩 Interpreting “no death” nights

No deaths can be caused by:

- Exorcist hitting the Demon
- protection roles (e.g., Innkeeper, Tea Lady)
- Demon-specific mechanics

This makes interpretation complex.

---

## 🧠 Exorcist in BMR specifically

Because Bad Moon Rising already features:

- protection
- multiple deaths
- unusual night outcomes

The Exorcist adds:

- another layer of ambiguity
- another possible explanation for every night

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Exorcist is not about stopping the Demon once — it is about proving who the Demon could be through how the night changes.**
