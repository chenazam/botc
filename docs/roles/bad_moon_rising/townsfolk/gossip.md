# Gossip (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each day, you may make a public statement. If it is true, a player dies tonight.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Gossip is a **day-triggered death role** that links **public statements to night outcomes**, creating powerful but risky information.

You provide:

- conditional extra kills
- information through cause-and-effect
- pressure on both teams through public statements

Your power lies in:

- turning correct reasoning into impact
- forcing the game to respond to your statements
- shaping how night deaths are interpreted

---

# 🟢 Playing Gossip (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just making statements — you are:

> **Turning your understanding of the game into real consequences**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Establish believable gossips

Default strong play:

> “I’ll make statements that are plausible and testable.”

Why:

- your statements must be believable
- wild guesses reduce trust
- consistency builds credibility

---

### 2. Start simple

Good early gossips:

- “At least one of these two players is evil.”
- “This player is not the Demon.”
- “There is at least one Outsider alive.”

These:

- are easier to evaluate
- give you interpretable results
- don’t overcommit

---

### 3. Think about consequences

You must consider:

- what happens if this is true?
- what happens if someone dies because of it?
- how will the town interpret that death?

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Refine your statements

Move from:

> “Broad, safe statements.”

to:

> **“Targeted statements that resolve key questions.”**

---

### Control how deaths are read

If someone dies after your gossip:

- it may confirm your statement
- but it may also be confused with Demon kills

You should:

- clarify your intent
- explain your reasoning
- guide interpretation

---

### Use layered logic

Strong Gossip play involves:

- building on previous statements
- narrowing possibilities
- combining with other roles

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Making overly vague statements

Statements that cannot meaningfully resolve anything are low value.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting too early

A wrong but specific statement can mislead the entire game.

---

### ❌ Ignoring death ambiguity

A death does not always cleanly confirm your statement.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Gossip (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create believable statements that shape how deaths are interpreted**

---

## 🟢 Why Gossip is a strong bluff

- explains extra deaths
- creates believable public reasoning
- fits naturally into BMR chaos
- gives control over narrative

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I gossiped that [statement].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Make plausible statements

### 🟢 Good statements

- things that _could_ be true
- things that support your team
- things that are hard to verify immediately

---

### ⚠️ Risky statements

- easily provable claims
- statements that contradict visible information

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- statements that collapse under basic scrutiny
- over-specific or rigid claims

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Align with deaths

### 🟢 Good situations

- nights with extra deaths
- chaotic or unclear nights
- games with multiple possible explanations

---

### 🔴 Risky situations

- nights with very clear kill sources
- patterns that contradict your statement

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- linking deaths to your gossip when helpful
- questioning that link when not
- maintaining ambiguity

---

### Example plays

- “If that death was from my gossip, this must be true.”
- “Or maybe it wasn’t — the night is messy.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- reinterpret earlier statements
- introduce alternative explanations
- avoid committing to one narrative

---

### Use your claim strategically

- explain extra deaths
- reinforce false worlds
- create doubt in correct reasoning

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overexplaining deaths

Gossip works best when the cause remains uncertain.

---

### ❌ Making bad statements

Weak or incorrect statements undermine your credibility.

---

### ❌ Ignoring consistency

Your statements must still fit the broader game state.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Cause vs. coincidence

If someone dies after your gossip:

- it may be because your statement was true
- or due to the Demon or other effects

This ambiguity is central to the role.

---

## 🧩 Gossip as a pressure tool

Gossip forces the game to react to:

- public reasoning
- shared information
- visible logic

It creates a direct link between **discussion and consequence**.

---

## 🧠 Gossip in BMR specifically

In a script with:

- multiple deaths
- protection
- volatile nights

Gossip adds:

- another possible source of death
- another layer of interpretation
- a strong connection between day and night

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Gossip is not about making statements — it is about making statements that the game must respond to.**
