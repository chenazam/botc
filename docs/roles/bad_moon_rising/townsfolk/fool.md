# Fool (Bad Moon Rising)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** The first time you die, you don't.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Fool is a **self-protection role** that survives the first attempt on their life, creating **false confirmation and ambiguity around death**.

You provide:

- survivability
- apparent confirmation
- disruption of kill interpretation

Your power lies in:

- surviving a lethal event
- influencing how that survival is read
- creating uncertainty around cause of death

---

# 🟢 Playing Fool (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just surviving — you are:

> **Turning your own survival into a misleading but powerful signal**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide your claim strategy

Default strong play:

> “I may want to claim later, depending on how my survival plays out.”

Why:

- your ability is strongest when unexpected
- early claims reduce ambiguity
- survival is your main information source

---

### 2. Stay alive, but not invisible

You should:

- be visible enough to be a plausible target
- avoid being so quiet that you’re ignored
- avoid being so loud that you’re executed

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Interpret your survival

Move from:

> “I lived.”

to:

> **“Why did I live?”**

Possible reasons:

- your ability triggered
- protection (Innkeeper, Sailor, Tea Lady)
- Demon chose not to kill you

---

### Use your survival carefully

You can:

- claim Fool after surviving
- use it to build trust
- or stay hidden and let the ambiguity exist

---

### Be aware of second death

After your ability triggers:

- you are vulnerable again
- your value changes
- your claim may become more important

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Assuming confirmation

Survival does not guarantee you are seen as good.

---

### ❌ Revealing too early

You lose ambiguity if you claim before it matters.

---

### ❌ Ignoring alternative explanations

Your survival may not be due to your ability.

---

# 😈 Bluffing Fool (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Use survival (or claimed survival) to create false confirmation**

---

## 🟢 Why Fool is a strong bluff

- explains survival
- creates apparent confirmation
- fits naturally into BMR protection-heavy nights
- is difficult to verify

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I should have died — I’m the Fool.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Align with survival

### 🟢 Good situations

- you survived unexpectedly
- a kill attempt appears to have failed
- protection could be ambiguous

---

### ⚠️ Risky situations

- survival is clearly explained
- death patterns contradict your claim

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming Fool without a believable survival event

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use confirmation carefully

### 🟢 Good approach

- present survival as possible confirmation
- allow doubt to remain

---

### 🔴 Risky approach

- insisting on full confirmation
- ignoring alternative explanations

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Shape interpretation

You should guide the table toward:

- trusting your survival
- questioning other explanations
- building a world around you

---

### Example plays

- “If I was attacked, this explains it.”
- “Either I’m Fool or something else protected me.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

- adjust your claim if contradictions appear
- lean into ambiguity
- avoid rigid narratives

---

### Use your claim strategically

- gain trust
- survive execution pressure
- support your team

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overclaiming confirmation

Too much certainty makes your bluff fragile.

---

### ❌ Ignoring protection roles

Other explanations must remain viable.

---

### ❌ Misreading the night

Your claim must fit observed outcomes.

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 First-death protection

The Fool:

- survives the first death attempt
- appears to have been targeted
- creates ambiguity around cause

---

## 🧩 Interaction with BMR

In a script with:

- multiple protection roles
- complex death patterns

Fool adds:

- another explanation for survival
- false confirmation signals
- increased ambiguity

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Fool is not about surviving once — it is about how that survival is interpreted.**
