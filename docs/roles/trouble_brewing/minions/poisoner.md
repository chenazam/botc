# Poisoner (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Minion  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a player: they are poisoned tonight and tomorrow day.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Poisoner is a **targeted misinformation role** that corrupts a player’s ability for a full cycle.

You provide:

- false or broken information to a chosen player

Your power lies in:

- creating convincing but incorrect worlds
- disrupting key information roles
- enabling the evil team to control the narrative

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- misinformation
- high-impact
- decision-heavy

---

# 😈 Playing Poisoner (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just picking targets — you are:

> **Deciding which piece of the game becomes unreliable**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Identify high-value targets

You should look for:

- early information roles (Chef, Investigator, Librarian, Washerwoman)
- players likely to reveal early
- confident or vocal players

---

### 2. Poison proactively

Your goal is:

> **to corrupt information before it becomes trusted**

---

### 3. Avoid randomness

Each poison should:

- serve a purpose
- align with a possible world

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Target strategically

Prioritize:

- confirmed or trusted players
- recurring info roles (Empath, Fortune Teller, Undertaker)
- players driving the game

---

### Support your team

You should:

- coordinate (if possible) with your Demon
- protect teammates indirectly
- reinforce your team’s narrative

---

### Think in “worlds”

Each poison should answer:

> **“What false world does this create?”**

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Poisoning randomly

- wastes your strongest ability

---

### ❌ Targeting low-impact players

- reduces your influence

---

### ❌ Ignoring timing

- poisoning too late is often useless

---

# 🎭 Bluffing as Poisoner (Rare)

## 🎯 Your Role

In rare cases, your goal is to:

> **Explain inconsistent or incorrect information as targeted poisoning**

---

## 🟢 When this works

- when multiple info roles conflict
- when chaos already exists
- when a Poisoner is expected in the setup

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I think there’s a Poisoner, and I might have been targeted.”

or

> “I might have been poisoned.”

---

## ⚠️ Risks

- Poisoner is often assumed anyway
- direct claims can attract attention
- requires careful alignment with game state

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poison vs Drunk

- **Poison** → targeted, time-limited, intentional
- **Drunk** → consistent, hidden, structural

Poison often:

- creates sharper contradictions
- enables precise manipulation

---

## 🧩 Poison timing

Poison is strongest when:

- it hits early information
- it corrupts trusted sources
- it reinforces existing suspicion

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Poisoner often:

- creates false confidence
- drives incorrect executions
- enables the evil team to control the narrative

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Poisoner does not create chaos — it creates believable lies at the perfect moment.**
