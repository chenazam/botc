# Saint (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** If you die by execution, your team loses.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Saint is a **high-risk Outsider** that introduces a **game-ending penalty if executed**.

You provide:

- a powerful restriction on execution decisions

Your power lies in:

- forcing the town to reconsider executions
- introducing risk into otherwise straightforward decisions
- shaping both early and late-game behavior

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- high-risk
- endgame
- social

---

# 🟢 Playing Saint (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just avoiding execution — you are:

> **Managing the town’s risk tolerance**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to reveal

Default strong play:

- often reveal early

Example:

> “I’m Saint.”

Why:

- prevents accidental execution
- informs the town of the risk
- shapes early decision-making

---

### 2. Accept suspicion

You should expect:

- doubt
- skepticism
- pressure

This is normal.

---

### 3. Stay cooperative

You should:

- contribute to solving
- remain open and consistent
- help the town despite your limitation

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Build trust

You want:

- enough credibility that:
  > **you are not executed**

---

### Manage your presence

You should:

- avoid becoming the main suspect
- avoid dominating discussion
- remain steady and consistent

---

### Help the town solve

Even without mechanical power:

- contribute logic
- evaluate claims
- support good worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Refusing to reveal

- risks accidental execution

---

### ❌ Becoming overly defensive

- draws more suspicion

---

### ❌ Withdrawing from the game

- reduces trust and influence

---

# 😈 Bluffing Saint (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Discourage execution on yourself and create risk around killing you**

---

## 🟢 Why Saint is a strong bluff

- creates execution hesitation
- introduces risk for good players
- simple and easy to claim
- no information required

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Saint.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Time your claim

### 🟢 Early claim

- prevents early execution
- establishes your identity

---

### 🟢 Reactive claim

- used when under pressure

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming too late when already heavily suspected

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Build credibility

You should:

- act consistently
- contribute to solving
- avoid erratic behavior

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use risk as leverage

You can:

- discourage votes on you
- influence execution decisions
- create hesitation

---

### Example plays

- “If I’m telling the truth, executing me loses the game.”
- “Are we willing to take that risk?”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay believable

- maintain consistent behavior
- avoid contradictions
- continue contributing

---

### Use your claim defensively

- avoid execution
- redirect suspicion
- slow down decisions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Over-relying on the claim

- players may still execute you

---

### ❌ Acting too defensively

- increases suspicion

---

### ❌ Ignoring long-term trust

- Saint bluff requires credibility

---

- a hard penalty for incorrect execution
- hesitation in otherwise clear situations

---

## 🧩 Interaction with final three

Saint heavily affects:

- execution decisions
- risk evaluation
- optimal play
