# Recluse (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** You might register as evil and as a Minion or Demon, even if dead.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Recluse is an **information-distorting Outsider** that can appear as evil to information roles.

You provide:

- false positive signals to roles that detect evil or specific characters

Your power lies in:

- creating misleading but believable information
- disrupting confirmation chains
- causing good players to suspect each other

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- misinformation
- passive
- high-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Recluse (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just a liability — you are:

> **A controlled source of misinformation that the town must correctly interpret**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to reveal

Default strong play:

- often reveal early

Example:

> “I’m Recluse.”

Why:

- your ability can mislead others
- early transparency prevents confusion
- helps information roles interpret results

---

### 2. Inform relevant players

You should:

- tell information roles (Investigator, Empath, etc.)
- help them understand your effect

---

### 3. Stay cooperative

You should:

- support solving
- explain inconsistencies
- remain open about your impact

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Explain contradictions

If:

- someone gets a suspicious result on you

You can:

> **explain it as Recluse interaction**

---

### Support correct worlds

You should:

- help identify when your presence explains anomalies
- prevent false accusations

---

### Avoid becoming a distraction

You should:

- clarify your role
- then step back
- let the game progress

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Staying hidden too long

- causes unnecessary confusion

---

### ❌ Over-explaining

- draws too much attention

---

### ❌ Ignoring your impact

- your ability affects multiple roles

---

# 😈 Bluffing Recluse (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain suspicious results and absorb pressure without requiring strong claims**

---

## 🟢 Why Recluse is a strong bluff

- explains being seen as evil
- low information requirement
- easy to maintain
- absorbs suspicion naturally

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Recluse.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use it defensively

### 🟢 When accused

- “That makes sense if I’m Recluse.”

---

### 🟢 When investigated

- explain why you appeared evil

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming Recluse without any pressure
- unnecessary early claims

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Absorb suspicion

You can:

- take pressure off teammates
- redirect focus
- slow down the game

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay low-impact

You should:

- avoid over-involvement
- not dominate discussion
- remain consistent

---

### Example plays

- “If I’m Recluse, that explains that result.”
- “That doesn’t necessarily mean I’m evil.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

- continue absorbing suspicion
- avoid strong commitments
- adapt to evolving worlds

---

### Support your team

- protect key players
- confuse information
- reinforce uncertainty

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overusing the bluff

- too many Recluse claims raise suspicion

---

### ❌ Being too passive

- you still need to contribute

---

### ❌ Ignoring setup logic

- Recluse must fit Outsider count

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Interaction with information roles

Recluse can affect:

- Investigator (appears as Minion)
- Empath (counts as evil neighbor)
- Fortune Teller (may register as Demon)

This creates:

- false positives
- misleading confirmation
- incorrect suspicion

---

## 🧩 Death does not remove effect

Even when dead:

- Recluse may still register as evil

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Recluse often:

- creates doubt in otherwise clean information
- weakens trust chains
- forces players to consider alternative explanations

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Recluse does not create lies — it makes true information point to the wrong conclusion.**
