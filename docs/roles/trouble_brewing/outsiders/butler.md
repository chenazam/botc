# Butler (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a player (not yourself): tomorrow, you may only vote if they are voting too.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Butler is a **mechanically constrained Outsider** whose voting is tied to another player.

You provide:

- indirect information through voting behavior
- a social signal based on who you choose to follow

Your power lies in:

- creating observable patterns
- subtly revealing trust or suspicion
- influencing voting dynamics

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- utility
- social
- low-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Butler (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just restricted — you are:

> **A source of social information through your choices and behavior**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to reveal

Default strong play:

- often reveal early

Example:

> “I’m Butler.”

Why:

- your ability affects voting
- transparency prevents confusion
- helps others interpret your behavior

---

### 2. Choose your master carefully

You should:

- select a player you trust
- prefer consistent and active players

---

### 3. Communicate clearly

You should:

- explain your choice
- keep others informed if relevant

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Use your choice as a signal

Your selection shows:

- who you trust
- how your reads evolve

---

### Adapt your target

You should:

- change your chosen player as needed
- respond to new information

---

### Contribute actively

Despite your limitation:

- participate in discussion
- help solve
- share your reasoning

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Staying hidden

- creates confusion around your votes

---

### ❌ Following blindly

- your choice should be intentional

---

### ❌ Ignoring your influence

- your behavior still matters

---

# 😈 Bluffing Butler (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain inconsistent or limited voting while appearing cooperative**

---

## 🟢 Why Butler is a strong bluff

- low expectation of impact
- explains voting restrictions
- easy to maintain
- socially flexible

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Butler.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use it to explain votes

You can:

- justify not voting
- explain unusual voting patterns

---

### 🟢 Safe approach

- follow a consistent player
- align with visible behavior

---

### 🟢 Strategic approach

- subtly support your team
- hide intent behind your restriction

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- contradicting visible voting behavior

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Stay believable

You should:

- track your own voting
- ensure consistency
- avoid obvious mistakes

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use it defensively

You can:

- reduce suspicion
- avoid responsibility
- explain passivity

---

### Example plays

- “I couldn’t vote there — my target didn’t vote.”
- “I’m tied to them for voting.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

- adapt your chosen player
- adjust to game state
- maintain consistency

---

### Support your team

- influence votes subtly
- avoid drawing attention
- reinforce narratives

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)
