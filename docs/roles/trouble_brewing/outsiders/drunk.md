# Drunk (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Outsider  
**Ability:** You do not know you are the Drunk. You think you are a Townsfolk character, but you are not.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Drunk is a **hidden Outsider** that believes they are a Townsfolk but receives **false or non-functional information**.

You provide:

- misinformation disguised as valid Townsfolk information

Your power lies in:

- creating false but internally consistent worlds
- misleading the town without appearing suspicious
- distorting trust chains and confirmations

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- misinformation
- hidden-role
- high-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Drunk (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not trying to play differently — you are:

> **A Townsfolk role that must remain open to being wrong**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Play as your believed role

Default strong play:

> Act exactly as your claimed Townsfolk role would.

Why:

- you do not know you are the Drunk
- your behavior should be indistinguishable from a real Townsfolk

---

### 2. Share information normally

You should:

- share your info
- build worlds
- contribute to solving

---

### 3. Stay open to doubt

You should always consider:

- your information might be incorrect

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Re-evaluate your information

If:

- contradictions appear
- your results do not align

Consider:

> **You might be the Drunk**

---

### Avoid overcommitting

Do not:

- lock into one world too strongly
- treat your info as absolute

---

### Support alternative worlds

You should:

- consider explanations where your info is wrong
- help test those worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Blindly trusting your information

- Drunk info can be completely wrong

---

### ❌ Ignoring contradictions

- inconsistencies are a key signal

---

### ❌ Refusing to consider being Drunk

- this can lock the town into false worlds

---

# 😈 Bluffing Drunk (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain incorrect or inconsistent information while maintaining credibility**

---

## 🟢 Why Drunk is a strong bluff

- explains incorrect info
- resolves contradictions
- reduces responsibility for accuracy
- fits many situations

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I think I might be the Drunk.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Identify when to use it

### 🟢 Defensive use

- when your information is challenged
- when contradictions appear

---

### 🟢 Strategic use

- to protect teammates
- to collapse a harmful world

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming Drunk too early without reason

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use it to resolve contradictions

You can:

- explain incorrect results
- remove pressure from yourself
- redirect suspicion

---

### Example plays

- “That doesn’t line up — I might be Drunk.”
- “If I’m Drunk, that explains everything.”

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay believable

You should:

- transition naturally into the claim
- not overuse it
- align it with the game state

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

- use Drunk to pivot out of bad positions
- support new worlds
- adapt to new information

---

### Use it to destabilize

- weaken trust chains
- introduce uncertainty
- prevent the town from locking a solution

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Claiming Drunk too early

- reduces your credibility

---

### ❌ Overusing the claim

- becomes suspicious

---

### ❌ Using it without justification

- must align with visible contradictions

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Interaction with information roles

Drunk most often affects:

- Washerwoman
- Librarian
- Investigator
- Chef
- Empath
- Fortune Teller

This can create:

- false confirmations
- misleading patterns
- incorrect trust chains

---

## 🧩 Drunk vs Poison

- **Drunk** → consistent misinformation
- **Poison** → targeted, changing misinformation

Drunk is often:

- easier to model
- harder to detect early

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Drunk often:

- creates highly believable but false worlds
- causes good players to mistrust each other
- rewards flexible thinking

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Drunk does not break logic — it replaces it with a different, consistent logic.**
