# Undertaker (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, you learn which character died by execution today.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Undertaker is a **recurring information role** that provides **confirmation of executed players’ true roles**.

You provide:

- the actual role of the player executed each day

Your power lies in:

- confirming or disproving claims
- validating or breaking trust chains
- anchoring mid- and late-game worlds

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- recurring-info

---

# 🟢 Playing Undertaker (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just confirming roles — you are:

> **The backbone of trust and verification in the game**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Stay alive

Default strong play:

> Do not reveal immediately unless necessary.

Why:

- your value increases over time
- you need executions to gain information
- early death removes your impact entirely

---

### 2. Gather claims quietly

Before revealing, try to:

- learn what players are claiming
- track who is suspicious
- prepare to verify key targets

---

### 3. Plan your reveal timing

You typically want to reveal:

- after you have at least one useful result
- when your information can meaningfully influence the game

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Confirm or break worlds

Your results can:

- confirm a claimed role
- expose a liar
- invalidate entire world structures

---

### Share information carefully

When revealing:

- provide your results clearly
- explain how they affect the game
- avoid overclaiming certainty

---

### Stay alive as long as possible

You are a high-value target:

- Demon will want you dead
- Poisoner will want to corrupt you

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Revealing too early

- makes you an immediate night kill target

---

### ❌ Revealing too late

- risks dying before sharing key information

---

### ❌ Over-trusting your results

- poisoning can make you confidently wrong

---

# 😈 Bluffing Undertaker (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Control the narrative of executions and create false confirmations**

---

## 🟢 Why Undertaker is a strong bluff

- highly trusted role
- influences mid- and late-game heavily
- reacts to public events (executions)
- can confirm or deny other players

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Undertaker, I saw them as [role].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Survive Day 1

### 🟢 Opening

> “I’m a role that gets info later.”

Why:

- Undertaker has no Day 1 info
- this is expected and believable

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming Undertaker with no execution context
- giving information too early

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose your results carefully

### 🟢 Safe approach

- confirm roles that fit the current world
- avoid creating contradictions

---

### 🟢 Strategic approach

- falsely confirm evil players
- mislabel good players
- reinforce a false narrative

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- contradicting known claims
- giving impossible results

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control trust

You are expected to be reliable.

---

### Example plays

- “That confirms their claim.”
- “That means someone else is lying.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay consistent

- your results must match the evolving game
- avoid sudden contradictions

---

### Adapt when needed

If challenged:

- suggest poisoning
- question other sources
- reduce certainty

---

### Use your authority

You can:

- validate your Demon
- discredit good players
- shape the dominant world

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Contradicting reality too obviously

- Undertaker is heavily cross-checked

---

### ❌ Claiming too early or too loudly

- draws attention before you have leverage

---

### ❌ Failing to plan ahead

- each result must fit future claims

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

Undertaker is extremely sensitive to poisoning:

- false confirmations are very powerful
- poisoned Undertaker can create entire false worlds

---

## 🧩 Interaction with executions

Your ability depends entirely on:

- who is executed
- what they claimed
- how the town interprets your result

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Undertaker often:

- becomes the most trusted player
- confirms or breaks key narratives
- heavily influences late-game decisions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Undertaker does not just reveal truth — it defines what the table believes is true.**
