# Slayer (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Once per game, during the day, publicly choose a player: if they are the Demon, they die.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Slayer is a **single-use, high-impact role** that can instantly **win the game by killing the Demon**.

You provide:

- a one-time shot that can directly eliminate the Demon

Your power lies in:

- threatening an instant win
- forcing the Demon to manage your presence
- shaping social dynamics through timing and pressure

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- high-impact
- single-use

---

# 🟢 Playing Slayer (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just taking a shot — you are:

> **Controlling the threat of an instant win**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to reveal

Default strong play:

- often stay hidden early

Why:

- revealing makes you a priority kill
- your threat is strongest when uncertain
- you want the Demon to fear your existence

---

### 2. Observe the game state

You should:

- listen to claims
- identify suspicious players
- track early information

---

### 3. Do not rush your shot

Your ability is:

- single-use
- irreversible

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Choose the right moment

You want to shoot when:

- suspicion is strong
- your shot can confirm or deny a key world
- killing the Demon is plausible

---

### Use your threat strategically

Even without shooting:

- you influence Demon behavior
- you create pressure

---

### Accept missed shots

If you miss:

- you still provided information
- the game continues

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Shooting too early

- wastes your ability
- low information value

---

### ❌ Never shooting

- unused ability provides no value

---

### ❌ Revealing too early

- makes you an easy target

---

# 😈 Bluffing Slayer (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Create pressure and credibility without ever needing to prove your ability**

---

## 🟢 Why Slayer is a strong bluff

- simple claim
- no ongoing information required
- easy to explain inactivity
- creates natural hesitation

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Slayer.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Decide your stance

### 🟢 Passive approach

- “I’m holding my shot for now.”

---

### 🟢 Active approach

- threaten to shoot
- create pressure on targets

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- committing to a shot you cannot justify

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Manage expectations

You should:

- justify why you haven’t shot yet
- align with the current game state

---

### Example plays

- “I don’t think we have enough info yet.”
- “I’m waiting for a stronger read.”

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use the threat

You can:

- pressure good players
- influence nominations
- support your Demon

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If challenged:

- delay your shot
- question other information
- shift focus

---

### Consider a fake shot (rare)

In some cases:

- you may claim to shoot and miss
- use this to create confusion

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Shooting randomly

- draws unnecessary attention

---

### ❌ Never engaging with your claim

- makes you irrelevant

---

### ❌ Contradicting the game state

- your behavior must match your claim

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

If your shot fails:

- you may be poisoned
- or drunk

This can:

- create confusion
- weaken confidence in your claim

---

## 🧩 Timing considerations

Slayer timing affects:

- Demon survival
- information flow
- execution decisions

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Slayer often:

- creates tension
- influences player behavior
- forces careful play from the Demon

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Slayer is not about taking the shot — it is about when the shot matters.**
