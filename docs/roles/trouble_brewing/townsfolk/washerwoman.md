# Washerwoman (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** You start knowing that 1 of 2 players is a particular Townsfolk.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Washerwoman is an **early-game information role** that helps establish a **trust foundation**.

You do not confirm a player outright — you provide a **binary pair**:

> One of these two players is a specific Townsfolk.

Your power lies in:

- creating early structure
- initiating trust chains
- guiding the first meaningful discussions

--

## 🗂️ Tags

- early-info

---

# 🟢 Playing Washerwoman (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just sharing information — you are:

> **Establishing an early trust anchor for the town**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Share your information (usually early)

Default strong play:

> “I’m Washerwoman, I saw either A or B as [role].”

Why:

- early information is most valuable
- it shapes the initial world-building
- it allows others to respond and verify

---

### 2. Speak to both players privately

Ask:

- “Are you that role?”
- “What info do you have?”
- “When did you get it?”

You are evaluating:

- confidence
- consistency
- timing
- willingness to engage

---

### 3. Compare behavior, not just claims

Both players may claim the role.

Look for:

- hesitation or uncertainty
- overly rehearsed answers
- inconsistencies over time
- contradictions with other information

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Narrow the pair

Move from:

> “A or B”

to:

> **“I believe it is A, because…”**

---

### Build a trust chain

If one player appears genuine:

- trust their information
- combine it with yours
- expand your shared world model

Example:

- You confirm A
- A provides ongoing info
  → forms a **core trusted group**

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Sitting on information too long

- reduces your impact
- wastes early-game influence

---

### ❌ Treating your info as confirmation

- your info is **not definitive**
- it is a starting point, not a solution

---

### ❌ Ignoring poisoning or drunkenness

- if things don’t add up, your info may be compromised

---

# 😈 Bluffing Washerwoman (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is not to be correct — it is to:

> **Create a believable early structure that benefits the evil team**

---

## 🟢 Why Washerwoman is a strong bluff

- simple and widely understood
- low information burden
- creates immediate influence
- introduces multiple players into discussion
- spreads suspicion safely

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Washerwoman, I saw either A or B as [role].”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your targets carefully

### ✔ One good + one evil (ideal)

- whichever player is trusted benefits you

---

### ✔ Two good players

- creates confusion and doubt
- forces good players to question each other

---

### ⚠️ Avoid

- players with already incompatible claims
- players likely to die immediately
- highly confirmed players

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose the role wisely

### 🟢 Safe choices

- Empath
- Undertaker
- Monk

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- Chef (easily contradicted)
- Investigator (creates complex chains)

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control the narrative

You are guiding interpretation.

---

### If both deny

> “That’s strange — one of you might be lying or drunk.”

---

### If one claims it

> “That lines up with what I saw.”

---

### If both claim it

> “Then one of you is definitely evil.”

→ creates a controlled conflict

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- claim possible drunkenness
- reduce certainty
- avoid hard commitments

---

### Use your pair strategically

- protect one player
- push suspicion on the other
- keep both alive for ongoing confusion

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Being too precise

Bad:

> “I saw A as Undertaker.”

Good:

> “I saw A or B as Undertaker.”

---

### ❌ Ignoring your own claim later

- your bluff must remain consistent
- adapt carefully, not abruptly

---

### ❌ Failing to create pressure

- Washerwoman is not passive
- it should generate discussion and tension

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

Incorrect Washerwoman info may result from:

- Poisoner
- Drunk

Do not assume immediately that:

- the Washerwoman is evil
- or the pair is false

---

## 🧩 Double Washerwoman claims

If multiple Washerwomen exist:

- compare pairs
- identify overlaps
- look for contradictions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Washerwoman does not solve the game — it starts it.**
