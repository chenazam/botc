# Ravenkeeper (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** If you die at night, you are woken to choose a player: you learn their character.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Ravenkeeper is a **reactive information role** that only gains information **if killed at night**.

You provide:

- a full role reveal of one chosen player (if you die at night)

Your power lies in:

- threatening the Demon with a strong information reveal
- creating uncertainty about whether you are safe to kill
- shaping night kill decisions through your behavior

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- reactive-info
- high-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Ravenkeeper (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just waiting to die — you are:

> **Manipulating the Demon into killing you at the right time**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Avoid looking too safe

Default strong play:

> Do not appear fully trusted.

Why:

- the Demon avoids killing clearly good players
- you need to look like a _worthwhile but safe_ target

---

### 2. Avoid looking too suspicious

You should not:

- get yourself executed
- become the main suspect

---

### 3. Strike a balance

Your goal is:

> **“Suspicious enough to kill, but not suspicious enough to execute”**

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Signal value subtly

You want the Demon to think:

- you might be important
- but not obviously dangerous

---

### Choose your target carefully

When you die:

- prioritize high-impact players
- investigate suspicious or influential players

---

### Consider when to reveal (if at all)

Sometimes:

- claiming Ravenkeeper early discourages kills

Often better:

- remain hidden
- let the Demon guess

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Being too trusted

- you will never be killed

---

### ❌ Being too suspicious

- you get executed instead

---

### ❌ Wasting your check

- choosing low-impact targets reduces your value

---

# 😈 Bluffing Ravenkeeper (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Appear valuable enough to justify survival or suspicion without needing to prove anything**

---

## 🟢 Why Ravenkeeper is a strong bluff

- no information required unless you die
- explains suspicious behavior
- low commitment
- flexible and reactive

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Ravenkeeper.”

or

> “I’d rather not say my role — it’s better if I die at night.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Use ambiguity

### 🟢 Stay vague early

- avoid giving detailed claims
- suggest you have value if killed

---

### 🟢 Hint at importance

- imply you have a strong ability
- avoid specifics

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- overexplaining your role
- committing to unnecessary details

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Manage your suspicion level

You should:

- appear slightly suspicious
- avoid being executed
- avoid being ignored

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use survival as a narrative tool

If you survive:

- “Maybe the Demon is avoiding me”
- “Maybe I’m not worth killing”

---

### Example plays

- “I’d prefer to be killed at night.”
- “If I die, I can help confirm something.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If challenged:

- claim you’re waiting for a night kill
- avoid giving fake results

---

### Use your bluff defensively

- explain lack of info
- justify unusual behavior
- avoid responsibility

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Overcommitting to the role

- Ravenkeeper works best when vague

---

### ❌ Acting too important

- makes you a target

---

### ❌ Contradicting your own behavior

- your actions must match your claim

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

Ravenkeeper can be:

- poisoned → learns incorrect role
- still appear trustworthy despite wrong info

---

## 🧩 Night kill psychology

The Demon chooses targets based on:

- perceived threat
- information value
- social positioning

Ravenkeeper’s strength is influencing that decision

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Ravenkeeper often:

- alters Demon kill priorities
- introduces high-impact late information
- creates uncertainty about who is safe to kill

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Ravenkeeper is not about surviving — it is about dying at the right time.**
