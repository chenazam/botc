# Librarian (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** You start knowing that 1 of 2 players is a particular Outsider. (Or that zero Outsiders are in play.)

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Librarian is an **early-game information role** focused on **Outsider detection and setup validation**.

You provide either:

- a **pair of players**, one of whom is a specific Outsider
- or confirmation that **no Outsiders are in play**

Your power lies in:

- helping determine the **setup (e.g. Baron presence)**
- identifying **hidden Outsiders (especially the Drunk)**
- creating early structural constraints on the game

--

## 🗂️ Tags

- early-info

---

# 🟢 Playing Librarian (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just identifying Outsiders — you are:

> **Helping the town understand the game setup**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Share your information (usually early)

Default strong play:

> “I’m Librarian, I saw either A or B as [Outsider].”  
> or  
> “I’m Librarian, I saw no Outsiders.”

Why:

- Outsider count is critical to setup solving
- early clarity prevents misinformation from spreading
- it anchors Baron / Drunk discussions

---

### 2. Speak to relevant players privately

If you saw a pair:

- “Are you that Outsider?”
- “Have you claimed anything else?”
- “Does your info/behavior match?”

If you saw zero Outsiders:

- track all Outsider claims carefully

---

### 3. Track Outsider count actively

You should always be asking:

- How many Outsiders should exist?
- Do claims match expected numbers?
- Is a Baron likely?

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Narrow your pair

Move from:

> “A or B”

to:

> **“I believe it is A, because…”**

---

### Solve the setup

Use:

- your info
- Outsider claims
- behavior

to determine:

- whether a Baron is in play
- whether a Drunk is likely

---

### Identify hidden roles

Your info is especially useful for:

- finding the Drunk
- validating or challenging Outsider claims

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Ignoring setup implications

- Librarian is heavily tied to **game structure**
- not just individual players

---

### ❌ Forgetting about the Drunk

- your Outsider may not know they are one
- contradictions may come from this

---

### ❌ Over-trusting claims

- Outsiders may lie (intentionally or unknowingly)
- always verify through behavior and consistency

---

# 😈 Bluffing Librarian (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Distort the perceived Outsider count and confuse the setup**

---

## 🟢 Why Librarian is a strong bluff

- interacts with hidden setup mechanics
- creates uncertainty about Baron / Drunk
- forces players to question Outsider claims
- low requirement for ongoing info

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Librarian, I saw either A or B as [Outsider].”  
> or  
> “I saw no Outsiders.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your version

### 🟢 Pair version (A or B is Outsider)

- creates targeted suspicion
- allows manipulation of specific players

---

### 🟢 “No Outsiders” version

- simpler to maintain
- disrupts real Outsider claims
- useful if multiple Outsiders exist

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Choose your Outsider wisely

### 🟢 Safe choices

- Recluse
- Saint

---

### ⚠️ Risky choices

- Drunk (harder to manage long-term)
- roles already claimed by others

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Shape the setup narrative

You are influencing:

- whether a Baron exists
- how many Outsiders people expect

---

### Example plays

- “If A is the Recluse, that explains X”
- “If we have this many Outsiders, there might be a Baron”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Adapt to Outsider claims

If your bluff conflicts:

- suggest drunkenness
- question other claims
- shift focus to inconsistencies

---

### Use your info as leverage

- protect an ally by placing them in your pair
- frame a good player as Outsider
- create doubt around real Outsiders

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Ignoring Outsider count logic

- if numbers don’t add up, suspicion rises quickly

---

### ❌ Picking impossible setups

- your claim must fit player count and known roles

---

### ❌ Being too rigid

- stay flexible if new claims emerge

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Drunk interaction

The Librarian is one of the few roles that can:

- indirectly identify the Drunk
- or be misled by them

Always consider:

- unexpected behavior may come from hidden Outsiders

---

## 🧩 Baron interaction

Your information is highly relevant when:

- multiple Outsiders are claimed
- setup feels inconsistent

---

## 🧠 Zero Outsider result

Seeing “no Outsiders” is powerful:

- strongly suggests no Baron
- challenges all Outsider claims

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Librarian does not just identify players — it helps define the structure of the game.**
