# Chef (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** You start knowing how many pairs of evil players there are.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Chef is an **early-game information role** that provides **numerical information about evil adjacency**.

You provide:

- a number indicating how many pairs of adjacent evil players exist

Your power lies in:

- constraining possible evil team distributions
- supporting or challenging world-building
- identifying likely evil pair locations

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- early-info

---

# 🟢 Playing Chef (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just giving a number — you are:

> **Constraining the possible worlds of the game**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Share your information (usually early)

Default strong play:

> “I’m Chef, I got a 0.”  
> or  
> “I’m Chef, I got a 1.”

Why:

- your information is most valuable early
- it shapes how players evaluate neighbors
- it supports or contradicts emerging worlds

---

### 2. Map adjacency carefully

You should:

- note seating order precisely
- identify all adjacent pairs
- track which pairs could match your number

---

### 3. Avoid overcommitting early

Your info is:

- broad, not specific
- supportive, not definitive

Use it to:

- guide discussion
- not to lock onto one pair immediately

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Apply your number to evolving worlds

As claims emerge:

- test whether they fit your number
- eliminate worlds that contradict it

---

### Combine with other information

Chef works best with:

- Investigator pairs
- Librarian Outsider info
- Empath readings
- Undertaker confirmations

---

### Re-evaluate constantly

Your number becomes stronger as:

- claims stabilize
- executions occur
- contradictions appear

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Over-interpreting your number

- Chef info is **very easy to misuse**
- many different worlds can fit the same number

---

### ❌ Tunneling a single pair

- especially dangerous with a “1”
- there are often multiple valid configurations

---

### ❌ Ignoring poisoning or drunkenness

- incorrect Chef info can create extremely convincing false worlds

---

# 😈 Bluffing Chef (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Provide a simple, believable number that subtly reinforces a false world**

---

## 🟢 Why Chef is a strong bluff

- extremely simple claim
- low information burden
- widely accepted role
- influences early reasoning
- easy to integrate into many worlds

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Chef, I got a 0.”  
> or  
> “I’m Chef, I got a 1.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your number

### 🟢 Chef 0 (safe default)

- avoids committing to any pair
- creates a “spread out evil” world
- low risk of contradiction

---

### 🟢 Chef 1 (stronger, more aggressive)

- creates a specific pair expectation
- can be used to frame neighbors

---

### 🔴 Risky choices

- numbers that contradict obvious claims or structure
- overly specific interpretations too early

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Position yourself relative to the number

You should:

- subtly support worlds that fit your number
- question worlds that contradict it

---

### Example plays

- “That world doesn’t work with my 1.”
- “We should look at adjacent pairs more closely.”

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use your number to guide suspicion

You can:

- reinforce suspicion on specific pairs
- deflect attention from your team
- support your demon’s position

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your number becomes awkward:

- suggest poisoning
- avoid overcommitting
- shift focus to other inconsistencies

---

### Use your number defensively

- protect your neighbors if needed
- redirect suspicion elsewhere
- reinforce alternative worlds

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Over-explaining your number

- Chef should remain simple
- detailed logic can trap you

---

### ❌ Choosing a number that collapses later

- your number must survive mid-game claims

---

### ❌ Ignoring adjacency implications

- your claim must always align with seating

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

Chef is highly sensitive to misinformation:

- incorrect numbers can create very convincing false worlds
- players often over-trust Chef info

---

## 🧩 Chef 0 vs Chef 1 dynamics

- **0** → suggests evil players are spread out
- **1** → suggests a single adjacent pair

Both can fit many different worlds

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Chef often:

- influences early discussions
- supports or challenges world-building
- appears more reliable than it actually is

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Chef does not identify evil — it eliminates impossible worlds.**
