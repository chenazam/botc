# Mayor (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** If only 3 players live and no execution occurs, your team wins. If you die at night, another player might die instead.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Mayor is an **endgame-focused role** with a **passive survival effect** and a **win condition tied to no-execution in final three**.

You provide:

- a potential alternate night death if targeted
- a powerful final three win condition

Your power lies in:

- surviving into the late game
- building enough trust to influence final decisions
- controlling whether execution happens in final three

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- endgame
- passive
- high-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Mayor (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just surviving — you are:

> **Positioning yourself as a trusted final decision-maker**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Stay hidden

Default strong play:

- do not reveal early

Why:

- you are a valuable late-game role
- revealing early makes you a target
- your ability has no early impact

---

### 2. Build social trust

You should:

- contribute actively to discussions
- appear reasonable and consistent
- avoid extreme or erratic behavior

---

### 3. Avoid unnecessary risk

You should:

- avoid being executed
- avoid becoming a central suspect

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Selectively build trust

You may:

- share your role with a few trusted players
- create a small trust network

---

### Maintain credibility

You should:

- continue contributing to solving
- remain consistent in your reasoning
- avoid suspicious pivots

---

### Prepare for endgame

You want:

- to reach final three
- to be trusted enough that:
  > **no-execution is considered**

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Revealing too early

- removes your surprise value
- makes you a target

---

### ❌ Staying completely silent

- prevents trust from forming

---

### ❌ Failing to build credibility

- your win condition requires trust

---

# 😈 Bluffing Mayor (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Position yourself as a trusted late-game player who can influence final three decisions**

---

## 🟢 Why Mayor is a strong bluff

- no information required
- explains long-term survival
- powerful endgame influence
- difficult to disprove until very late

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Mayor.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Build trust early

You should:

- appear reasonable and cooperative
- contribute to solving
- avoid suspicion

---

### 🟢 Safe approach

- act like a normal Townsfolk
- delay claiming

---

### 🟢 Strategic approach

- softly signal your role to trusted players

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- early public claims without reason

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Position for endgame

You want:

- to survive to final three
- to be seen as trustworthy

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Control the final three

Your goal:

- push for or against execution based on your team’s needs

---

### Example plays

- “We shouldn’t execute here.”
- “No execution might be the safest option.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If challenged:

- adapt your stance
- avoid hard commitments
- shift focus

---

### Use your claim defensively

- justify survival
- reduce suspicion
- influence decisions

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Claiming too early

- makes you a target

---

### ❌ Failing to build trust

- Mayor bluff depends on credibility

---

### ❌ Mishandling final three

- wrong push can lose the game immediately

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Night death redirection

If the Mayor is targeted at night:

- another player may die instead

This creates:

- confusing kill patterns
- indirect protection effects

---

## 🧩 Final three dynamics

If the Mayor is good:

- no execution wins the game

If the Mayor is evil:

- they may try to mimic this behavior

This creates:

- high-stakes decision-making

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Mayor often:

- becomes central in final discussions
- influences execution decisions
- defines endgame outcomes

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Mayor is not about surviving — it is about being trusted when it matters most.**
