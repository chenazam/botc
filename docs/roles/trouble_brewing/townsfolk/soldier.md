# Soldier (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** You are safe from the Demon.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Soldier is a **passive defensive role** that cannot be killed by the Demon.

You provide:

- survivability that can disrupt Demon kill patterns

Your power lies in:

- absorbing night kills
- creating confusion through unexpected survival
- forcing the Demon to adapt their targeting

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- utility
- passive

---

# 🟢 Playing Soldier (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just surviving — you are:

> **Altering the Demon’s kill options and creating misleading survival patterns**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to reveal

Default strong play:

- usually stay hidden

Why:

- revealing removes your defensive value
- the Demon will avoid you
- your ability works best when unknown

---

### 2. Blend in

You should:

- behave like a normal Townsfolk
- contribute to discussion
- avoid standing out too much

---

### 3. Observe night outcomes

You should track:

- unexpected survival
- missing kills
- unusual patterns

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Use survival as information

If you:

- are targeted and survive  
  → you may not know it directly, but patterns may emerge

---

### Consider revealing strategically

Reveal when:

- your survival explains key events
- your claim can resolve contradictions
- your continued survival becomes suspicious

---

### Support the town actively

Even without information:

- contribute to world-building
- apply pressure
- help evaluate claims

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Revealing too early

- removes your protective value

---

### ❌ Assuming survival confirms you

- survival alone is not proof

---

### ❌ Playing too passively

- you still need to contribute

---

# 😈 Bluffing Soldier (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain your survival without needing to provide information**

---

## 🟢 Why Soldier is a strong bluff

- no information required
- explains long-term survival
- low mechanical complexity
- easy to maintain

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Soldier.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose when to claim

### 🟢 Reactive claim

- claim after surviving multiple nights
- use survival as justification

---

### 🟢 Defensive claim

- claim when under suspicion

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming too early without reason

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use survival as your story

You can:

- explain why you are still alive
- justify lack of night kills

---

### Example plays

- “I think they tried to kill me.”
- “That might explain the missing kill.”

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay consistent

Your behavior should:

- match a non-information role
- avoid unnecessary claims
- remain stable over time

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If challenged:

- suggest alternative explanations
- avoid hard commitments
- shift focus to other players

---

### Use your claim defensively

- avoid execution
- reduce suspicion
- explain survival

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Claiming too early

- makes you easy to avoid

---

### ❌ Over-relying on survival

- survival alone is not convincing

---

### ❌ Contradicting kill patterns

- your claim must align with observed events

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

If poisoned or drunk:

- you may die unexpectedly
- this can create confusion

---

## 🧩 Interaction with kill patterns

Soldier affects:

- Demon targeting decisions
- perceived safety of players
- interpretation of missing kills

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Soldier often:

- creates uncertainty around survival
- explains unusual night outcomes
- reduces clarity in kill patterns

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Soldier does not confirm itself — it distorts the meaning of survival.**
