# Virgin (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** The first time you are nominated, if the nominator is a Townsfolk, they are executed immediately.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Virgin is a **confirmation role** that can instantly **prove a Townsfolk player** through nomination.

You provide:

- a one-time confirmation event that identifies a Townsfolk

Your power lies in:

- creating a confirmed good player
- establishing a strong trust chain
- forcing early structure in the game

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- confirmation
- high-impact

---

# 🟢 Playing Virgin (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just waiting to be nominated — you are:

> **Creating a confirmed good player at the right time**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Decide whether to reveal

Default strong play:

- often reveal early, but not blindly

Example:

> “I’m Virgin. I’m willing to be nominated.”

Why:

- early confirmation is powerful
- it creates immediate structure
- it builds trust chains

---

### 2. Choose your nominator carefully

You should:

- coordinate with a player you trust
- avoid random nominations
- prefer players who:
  - have consistent claims
  - are useful if confirmed

---

### 3. Control the timing

You do not have to:

- trigger immediately

You can:

- wait for more information
- use the threat of your ability

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Use confirmation wisely

If your ability triggers:

- a Townsfolk is confirmed
- use that player as a trust anchor

---

### If it does NOT trigger

Consider:

- the nominator may not be Townsfolk
- you may be drunk or poisoned
- the Virgin claim may be false

---

### Adapt your role

After your ability:

- you are effectively a normal Townsfolk
- continue contributing to solving

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Triggering too early without thought

- wastes your confirmation potential

---

### ❌ Trusting the result blindly

- poisoning or drunkenness can break the expected outcome

---

### ❌ Not coordinating nominations

- random triggers are weaker than planned ones

---

# 😈 Bluffing Virgin (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Threaten confirmation without actually allowing it to happen**

---

## 🟢 Why Virgin is a strong bluff

- highly respected role
- creates hesitation in nominations
- discourages random pressure
- influences early game structure

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Virgin.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Control nominations

You should:

- avoid being nominated by Townsfolk
- steer nominations toward:
  - evil players
  - or controlled situations

---

### 🟢 Safe approach

- discourage nomination early
- suggest waiting

---

### 🟢 Strategic approach

- allow nomination by a teammate
- create a fake “non-trigger” explanation

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- being nominated by a confirmed Townsfolk
- situations you cannot control

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Prepare explanations

If your ability does not trigger:

You can claim:

- “They are not Townsfolk”
- “I might be drunk”
- “I might be poisoned”

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Use threat as leverage

You can:

- control who nominates you
- delay executions
- influence early game decisions

---

### Example plays

- “We shouldn’t risk it yet.”
- “Let’s wait until we have more info.”

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If your bluff weakens:

- pivot to uncertainty
- suggest poisoning
- reduce certainty

---

### Use your claim defensively

- avoid execution
- redirect suspicion
- influence nomination flow

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Allowing uncontrolled nomination

- this can instantly expose you

---

### ❌ Overusing the bluff

- repeated avoidance becomes suspicious

---

### ❌ Failing to explain non-triggers

- you must always have a reason ready

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

Virgin is highly sensitive to interference:

- poisoned or drunk Virgin will not trigger
- this creates extremely confusing situations

---

## 🧩 Interaction with nominations

The ability depends on:

- who nominates
- their role
- the Virgin’s state

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Virgin often:

- creates early confirmation chains
- influences nomination behavior
- introduces high-risk, high-reward decisions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Virgin is not about triggering — it is about controlling when (and if) the trigger happens.**
