# Monk (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Townsfolk  
**Ability:** Each night, choose a player (not yourself): they are safe from the Demon tonight.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Monk is a **protective utility role** that prevents Demon kills on a chosen player.

You provide:

- protection that can block the Demon’s kill

Your power lies in:

- preserving high-value good players
- disrupting the Demon’s plans
- indirectly revealing information through survival patterns

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- utility
- protection

---

# 🟢 Playing Monk (Good)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just protecting players — you are:

> **Controlling who is allowed to die and shaping the flow of information**

---

## 🟢 Day 1 Priorities

### 1. Stay hidden

Default strong play:

> Do not reveal early.

Why:

- you are a high-value night target
- revealing removes your protection value
- you provide no immediate public info

---

### 2. Identify high-value targets

You should look for:

- strong information roles
- trusted players
- players driving useful discussion

---

### 3. Avoid predictable patterns

You should:

- vary your targets
- avoid obvious protection choices
- prevent the Demon from playing around you

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Protect strategically, not emotionally

Do not just protect:

- people you like
- or people you trust

Instead, protect:

> **players whose survival benefits the town most**

---

### Track survival patterns

If:

- a player survives unexpectedly  
  → they may have been protected

If:

- a player dies despite protection  
  → you may have:
- been poisoned
- chosen incorrectly
- or faced a different interaction

---

### Consider revealing at the right time

Reveal when:

- your protection explains key events
- your survival becomes suspicious
- your information can help confirm a world

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Good)

### ❌ Always protecting the same player

- makes you predictable
- allows the Demon to avoid your protection

---

### ❌ Protecting low-impact players

- wastes your ability

---

### ❌ Revealing too early

- removes your influence
- makes you a priority kill

---

# 😈 Bluffing Monk (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

As evil, your goal is to:

> **Explain unexpected survival while avoiding responsibility for deaths**

---

## 🟢 Why Monk is a strong bluff

- no hard information required
- flexible and reactive
- explains failed kills
- low expectation of proof

---

## 🧩 Core Bluff

> “I’m Monk, I protected X last night.”

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose your targets carefully

### 🟢 Safe approach

- claim to protect players who were likely kill targets
- align your claims with observed outcomes

---

### 🟢 Strategic approach

- protect your Demon in your claims
- explain why they are still alive

---

### 🚫 Avoid

- claiming protection on players who died
- contradicting obvious kill patterns

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Use protection claims to shape narrative

You can:

- explain why strong players survived
- support trusted players
- reinforce a specific world

---

### Example plays

- “I protected them, that’s why they didn’t die.”
- “I’ve been on them for two nights now.”

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Stay vague when needed

You do not need to:

- announce every protection
- commit to a fixed pattern

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as evil

### Stay flexible

If challenged:

- suggest other explanations
- adjust your protection claims
- avoid hard commitments

---

### Use your claim defensively

- explain your own survival
- justify lack of kills
- support your team’s narrative

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Claiming impossible protections

- obvious contradictions expose you

---

### ❌ Overcommitting to patterns

- predictable behavior invites scrutiny

---

### ❌ Failing to align with kill outcomes

- your claims must match what actually happened

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Poisoning / Drunkenness

If your protection fails:

- you may be poisoned
- or the target was not attacked

This ambiguity makes Monk both:

- powerful for good
- flexible for evil

---

## 🧩 Interaction with kills

Monk indirectly interacts with:

- Demon targeting decisions
- perceived threat levels
- survival expectations

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Monk often:

- influences who is trusted
- explains unusual survival
- creates uncertainty about night actions

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Monk does not generate information — it shapes what information is allowed to exist.**
