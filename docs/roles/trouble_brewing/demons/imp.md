# Imp (Trouble Brewing)

**Role Type:** Demon  
**Ability:** Each night\*, choose a player: they die. If you kill yourself this way, a Minion becomes the Imp.

---

## 🧠 Overview

The Imp is the **primary win condition for the evil team**, responsible for eliminating players and surviving to the end.

You provide:

- nightly kills that shape the game
- strategic control over player survival

Your power lies in:

- controlling the pace of the game
- selecting which information survives
- manipulating perception through kill choices

---

## 🗂️ Tags

- high-impact
- decision-heavy
- endgame

---

# 😈 Playing Imp (Evil)

## 🎯 Your Role

You are not just killing players — you are:

> **Deciding which information is allowed to exist**

---

## 🟢 Early-game Priorities

### 1. Choose a strong bluff

Your bluff should:

- fit the setup
- avoid early contradictions
- allow flexibility

---

### 2. Avoid early suspicion

You should:

- blend in
- contribute naturally
- avoid extreme positions

---

### 3. Make your first kill count

You should consider:

- removing strong information roles
- disrupting early structure
- shaping the narrative

---

## 🧠 Mid-game Strategy

### Kill with purpose

Each kill should answer:

> **“What world does this create?”**

---

### Types of kills

#### 🟢 Information removal

- eliminate key roles (Empath, Undertaker, Fortune Teller)

---

#### 🟢 Disruption kills

- break trust chains
- remove confirmed players

---

#### 🟢 Misdirection kills

- kill players that “don’t make sense”
- create doubt about the game state

---

### Use counterintuitive kills

As you said:

> “Why would the Demon kill them?”

This can:

- protect your team
- reinforce false worlds
- confuse deduction

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Evil)

### ❌ Killing randomly

- wastes your influence

---

### ❌ Always killing optimally

- predictable play can expose you

---

### ❌ Ignoring social context

- kills must align with the narrative

---

# 🎭 Bluffing as Imp

## 🎯 Your Role

Your goal is to:

> **Maintain a believable identity while controlling the game from behind the scenes**

---

## 🟢 Core principles

- act like a Townsfolk
- contribute to solving
- avoid contradictions

---

## 🔑 Step 1: Choose a sustainable role

You should:

- pick a role you can maintain
- avoid high-risk claims unless confident

---

## 🔑 Step 2: Manage suspicion

You should:

- avoid becoming the main suspect
- redirect attention when needed

---

## 🔑 Step 3: Align with your kills

Your behavior must:

- make sense given who dies
- not contradict your role

---

### Example plays

- supporting worlds your kills reinforce
- questioning conclusions your kills undermine

---

## 🔄 Mid-game as Demon

### Adapt constantly

- respond to new information
- adjust your strategy
- remain flexible

---

### Coordinate indirectly

If possible:

- align with your Minions
- support their claims
- avoid exposing them

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Bluffing)

### ❌ Contradicting your own kills

- creates suspicion

---

### ❌ Overacting

- draws attention

---

### ❌ Failing to adapt

- rigid play gets exposed

---

# 🧠 Advanced Notes

## 🔀 Star-pass (Imp self-kill)

If you kill yourself:

- a Minion becomes the new Imp

This allows:

- resetting suspicion
- escaping dangerous positions
- repositioning the Demon

---

## 🧩 Kill psychology

Kills influence:

- what players believe
- which worlds seem plausible
- who appears safe

---

## 🧠 Social impact

Imp often:

- defines the flow of the game
- controls which players remain influential
- shapes the final three

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> **Imp does not win by killing the right players — it wins by creating the right story.**
