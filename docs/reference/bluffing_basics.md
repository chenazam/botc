# Bluffing Basics

Bluffing well is not about telling the most impressive lie.

It is about:

- having a believable role identity
- fitting the current game state
- staying consistent over time
- creating useful pressure
- surviving long enough for evil to win

## Core principles

### 1. Claim a role that lets you function

A good bluff gives you a reason to speak, vote, and participate naturally.

### 2. Do not over-explain early

Vagueness is often safer than unnecessary detail.

### 3. Stay consistent

A simple, stable bluff is usually better than a clever but fragile one.

### 4. Use your bluff to shape the game

A bluff should not only protect you. It should also help evil.

### 5. Adapt carefully

Adjust when needed, but avoid abrupt pivots unless you can justify them.

## Common bluff categories

- first-night info
- recurring info
- utility / protection
- outsider
- endgame role

## Common bluffing mistakes

- claiming too specifically too early
- picking a role that cannot survive comparison
- forgetting your own public story
- playing too passively
- panicking in a double claim
