# Good Play Basics

Good play is not just about being correct.

It is about:

- building worlds carefully
- sharing information clearly
- testing assumptions
- applying pressure well
- managing uncertainty

## Core principles

### 1. Keep multiple worlds alive

Do not commit too early when multiple explanations still fit.

### 2. Share information clearly

Useful information is often strongest when shared before the game narrows.

### 3. Pressure quiet players

Unpressured evil players often survive too easily.

### 4. Track consistency over time

Claims become more meaningful as the game develops.

### 5. Respect uncertainty

A world that feels clean may still be false.

## Common good-player mistakes

- tunnel vision
- over-trusting confirmation chains
- ignoring poison/drunk possibilities
- focusing too much on obvious suspects
- failing to generate alternative worlds
