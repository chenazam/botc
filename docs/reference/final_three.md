# Final Three

Final three is not just the end of the game.

It is a different kind of decision space, where:

- confidence is dangerous
- role claims matter more mechanically
- one wrong execution often loses the game immediately

## Core principle

In final three, do not ask only:

> Which world do I believe?

Also ask:

> What action preserves the most winning chances if I am wrong?

## Important considerations

- Mayor changes no-execution logic
- Saint changes execution risk
- poisoned or drunk information may still be distorting the game
- quiet players may have been under-tested all game

## Common mistakes

- treating final three like early-game solving
- executing from confidence rather than necessity
- ignoring mechanical win conditions
- collapsing to one world too quickly
