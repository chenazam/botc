# Poisoning and Drunkenness

Poisoning and drunkenness are among the most important sources of misinformation in Blood on the Clocktower.

They can:

- create false confirmations
- make good players look evil
- support coherent but incorrect worlds
- punish overconfidence

## Core principle

A wrong piece of information does not automatically mean:

- the source is evil
- the target is evil
- the world is solved

It may simply mean:

- the source was poisoned
- the source is drunk
- the target was affected by misinformation elsewhere

## Strategic implications

### For good

- do not overcommit to a clean world too early
- test whether a world still works if one key piece of info is wrong
- consider whether misinformation explains multiple anomalies at once

### For evil

- poisoning is strongest when it supports believable worlds
- misinformation is most effective when it reinforces existing suspicion
- one good poison often matters more than random chaos

## Common mistakes

- assuming poisoned info is random
- assuming wrong info means evil
- failing to revisit old conclusions after new contradictions appear
