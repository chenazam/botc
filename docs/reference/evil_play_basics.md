# Evil Play Basics

Evil play is not just about avoiding execution.

It is about:

- shaping the town’s world-building
- keeping suspicion off the evil team
- creating false confidence
- managing pressure
- reaching a winning endgame

## Core principles

### 1. Have a believable identity

You need a role claim that lets you act naturally.

### 2. Support the team

Your bluff should help the demon and minions, not only yourself.

### 3. Spread pressure intelligently

The best evil games often keep the town focused on the wrong people.

### 4. Create useful confusion

You do not need chaos everywhere. You need uncertainty in the right places.

### 5. Think ahead

Your Day 1 bluff should still make sense on Day 3.

## Common evil-player mistakes

- claiming too late
- staying too vague for too long
- creating contradictions
- failing to coordinate when possible
- choosing a bluff that offers no strategic value
