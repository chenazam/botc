# How to Evaluate a Claim

Every game of Blood on the Clocktower is built on player claims.

Roles, information, actions — everything enters the game through what players say.

Learning how to evaluate a claim is one of the most important skills in the game.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **A claim is not just what is said — it is how it fits into the game.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

Many players:

- accept claims at face value
- reject claims too quickly
- focus only on content, not context

Strong players evaluate claims based on:

- consistency
- context
- timing
- interaction with the rest of the game

---

# 🧩 Step 1 — Check Internal Consistency

Start with the claim itself:

- does the story make sense?
- is it complete?
- does it contradict itself?

---

## 🧠 Example

A player claims:

- a role
- but cannot explain how their information was generated

👉 Possible issue:

- incomplete or fabricated claim

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **A good claim should make sense on its own.**

---

# 🔍 Step 2 — Check External Consistency

Next, compare the claim to the rest of the game:

- does it align with other information?
- does it create contradictions?
- does it require unlikely assumptions?

---

## 🧠 Example

- Empath says “0”
- neighbor claims evil role

👉 Possible conclusions:

- one is lying
- one is poisoned
- world needs adjustment

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **A claim must fit into a possible world — not just exist in isolation.**

---

# ⏱️ Step 3 — Consider Timing

When a claim is made matters:

- early claims → often less pressured
- late claims → often reactive
- sudden claims → may respond to pressure

---

## 🧠 What to look for

- delayed information
- changing stories
- reactive positioning

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Timing often reveals why a claim was made — not just what it says.**

---

# 🔄 Step 4 — Evaluate Motivation

Ask:

> **Why is this player making this claim?**

Possible reasons:

- sharing real information
- protecting themselves
- supporting a world
- misleading the group

---

## 🧠 Example

A player claims a powerful role late:

- could be true
- could be defensive
- could be strategic

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Every claim has a purpose — identify it.**

---

# 🧠 Step 5 — Adapt to the Script

Claims behave differently depending on the script.

---

## 🟢 Trouble Brewing

- claims are often reliable
- contradictions are meaningful

👉 Evaluate based on:

- logical consistency
- setup constraints

---

## 🟣 Sects and Violets

- claims may be incomplete or distorted
- multiple claims may coexist

👉 Evaluate based on:

- flexibility
- interaction with other claims

---

## 🔴 Bad Moon Rising

- claims are heavily context-dependent
- outcomes matter more than statements

👉 Evaluate based on:

- night events
- cause-and-effect

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **A claim’s reliability depends on the script you are playing.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Taking claims at face value

Claims must be tested.

---

### ❌ Rejecting claims too quickly

Even suspicious claims may be true.

---

### ❌ Ignoring context

Claims do not exist in isolation.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing confidence

Confidence is not proof.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When evaluating a claim, ask:

1. Does this make sense on its own?
2. Does it fit with the rest of the game?
3. Why was this claim made now?
4. What motivation could explain it?
5. How much does this script allow me to trust it?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **A claim is not evidence by itself — it becomes evidence when it fits into a consistent world.**
