# When the Game Might Be Different Than You Think

Most games of Blood on the Clocktower follow a familiar structure:

- there is a Demon
- information behaves consistently
- winning follows clear rules

But sometimes, those assumptions break.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **Before you solve the game, you must confirm what kind of game you are playing.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

Many losses come from:

- solving the wrong structure
- trusting the wrong information
- pursuing the wrong win condition

Strong players recognize when:

- the game is not behaving normally
- their assumptions may no longer apply

---

# 🧩 The Three Types of “Different” Games

There are three fundamentally different ways a game can break expectations:

---

## 🔴 1. Structural Break (**:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}**)

### What changes

- evil is distributed
- there is no single clear center
- executions behave differently

---

### What it feels like

- everyone seems plausible
- no world fully resolves
- executions fail to clarify

---

### Core signal

> **The game lacks structure.**

---

## 🟡 2. Win Condition Break (**:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}**)

### What changes

- winning may mean losing
- correct decisions may backfire

---

### What it feels like

- the game seems solved
- but acting on the solution feels dangerous
- players hesitate at the end

---

### Core signal

> **The game’s outcome feels wrong, even when the logic is right.**

---

## 🔵 3. Reality Break (**:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}**)

### What changes

- the Storyteller becomes the source of deception
- there may be no evil players

---

### What it feels like

- contradictions accumulate
- no world fully works
- the game feels constructed

---

### Core signal

> **The game behaves in ways no real game should.**

---

# 🔍 How to Diagnose the Game

When the game feels wrong, ask:

---

## Step 1 — What kind of “wrong” is it?

- **Flat and unresolved?** → consider Legion
- **Solved but risky?** → consider Heretic
- **Contradictory and impossible?** → consider Atheist

---

## Step 2 — Which explanation is simplest?

Compare:

- normal game explanations
- alternative structures

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **The correct explanation is the one that explains the most with the least strain.**

---

## Step 3 — Re-evaluate your goal

Depending on the situation:

- Legion → identify structure
- Heretic → question outcome
- Atheist → question reality

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Different games require different solving strategies.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Forcing a normal game

Not every game follows standard rules.

---

### ❌ Jumping to conclusions too early

Alternative explanations require evidence.

---

### ❌ Ignoring your own discomfort

If something feels wrong, investigate why.

---

### ❌ Mixing explanations incorrectly

Not every weird game is Legion, Heretic, or Atheist.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When something feels off, ask:

1. Does the structure make sense?
2. Does the outcome make sense?
3. Does the game itself make sense?
4. Which explanation resolves the most issues?
5. Are we solving the right problem?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **The hardest games are not the ones that are difficult to solve — they are the ones where you are solving the wrong thing.**
