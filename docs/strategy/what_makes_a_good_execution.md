# What Makes a Good Execution?

Every day in Blood on the Clocktower ends with a decision:

> **Who should we execute?**

Many games are won or lost not by information — but by how that information is used to make execution decisions.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **A good execution is not about being right — it is about improving your position.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

Players often:

- try to execute “the most suspicious player”
- aim to immediately kill the Demon
- treat executions as final answers

Strong players instead use executions to:

- test worlds
- gain information
- reduce uncertainty

---

# 🧩 Step 1 — Ask What the Execution Achieves

Before voting, ask:

> **“What do we gain if this player dies?”**

Possible outcomes:

- confirms information
- breaks a world
- narrows possibilities
- reveals new data

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Every execution should have a purpose.**

---

# 🔍 Step 2 — Evaluate Information Gain

A strong execution often:

- tests a claim
- verifies or falsifies a world
- creates new information

---

## 🧠 Example

Executing:

- a claimed Outsider → tests setup logic
- a conflicting info role → resolves contradictions

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Executions are most powerful when they produce information.**

---

# 🔄 Step 3 — Compare Execution Options

Instead of asking:

> “Who is most suspicious?”

Ask:

> **“Which execution improves our understanding the most?”**

---

## 🧠 Example

- Player A → highly suspicious, but low information gain
- Player B → unclear, but resolves multiple worlds

👉 Often, Player B is the better execution

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Best execution ≠ most suspicious player.**

---

# 🧠 Step 4 — Adapt to the Script

## 🟢 Trouble Brewing

- executions test logical worlds
- confirmation is strong

👉 Focus on:

- consistency
- setup logic

---

## 🟣 Sects and Violets

- executions reduce uncertainty
- information is less stable

👉 Focus on:

- narrowing possibilities
- managing chaos

---

## 🔴 Bad Moon Rising

- executions may not kill
- survival is ambiguous

👉 Focus on:

- patterns
- interactions
- pressure

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Execution value depends on how the script behaves.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Executing purely on suspicion

Suspicion without purpose wastes executions.

---

### ❌ Ignoring information gain

Killing someone without learning anything is often a loss.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing certainty

You do not need to be sure to make a good execution.

---

### ❌ Treating executions as final answers

Executions are tools, not conclusions.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

Before executing, ask:

1. What does this execution test?
2. What worlds does it eliminate?
3. What new information will we gain?
4. Is this better than alternative executions?
5. Does this fit the script we are playing?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **The best executions are not the ones that feel right — they are the ones that make the game clearer.**
