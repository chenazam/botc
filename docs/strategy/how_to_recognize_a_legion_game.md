# How to Recognize a Legion Game

Legion games do not look like normal games of Blood on the Clocktower.

The challenge is not just identifying evil players — it is recognizing that the _entire structure of the game is different_.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **A Legion game feels “too normal” — but never becomes solvable — and occasionally behaves in ways normal games cannot.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

If you do not recognize Legion early:

- you will build worlds that never resolve
- you will trust information that leads nowhere
- you will misinterpret key mechanical signals

Recognizing Legion changes:

- how you evaluate claims
- how you interpret information
- how you read executions and votes

---

# 🧩 Step 1 — Look for a Lack of Resolution

In normal games:

- contradictions appear
- worlds collapse
- players become clearly suspicious

In Legion games:

- everything remains plausible
- no world fully breaks
- no player becomes clearly evil

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **If the game never sharpens, something is wrong.**

---

# 🔍 Step 2 — Evaluate Claim Distribution

In Legion:

- claims are evenly spread
- most players sound reasonable
- no cluster of obvious evil roles emerges

---

## 🧠 What to look for

- no strong conflicts between claims
- no clear outsider manipulation signals
- no obvious misinformation patterns

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Legion creates a “flat” claim space — everything looks equally possible.**

---

# 🔄 Step 3 — Watch for Failed Executions

This is the **single strongest mechanical signal** of Legion.

In Legion:

- executions fail if only evil voted

---

## 🧠 What this means

If an execution fails:

- it is NOT normal protection
- it is NOT standard survival logic
- it may indicate that **only evil players voted**

---

## 🧠 What to analyze

- who voted
- who abstained
- whether the vote composition makes sense in a normal game

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **A failed execution is not just an outcome — it is a data point about who is evil.**

---

# 🧠 Step 4 — Question Information Results

Legion registers as a Minion too, which means:

- role-detection information may behave strangely
- Minion-based reads may not match expectations
- setup logic may feel inconsistent

---

## 🧠 Example signals

- unexpected Minion information
- too many players appearing “evil-adjacent”
- role-based reads that don’t fit a normal distribution

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **If information behaves incorrectly but consistently, question the structure — not the players.**

---

# 🔄 Step 5 — Compare Against Script Expectations

Ask:

- does this match how this script usually behaves?
- are expected mechanics missing?
- are unexpected mechanics appearing?

---

## 🧠 Example

In a Trouble Brewing-style game:

- you expect clean logic
- you expect contradictions

If instead you get:

- flat plausibility
- strange execution behavior
- inconsistent role signals

👉 Legion becomes a strong possibility

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Legion reveals itself through both absence (no resolution) and presence (impossible mechanics).**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Forcing a normal world

Trying to solve Legion using standard logic.

---

### ❌ Ignoring failed executions

This is the most direct signal — do not overlook it.

---

### ❌ Overtrusting consistent claims

Legion players are designed to appear believable.

---

### ❌ Treating all ambiguity as social

Some ambiguity is mechanical, not behavioral.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When you suspect Legion, ask:

1. Has the game failed to resolve over time?
2. Are most players equally plausible?
3. Has an execution failed in a way that suggests evil-only voting?
4. Does information behave strangely but consistently?
5. Would Legion explain all of this cleanly?

---

# 🔄 What To Do If It Is Legion

If Legion is likely:

- slow down and analyze vote patterns carefully
- treat failed executions as key information
- stop solving for a single Demon
- consider that evil is distributed

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **The moment you identify Legion, you must change how you interpret both votes and information.**

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **Legion is not just detected through lack of contradictions — it is revealed when the game behaves in ways normal games cannot.**
