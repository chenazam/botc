# Reading No-Death Nights (Bad Moon Rising)

In Bad Moon Rising, a night where no one dies is one of the most informative — and most misleading — events in the game.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **A no-death night does not mean nothing happened — it means something interfered.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

In most scripts:

- no deaths → something went wrong

In Bad Moon Rising:

- no deaths → many things could have happened

This makes no-death nights:

- highly informative
- highly ambiguous
- easy to misinterpret

---

# 🧩 Possible Causes of No-Death Nights

## 🛡️ Protection

- Sailor
- Innkeeper
- Tea Lady
- Fool

These can:

- prevent one or more deaths
- create partial or full survival

---

## 🚫 Demon Interference

- Exorcist (blocks Demon)
- Courtier (disables Demon ability)

These can:

- prevent the Demon from killing entirely

---

## 🧪 Drunkenness / Poisoning

- may cause protection to behave differently
- may distort expected outcomes

---

## ⏳ Tempo Mechanics

- Po (charging, no kill)
- Zombuul (kill condition not met)

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **A no-death night always has a cause — your job is to find the most likely one.**

---

# 🔍 Step 1 — Start with Expected Deaths

Ask:

> **“How many players should have died?”**

Then:

> **“What could have prevented all of them?”**

---

## 🧠 Example

If 1 death is expected:

Possible explanations:

- protected target
- Demon blocked
- Po skipped

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Always start from expectation, not observation.**

---

# 🔄 Step 2 — Compare Explanations

You should evaluate:

- World A → protection
- World B → Demon blocked
- World C → tempo mechanic

---

## 🧠 Key Differences

- Protection → usually localized
- Demon block → global
- Tempo → pattern-based

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Different causes produce different future patterns — track them.**

---

# 🧠 Step 3 — Track Patterns Over Time

A single no-death night is ambiguous.

Multiple nights reveal patterns:

---

## 🧩 Pattern Examples

### 🛡️ Repeated survival

→ likely protection roles

---

### 🚫 Single full stop

→ likely Exorcist or Courtier

---

### ⏳ Alternating death / no death

→ likely Po

---

### 🧟 Irregular conditions

→ possible Zombuul

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **No-death nights become meaningful when viewed in sequence.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Assuming protection immediately

Many causes exist — do not lock in too early.

---

### ❌ Ignoring tempo mechanics

Po and Zombuul create unique patterns.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing a single night

One data point is rarely enough.

---

### ❌ Forcing consistency

BMR often produces messy sequences.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When a no-death night occurs:

1. How many deaths were expected?
2. What could prevent all of them?
3. Does this fit with previous nights?
4. What pattern would this cause going forward?
5. What explanation requires the fewest assumptions?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **In Bad Moon Rising, a quiet night is not the absence of action — it is the result of hidden interaction.**
