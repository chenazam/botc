# Protection Layers (Bad Moon Rising)

In Bad Moon Rising, survival is not a simple yes/no outcome.

Multiple effects can:

- prevent death
- delay death
- fake death
- interfere with the Demon

This creates **layers of protection** that make survival difficult to interpret.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **If someone survives, ask “what layers could have prevented their death?” — not “why didn’t they die?”**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

In Bad Moon Rising:

- survival is common
- protection overlaps
- multiple explanations are always possible

This means:

- survival rarely confirms anything
- failure to die is often ambiguous
- death patterns become unreliable

---

# 🛡️ Types of Protection

## 🟢 Direct Protection

These roles actively prevent death:

- Sailor
- Innkeeper
- Tea Lady
- Fool

---

### 🧠 Characteristics

- target-based
- often conditional
- may introduce drunkenness

---

## 🟡 Indirect Protection

These roles interfere with the Demon:

- Exorcist (blocks Demon action)
- Courtier (disables Demon ability)

---

### 🧠 Characteristics

- prevent the kill from happening at all
- may create “no death” nights
- often harder to detect

---

## 🔵 Conditional Survival

Some effects do not “protect” directly, but still prevent death:

- Fool (first death fails)
- Tea Lady (requires correct neighbors)

---

### 🧠 Characteristics

- depend on conditions
- may appear inconsistent
- can be misread as other effects

---

## 🔴 Apparent Survival (Not Protection)

Some mechanics create survival-like outcomes:

- Zombuul (fake death)
- Shabaloth (revival)

---

### 🧠 Characteristics

- distort perception of death
- create false confirmation
- break assumptions about finality

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Not all survival is protection — and not all protection looks the same.**

---

# 🧩 Step 1 — Identify Possible Layers

When someone survives:

Ask:

- could they have been protected?
- could the Demon have been blocked?
- could the death have been altered?

---

# 🔄 Step 2 — Combine Explanations

Instead of:

> “They were protected.”

Think:

> **“What combination of effects could explain this?”**

---

## 🧠 Example

A player survives:

Possible explanations:

- Innkeeper protected them
- Sailor caused drunkenness
- Demon was Exorcised

👉 Multiple layers may apply simultaneously

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Always consider multiple overlapping effects.**

---

# 🧠 Step 3 — Compare Worlds

You should evaluate:

- World A → direct protection
- World B → Demon interference
- World C → layered interaction

Then ask:

- which explains the full night best?
- which introduces fewer contradictions?

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **The correct explanation is the one that fits the most events with the fewest assumptions.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Treating survival as confirmation

Survival does not prove alignment.

---

### ❌ Assuming a single cause

Most survival events have multiple possible explanations.

---

### ❌ Ignoring interference

Sometimes the Demon never acted.

---

### ❌ Overvaluing protection roles

Protection may exist — but not always where you expect.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When someone survives:

1. Could they have been directly protected?
2. Could the Demon have been blocked?
3. Could the death have been delayed or altered?
4. Could multiple effects overlap?
5. Which explanation fits the entire night best?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **In Bad Moon Rising, survival is not the absence of death — it is the result of layered interactions.**
