# How to Read a Night (Bad Moon Rising)

Bad Moon Rising is fundamentally a script about **interpreting the night**.

Unlike other scripts, you cannot assume:

- that deaths came from the Demon
- that survival means protection
- that information is reliable
- that events happened in a simple way

---

## 🧠 The Core Principle

> **Every night has multiple valid explanations. Your job is to compare them — not pick the first one that fits.**

---

# 🔍 Step 1 — Count Deaths (But Don’t Trust Them)

Start with the most obvious question:

> “How many players died?”

But immediately follow it with:

> **“How many _should_ have died?”**

---

## 🧩 Possible Death Sources

A death in BMR could come from:

### 🔪 Demon kills

- standard Demon ability
- Po burst
- Shabaloth double kill

---

### ⚠️ Non-Demon deaths

- Gossip
- Gambler
- Godfather
- Tinker
- Moonchild

---

### 🔄 Delayed or altered deaths

- Pukka (previous night)
- protection interactions

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Never assume all deaths came from the Demon.**

---

# 🛡️ Step 2 — Check for Missing Deaths

If fewer players died than expected:

> **Something interfered with the night.**

---

## 🧩 Possible Causes

### 🛡️ Protection

- Sailor
- Innkeeper
- Tea Lady
- Fool

---

### 🚫 Demon disruption

- Exorcist
- Courtier (on Demon)

---

### 🧪 Global interference

- Minstrel

---

### 🧠 Tempo mechanics

- Po (charging)
- Zombuul (kill condition not met)

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **A quiet night is just as meaningful as a deadly one.**

---

# ⏱️ Step 3 — Think in Timelines, Not Moments

In BMR:

> **What happened tonight may have been caused yesterday.**

---

## 🧩 Delayed Effects

### ⏳ Pukka

- poison now
- death later

---

### 💀 Gambler

- death depends on previous decision

---

### 🗣️ Gossip

- death depends on earlier statement

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Always ask: “Was this caused earlier?”**

---

# 🧠 Step 4 — Separate Survival from Safety

If someone lived:

> ❗ That does NOT mean they were safe  
> ❗ That does NOT mean they are good

---

## 🧩 Possible Reasons for Survival

- protection (Sailor, Innkeeper, Tea Lady)
- Fool ability
- Demon choice
- interference (Exorcist, Courtier)
- Zombuul mechanics

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Survival is information — but it is not confirmation.**

---

# 🔄 Step 5 — Look for Layered Effects

The hardest part of BMR:

> **Multiple things can happen at once.**

---

## 🧩 Example Night

- 2 deaths occur
- 1 player survives unexpectedly
- 1 player gives wrong info

Possible explanation:

- Shabaloth (2 kills)
- Innkeeper (protected one target)
- Sailor (caused drunkenness)

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Do not look for one explanation — look for combinations.**

---

# 🧩 Step 6 — Build Worlds, Not Conclusions

Instead of:

> “This is what happened.”

You should think:

> **“These are the possible worlds that explain the night.”**

---

## 🧠 Good Practice

Maintain multiple possibilities:

- World A → Demon caused all deaths
- World B → Gossip + Demon
- World C → Godfather + protection

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **BMR punishes early certainty.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

## ❌ “Clean night” thinking

Assuming:

- 1 death = Demon
- 0 deaths = protection

This is often wrong.

---

## ❌ Ignoring Outsiders

Outsiders can:

- add deaths
- distort alignment
- break assumptions

---

## ❌ Overtrusting confirmation

Even “confirmed” information can be:

- poisoned
- drunk
- misinterpreted

---

## ❌ Forcing a single explanation

BMR rarely has only one valid interpretation.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When analyzing a night, ask:

1. **How many died?**
2. **How many should have died?**
3. **What could have added deaths?**
4. **What could have prevented deaths?**
5. **Could this be delayed from a previous night?**
6. **What combinations explain this cleanly?**

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **Bad Moon Rising is not about solving the night — it is about surviving long enough to understand it.**
