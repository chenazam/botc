# Bad Moon Rising

Bad Moon Rising is a Blood on the Clocktower script defined by **night volatility, survival uncertainty, and tempo swings**.

It rewards:

- careful interpretation of night outcomes
- flexible reasoning under pressure
- strong timing and risk assessment

---

## 🧠 What Makes This Script Unique

### 🌙 Heavy Night Play

Many important effects happen at night:

- kills
- protection
- repeated role actions
- survival changes

---

### ☠️ Deaths Are Hard to Read

The number and pattern of deaths often matter more than any single claim.

Players must ask:

- why did this player die?
- why did this player survive?
- why were there multiple deaths?
- why were there no deaths?

---

### 🛡️ Protection Is Central

Protection and survival are not edge cases in BMR — they are core mechanics.

This means:

- survival is not always suspicious
- death is not always straightforward
- the town must think carefully before interpreting either

---

## 🧭 Core Themes

### 1. The night tells a story

Bad Moon Rising is often solved by understanding what the night was trying to communicate.

---

### 2. Tempo matters

The pace of the game can swing dramatically depending on the Demon and supporting roles.

---

### 3. Volatility punishes rigid worlds

A clean theory can collapse after a single complicated night.

---

### 4. Survival is part of the puzzle

Protection, interruption, and unusual kill patterns make survival meaningful.

---

## 🎯 How to Approach the Script

### As Good

- Track each night carefully
- Avoid jumping to obvious conclusions
- Compare multiple explanations for deaths and survival
- Re-evaluate worlds often

---

### As Evil

- Use the script’s volatility to your advantage
- Support believable explanations for unusual nights
- Control tempo through kills and pressure
- Let the town overinterpret incomplete patterns

---

## ⚠️ Common Good-Player Mistakes

- assuming deaths always mean the obvious thing
- overcommitting to one explanation of the night
- ignoring protection worlds
- underestimating how much tempo matters

---

## 😈 Common Evil Goals

- create confusing but believable nights
- make survival hard to interpret
- exploit volatility to break clean deduction
- control the pace of the game
