# Trouble Brewing

Trouble Brewing is the foundational Blood on the Clocktower script.

It is often taught first because:

- roles are readable
- information is structured
- bluffing space is accessible
- core mechanics are easy to understand

At the same time, it remains strategically rich due to:

- poisoning and drunkenness
- outsider count manipulation
- false trust chains

---

## 🧠 What Makes This Script Unique

### 🔍 Reliable Information (with exceptions)

Most information is:

- clear
- actionable
- structurally meaningful

But can be disrupted by:

- poisoning
- drunkenness
- evil bluffing

---

### ⚖️ Structured Logic

The script rewards:

- building clean worlds
- tracking claims
- validating consistency

---

### 🎭 Social Trust

Even with strong mechanics, the game is decided by:

- who is believed
- when information is shared
- how executions are chosen

---

## 🧭 Core Themes

### 1. Early structure matters

Roles like Washerwoman, Librarian, Investigator, and Chef often define the first major worlds.

---

### 2. Misinformation is subtle but powerful

A single poisoned or drunk result can:

- distort the entire game
- create a fully believable false world

---

### 3. Setup logic matters

Outsider count and role distribution:

- strongly influence interpretation
- help identify possible worlds

---

### 4. Endgames are sharp

Final three decisions are critical, with:

- Mayor
- Saint
- poisoning
- false confirmations

---

## 🎯 How to Approach the Script

### As Good

- Build structured, consistent worlds
- Share information early
- Cross-check claims
- Remain open to poison/drunk explanations

---

### As Evil

- Create believable early structure
- Support consistent but incorrect worlds
- Exploit trust chains
- Survive into a manageable final three

---

## ⚠️ Common Good-Player Mistakes

- overcommitting to one world
- treating early information as solved
- ignoring poison/drunk possibilities
- failing to pressure quiet players
- making overconfident final three decisions

---

## 😈 Common Evil Goals

- create believable early structure
- distort setup interpretation
- reinforce false trust chains
- keep suspicion concentrated
- reach a favorable endgame
