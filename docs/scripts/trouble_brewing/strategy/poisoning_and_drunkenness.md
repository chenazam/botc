# Poisoning and Drunkenness (Trouble Brewing)

In Trouble Brewing, most information is reliable — but not always.

Poisoning and drunkenness are the primary ways the game introduces **false information into otherwise clean logic**.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **If something doesn’t make sense, assume misinformation before abandoning logic entirely.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

Without poisoning or drunkenness:

- the game is highly solvable
- contradictions clearly identify evil

With them:

- good players can appear wrong
- evil players can appear correct
- worlds become harder to evaluate

---

# 🧩 Sources of Misinformation

## 🍷 Drunkenness

- The Drunk (outsider)
- behaves like a Townsfolk
- receives incorrect information

---

## 🧪 Poisoning

- Poisoner
- Spy (indirectly, via misinformation influence)

Poisoning causes:

- incorrect information
- broken confirmations
- misleading results

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **False information does not mean the player is evil.**

---

# 🔍 Step 1 — Identify Possible Poisoning

When information conflicts:

Ask:

- could one player be poisoned?
- does this resolve all contradictions?

---

## 🧠 Example

- Empath says “0 evil neighbors”
- Investigator says neighbor is evil

👉 Possible explanation:

- Empath is poisoned

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Always try a “poisoned world” before discarding multiple claims.**

---

# 🔄 Step 2 — Compare Worlds

You should consider:

- World A → player is lying
- World B → player is poisoned

Then ask:

- which requires fewer assumptions?
- which explains more information cleanly?

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Poisoning is often the simplest explanation for contradictions.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Assuming all information is true

This leads to impossible worlds.

---

### ❌ Assuming all incorrect info is evil

Good players can be wrong.

---

### ❌ Overusing poisoning

Not every contradiction requires poisoning.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When information conflicts:

1. Can this be explained by one poisoned player?
2. Does that fix all contradictions?
3. Is that simpler than multiple liars?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **In Trouble Brewing, misinformation bends the truth — but it rarely breaks it completely.**
