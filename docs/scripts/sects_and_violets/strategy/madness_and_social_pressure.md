# Madness and Social Pressure (Sects and Violets)

Madness is a mechanic that transforms the game from logical deduction into **social pressure and behavioral play**.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **Madness is not about truth — it is about controlling behavior.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

Madness introduces:

- forced claims
- restricted communication
- execution risk for breaking rules

This shifts the game toward:

- social reads
- pressure management
- behavioral analysis

---

# 🧩 Step 1 — Understand the Goal

Madness is designed to:

- restrict what players can say
- force uncomfortable positions
- create visible pressure

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Madness is about how players act, not what they say.**

---

# 🔍 Step 2 — Read Behavior

When someone is mad:

- are they confident or hesitant?
- do they overcommit or avoid detail?
- do they slip or contradict themselves?

---

## 🧠 Example

A player claiming a role:

- avoids specifics
- hesitates under pressure

👉 Possible sign of madness

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Madness creates behavioral tells — use them.**

---

# 🔄 Step 3 — Play Around Madness

As a player:

- avoid forcing obvious breaks
- apply pressure carefully
- observe reactions

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Breaking madness is powerful — but forcing it can backfire.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Ignoring madness

It is a central mechanic, not a side effect.

---

### ❌ Taking claims at face value

Mad players are constrained.

---

### ❌ Overpressuring

Too much pressure can create chaos without clarity.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When dealing with madness:

1. Does the behavior match the claim?
2. Is the player under visible pressure?
3. What would they say if they were not restricted?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **In Sects and Violets, madness turns information into performance — and performance into information.**
