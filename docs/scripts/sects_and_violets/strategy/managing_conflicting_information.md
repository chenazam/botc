# Managing Conflicting Information (Sects and Violets)

Sects and Violets is defined by **information that does not agree with itself**.

Unlike Trouble Brewing, contradictions are not exceptions — they are expected.

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **Conflicting information is not a problem to solve — it is the core of the game.**

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

In Sects and Violets:

- multiple players can be “correct” in different ways
- information may be incomplete or misleading
- clean worlds are rare

---

# 🧩 Step 1 — Accept Multiple Truths

Instead of:

> “One of these must be wrong.”

Think:

> **“How could both of these be true?”**

---

## 🧠 Example

- Dreamer result conflicts with Philosopher claim

Possible explanations:

- misinformation
- role interaction
- alignment shift

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **Do not force contradictions into a single correct answer too early.**

---

# 🔄 Step 2 — Track Multiple Worlds

Maintain:

- World A → Dreamer is correct
- World B → Philosopher is correct
- World C → both are distorted

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **S&V is about managing uncertainty, not eliminating it.**

---

# 🧠 Step 3 — Look for Stability

Over time:

- some worlds collapse
- others remain consistent

You should:

- track which explanations survive
- eliminate those that break

---

## ⚠️ Key Rule

> **The correct world is the one that survives the longest.**

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Forcing clean logic

S&V rarely supports clean solutions early.

---

### ❌ Discarding information too quickly

Even conflicting info may still be useful.

---

### ❌ Overcommitting

Early certainty is often punished.

---

# 🎯 Practical Heuristic

When information conflicts:

1. Can both be true in some world?
2. What assumptions are required?
3. Which world stays consistent longer?

---

# 🧠 Final Insight

> **In Sects and Violets, you win by managing uncertainty better than your opponents — not by eliminating it first.**
